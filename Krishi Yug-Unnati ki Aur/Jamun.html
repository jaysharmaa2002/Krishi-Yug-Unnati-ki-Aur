Jamun

Jamun- It is an evergreen tree, whose average height is 30 meters. Its fruits are green in color, which turn dark purple as they ripen. While on the one hand its fruits are delicious to eat and beneficial for health, on the other hand, the seeds of Jamun are also used in the manufacture of Ayurvedic medicine. It is widely used in diabetes.

Jamun, the highly nutritious, refreshing and succulent fruit flooding the summer markets has innumerable health benefits. Commonly known as Java plum or Indian blackberry in English, Jamun or Jambul in Hindi, Jambufalam or Mahaphala in Sanskrit, Naavar Pazham in Tamil and Neredu in Telugu, it goes by the botanical name Syzygium cumini.

Jamun, the tall tree with a heavy trunk is native to the Indian sub-continent but is also widely found in various Asian countries. The tree bears fruits which are oblong in shape - that are green when raw but turn pink or purple as they ripen. This juicy fruit holds a great significance in holistic treatments like Ayurvedic, Unani and Chinese medicine as it attenuates Kapha and Pitta.

In fact, jamun found a special mention in Ramayana and is prized as the "Fruit of Gods" as Lord Rama survived eating this berry during his 14 years of exile in the forest.

Available in two varieties - Jamuns with white toned flesh and has a good amount of pectin while the other one with dark purplish flesh has lower amounts of pectin. Pectin is a polysaccharide substance present in the flesh of berries, fruits like apples that serves as a thickening agent, while making jams and jellies.


Climate for Blackberry Cultivation A temperate and tropical climate is required for the Jamun tree to grow. Centuries of frost and extreme heat in summer affect the Jamun tree. Excessive rainfall during flowering stage reduces production. Small Jamun plants need protection from hot wind and frost.

Selection of soil Well drained loamy soil is suitable for high yield of Jamun. pH of the land for horticulture/cultivation of Jamun. The value should be between 5 and 8.

Improved varieties of Jamun There are many improved varieties of Jamun from which higher production of high quality Jamun can be obtained. Raja Jamu: This variety is the most grown in the country. The fruits of this variety are large and dark purple in color, which are very juicy and large in size, while the berries of the kernel variety are small in size.

CISHJ-45: It is a seedless variety of Jamun with oval fruits of normal thickness. After ripening, they become black and dark blue in color. The fruits of this variety are juicy and sweet in taste. This variety of Jamun is famous in North India.

Ri Jamun: Plants of this variety give fruits in the rainy season, whose fruits are dark purple or blue in color. Its fruits are oval and the kernels are very small, inside which are sweet juicy anus with slight sourness. This variety is grown more in Punjab.

Goma Priyanka: This variety of Jamun is considered very good. The fruit of this variety is sweet in taste and has a high amount of pulp. The fruits are ready to ripen during the rainy season.

Katha: Fruits of this variety are dark purple and small in size. It is sour in taste and less pulpy.

CISHJ-37: This variety of Jamun has been extracted from Central for Sub-Tropical Horticulture, Lucknow. The fruits of this variety are dark black in colour, small in kernel size, sweet and juicy in the flesh. This variety is also ready by ripening in the rainy season.
Apart from these, Narendra 6, Konkan Bhadoli, and Rajendra 1 are also improved varieties of Jamun. From which good production can be taken in different areas.

Method of sowing/ planting The seed should be treated with Bavistin medicine. Sow 4-5 cm deep at a distance of 25 X 15 cm in the nursery of friable soil. Germination starts from the seeds after 10 to 15 days. After about a year, transplant jamun plants when the monsoon starts. At the time of planting, fill the pit with good soil and rotted manure in 3:1 ratio.

Plant protection Many types of pests and diseases can occur on the Jamun tree, due to which there is a possibility of a huge decline in production. The details of pests and diseases on Jamun trees are as follows--

  • Spider: This six-legged small creature sucks the juice from the leaves and twists it and covers the leaves with a web, which adversely affects the photosynthesis activity by the trees and the tree does not know how to make food. Not only this, the fruits get spoiled by mixing with each other. To prevent this disease, pluck the collected leaves before they bear fruit and burn them. Along with this, 1 ml of Chlorpyrifos should be sprayed in 1 liter of water.
  • Thrips: This insect causes white spots on the leaves, which later turn the leaves yellow. It is a sucking pest for which spraying should be done by mixing Profenophos 50% EC @ 30 ml or Acephate 75% SP @ 18 gm or Fipronil 5% SC @ 25 ml per 15 liters of water.
  • Fruit borer: This insect makes a tunnel inside the fruit and rots the fruit. To prevent this, spray Imamectin Benzoate 5% SG @ 0.5 per liter of water or as a biological treatment, spray Bavaria bassiana @ 2.5 g per liter of water.
  • Leaf scorch disease: Due to this disease, brownish-yellow spots are formed on the leaves of trees and the leaves dry up from the edges and start shrinking. Due to which the tree looks scorched. For its prevention, spraying of Mancozeb 75 WP at the rate of 2 grams per liter can be done.
  • Anthracnose disease: With this disease, symptoms like spots on the leaves, drop of leaves and drying of twigs etc. are seen. For its control, take spray of Mancozeb 75 WP @ 2 g/Ltr of water per acre or as organic spray with Trichoderma viridi @ 2 g/Ltr of water.

Manure & Fertilizer Apply well decomposed cow dung at 20-25 kg per plant per year. When the plants reach the fruiting stage, increase the amount of manure to 50-60 kg per plant per year. After that the tree should be given Nitrogen 500 gm, Potash 600 gm and Phosphorus 300 gm per plant per year.

Irrigation arrangement Initially, when the plant is planted in the pit, then light irrigation should be given. After this, continuous irrigation is required. Jamun tree is considered tough, so irrigation 6-7 times throughout the year is sufficient. Most of it is irrigated by Thala method. Give water as required at an interval of 10-15 days during summer and 20-25 days in winter.It does not need watering during the rainy season. Plants prepared from seed start bearing fruit after 6-7 years, whereas plants prepared by perch bonding or cuttings start bearing fruit after 3-4 years.

Weed management Growing weeds around Jamun trees increases the problem of pests and diseases. Work to remove weeds by hand or agricultural machinery. Weeds should be avoided because its opposite effect can be seen on trees as well.

Fruit harvesting and storage Harvesting of fruits usually starts from May-June and continues till August. Its fruit should be plucked every day. When the fruits are black or dark purple, they become suitable for harvesting. The fruits are then packed in bamboo baskets or wooden boxes. To keep the jamuns for 5-6 days, they have to be kept at low temperature. Pack the fruits well to avoid damage and take them to the market for sale at the right time.

Yield 80-100 kg of fruits can be taken from one Jamun tree.

Cost and profit in Jamun orchards The cost of setting up a Jamun ka orchard is around Rs 35000 and the annual maintenance cost is estimated at Rs 25000. Its profits depend on the variety and maintenance of the orchards, for which a net profit of three times the cost is obtained.
