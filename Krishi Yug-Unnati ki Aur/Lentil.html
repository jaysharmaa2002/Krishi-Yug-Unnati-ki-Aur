Lentil

Lentil-Lentils are one of the most important protein rich pulse crops of the Rabi season. It is mostly eaten as a lentil, divided into 2 cotbels, deep orange-red or orange-yellow. Lentil crop is more tolerant to low temperature, drought and moisture than gram and peas. It is mainly done in Uttar Pradesh, Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh and Bihar and in all the states of India. India is the largest producer of pulses in the world.

The plant varies from 15 to 45 cm (6 to 18 inches) in height and has many long ascending branches. The compound leaves are alternate, with six pairs of oblong-linear leaflets about 15 mm (0.5 inch) long and ending in a spine. Two to four pale blue flowers are borne in the axils of the leaves in June or early July.There are many cultivated varieties of the plant, differing in size, hairiness, and colour of the leaves, flowers, and seeds. The seeds may be more or less compressed in shape and can be white, yellow, orange, tan, green, gray, or dark brown in colour; they are also sometimes mottled or speckled.


Time of sowing In India, lentils are sown from the first week of October to the first week of November if moisture is available after harvesting the Kharif crop in the non-irrigated stage. And in irrigated condition, sowing can be done from mid-October to mid-November.

Distance Seeds should be sown in lines 22 cm apart. Under late sowing conditions, the row-spacing should be reduced to 20 cm.

Seed depth Sow at a depth of 3-4 cm at sowing depth.

Sowing Method For sowing method use pora method or seed cum fertilizer drill. Seeds can also be sown by manual broadcasting.

Seed Quantity
Small grain variety 40 to 45 kg per hectare.
Large grain variety 55 to 60 kg per hectare.
60 to 80 kg per hectare in waterlogged area.

Seed treatment Before sowing, seed should be treated with Captan or Thiram @ 3 g/kg of seed.

Land selection Lentils can be grown on all types of soil. Saline, alkaline or waterlogged soils should be avoided. The soil should be friable and weed free so that the seeds can be placed at the same depth.

Farm Preparation Lentil cultivation requires less tillage to prepare seed beds in light soils. In heavy soils, one deep plowing is followed by cross ploughing with 3-4 harrows. 2-3 ploughings are sufficient to level the land. The land should be flat for proper distribution of water. Proper moisture should be present in the field at the time of sowing the seeds.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers Use organic manure or compost manure for good production of lentils. In chemical fertilizer, apply Nitrogen 5 kg, Phosphorus 8 kg per acre at the time of sowing. The seeds should be treated with Rhizobium before sowing. If the seeds are not treated with Rhizobium before sowing, the amount of phosphorus should be doubled.

Pest and Disease Prevention Foliar borer- In case of infestation of this pest in lentil cultivation, spray the solution with Profenophos 50 EC, 2 ml per liter of water or Imamectin benzoate 5SG at the rate of 0.2 g per liter of water.

Mahu (Aphid)- To prevent this pest, spray Dimethoate 30 EC at the rate of 1.7 ml per liter of water or Imidachloroprid 17.8 SL at the rate of 0.2 ml per liter of water as soon as the outbreak starts.

Rust-To protect against this kit, make 0.2 percent (2 grams per liter of water) solution of Mancozeb 75 WP fungicide on the crop and spray it after 50 days of sowing and after 10 to 12 days according to the need.

Ukatha (Wilt)-Before sowing, sow the seed after treating the seed with Thiram and Carbendazim (2:1) at 3 grams per kg of seed, Ukatha resistant and tolerant varieties like Pant Masoor-5, IPL- 316, R Grow VL- 31, Shekhar Masoor-2, Shekhar Masoor-3 etc.

Weed control The main weeds found in lentils are Chenopodium album (Bathua), Vicia sativa (Akri), Lathyrus spp. (Chatimetri) etc. They can be controlled with 2 hoe at an interval of 30 and 60 days. Weed free period of 45-60 days should be maintained for proper crop growth and yield. Pre-emergence application of Stomp 30EC @ 550ml/acre with a spade 50 days after sowing, helps in effective weed control.

Irrigation Lentil is mainly grown as a rainfed crop. Irrigated conditions require 2-3 irrigations depending on climatic conditions. One irrigation should be provided after 4 weeks of sowing and the other at flowering stage. Pod formation and flower initiation are important stages of water requirement.

Harvesting Harvesting should be done at the appropriate time when the plants are dry and the pods are mature. Avoid over-ripening of pods as the yield may be lost due to scattering. The crop is also removed by beating it with a stick.

Storage After threshing, the seeds are cleaned, and dried in the sun. The moisture content at the time of storage should be 12%. According to the variety, 8-10 quintals and 15-16 quintals per hectare lentil yield is obtained in water-rich areas.
