Maize

Maize- Maize is a major food crop, which comes under the category of coarse cereals. It is the form of a maize or corn. The states where maize is widely cultivated in India are Andhra Pradesh, Bihar, Karnataka, Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh etc. Out of these, Rajasthan has the largest area of maize and Andhra has the highest production. But the importance of maize is also very high in Jammu and Kashmir, Himachal, Northeast states, Madhya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, Maharashtra, Gujarat and Jharkhand. More than 80% of the total maize production Andhra Pradesh (20.9%), Karnataka (16.5%), Rajasthan (9.9%), Maharashtra (9.1%), Bihar (8.9%), Uttar Pradesh (6.1%), Madhya Pradesh (5.7%), Himachal Pradesh (4.4%) etc. Happens in the states. Now maize has got recognition in many forms like corn, pop corn, sweet corn, baby corn etc. Where is this diversity found in any other crop? Maize cultivation is prevalent in many countries of the world, in which the United States of America, China and Brazil are ranked first, second and third respectively in the world in terms of area and production.


Famous varieties :- Hybrid Species - Ganga-1, Ganga-4, Ganga-11, Deccan-107, KH-510, DHM-103, DHM-109, Him-129, Pusa Early Ha-1 & 2, Vivek Ha-4, DHM- 15 etc. Composite Species - Narmada Moti, Jawahar Maize-216, Sandalwood Maize-1, 2 and 3, Sandalwood White Maize-2, Pusa Composite-1, 2 and 3, Mahi Kanchan, Arun, Kiran, Jawahar Maize-8, 12 and 216 , Prabhat, Navjot etc.

Seed Quantity:-

  • Hybrid Species :- 12 to 15 kg/ha.
  • Composite species:- 15 to 20 kg/ha.
  • For green fodder :- 40 to 45 kg/ha.(According to small or big grains also the quantity of seeds is more or less.)


Maize sowing time:
1. Kharif: June to July.
2. Rabi: October to November.
3. Zayed: February to March.

Seed Treatment :- Fungicide seed treatment- Before sowing, treat the seed with thiram or carbendazim at the rate of 3 grams per kg of seed, mix them in water and make a wet paste and apply it on the seed. Insecticide seed treatment- Seed treatment with insecticide is necessary to protect the seeds and new plants from sucking sap and insects living in the soil. Treat the seed with thiomethoxam or imidacloprid @ 1 to 2 g/kg of seed.
Seed treatment with biological vaccine- After treatment with fungicide and insecticide, sow the seed immediately after treating the seed with Azotobacter @ 5 gm/kg of seed.

Sowing Method:- Seed can be done by digging a pit by hand or by making a bund with the help of tractor and seed drill in modern way. Seeds should be planted 3-4 cm apart. Sow deeply. Sweet corn sowing 2.5 cm Do it in depth.

Above land :- Maize can be cultivated in all types of land. But for good growth and productivity of maize, loamy and medium to heavy soil with adequate amount of biomass and proper drainage system is suitable. For this, such land where there is good drainage of water is suitable.

Field preparation: For the cultivation of maize, before sowing, the field should be plowed thoroughly 2-3 times with a native plow or cultivator, so that the soil becomes friable, then after this, prepare the field for sowing by running the pad.

Favorable Climate :- Maize is a warm season plant. The suitable temperature for germination is 21C and for growth is 32C.

Fertilizers and Chemicals :- While preparing the land for the cultivation of maize, 5 to 8 tonnes of well-rotted cow dung should be mixed in the field and after soil test, where there is a shortage of zinc, 25 kg/ha zinc sulphate should be applied before rain. Apply Phosphorus 75-150 kg, Urea 75-110 kg and Potash 15-20 kg per acre. Keep in mind that chemical fertilizers should be used only on the basis of soil test.

Harmful pests and their prevention:- Stem borer - For its control, apply Carbofuran 3G 20 kg or Phorate 10% CG 20 kg or Dimethoate 30% EC 1.0 ltr per hectare or Cunalphos 25 per cent EC 1.50 ltr.
Pink borer - To control it, treat seed with Carbofuron 5% w/w @ 2.5 gm/kg of seed. Apart from this, applying 4 kg Trichocard per acre after 10 days from germination can also prevent damage. Lights and pheromone cards are also used to catch moths.
Corn worm - For chemical control, mix 10 kg phorate 10 g in the field one week before sowing.
Branch pest - To control it, dimethoate 2 ml. Take spray of this mixture per liter of water.
In case of termite infestation in standing crop, apply Chlorpyriphos 20% EC @ 2.5 liters per hectare with irrigation water.
Branch fly - At the time of sowing, apply phorate 10% CG @ 5 kg per acre in the soil. In addition, Dimethoate 30 percent EC 300 ml. or Methyl Demetan 25% EC 450ml. Spray per acre.

Stem rot - To prevent this, do not let the water stand and pay attention to the drainage. Spraying at the rate of 15 grams of streptomycin or 60 grams of agrimycin and 500 grams of copper oxychloride per hectare gives more benefit. or 150 g. 100 liters to the captain. It should be mixed in water and applied on the roots.
Leaf blight - For its prevention, taking 2-4 sprays at an interval of 7-10 days after mixing Dithane M-45 or Zineb 2.0-2.5 gm per liter of water can prevent this disease at an early stage.
Brown spots on the underside of leaves - For its prevention, uproot and destroy the affected plants and take spray of Metalaxyl@1gm or Metalaxyl+Mancozeb@2.5gm/Ltr of water.
Tulasita disease - For its prevention, spraying of 80 percent zinc-manganese carbamate or geranium, two kilograms or 27 percent at the rate of three liters per hectare should be done by dissolving in the required quantity of water.

Weed control :- After 15-20 days of sowing, weeding should be done by running string or chemical insecticide called Atrazine should be used. For use of Atrazine, spray at the rate of 600-800 g/acre before germination. After this, after about 25-30 days, offer soil.

Irrigation :- Maize is mostly grown in areas where annual rainfall is between 60 cm to 110 cm. Zaid crop needs more irrigation. If irrigation is not done at the right time, there is a huge reduction in the yield. Apply first irrigation 15 to 20 days after sowing. After that, irrigation must be done when the crop is up to the knee, when the male flowers emerge, at the time of corn formation and while filling the grain. Crop needs 5-6 irrigations.

Harvesting and Threshing :- The crop should be harvested when the leaves covering the corn become yellow or brown and the moisture content of the grains is less than 30 percent, the plant remains green after cutting the corn, use it for animal feed.
After harvesting, the most important work in the maize crop is threshing, in which the cellar is used to extract the grains. In the absence of a cellar, maize can be threshed by improving the ordinary thresher, in which there is no need to remove the corn kernels. When the corn is dried directly, threshing can be done by putting it in the thresher, and there is no erosion of the grain.

Storage :- The grains obtained after harvesting and threshing should be dried thoroughly in the sun and stored. If the grains are to be used for seeds, then dry them so that the moisture content is about 12 percent. And also store it in a moisture-free place. Production Capacity: Gives yield of 10 to 20 quintals per acre. With the hybrid we can get yield up to 40 quintals per acre.
