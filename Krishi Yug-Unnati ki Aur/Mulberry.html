Mulberry

Mulberry- The primary use of mulberry leaves is to feed silkworms. Mulberry has wide medicinal uses such as for making blood tonic, for dizziness, constipation, tinnitus, urinary incontinence etc. It is also used to make fruit juice which is very popular in Korea, Japan and China.It is a perennial plant with an average height of 40-60 feet. The flowers are compactly arranged which bears purple black colored fruits. Punjab, Haryana, Himachal Pradesh, West Bengal, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh and Tamil Nadu are the major mulberry growing states of India.It is also cultivated for rearing silk worms and for fruits. Strong fiber is obtained from the branches and baskets are made from them. It is also used as firewood and fodder.

Mulberry trees come in a variety of shapes and sizes, but all produce the clustered mulberry fruit. Most species were imported to the United States from Asia, but now thrive and are even considered invasive in some areas. Some cities in North America, such as El Paso, Texas and Phoenix, Arizona have banned the planting of new mulberry trees due to the amount of pollen they produce.

The height of mulberry trees varies significantly depending on the variety. The white mulberry can tower up to 80 feet, while the black mulberry barely reaches 30 feet, and often grows as a bush if not trained. The red mulberry lies somewhere in between, with a maximum height of about 70 feet in good conditions. Mulberry trees are fast growers when young but slow as they age. All mulberry varieties are deciduous. The white mulberry has thin, very light green lobed or unlobed leaves, with a drooping or upright form.


Crop Variety S-36: This variety has heart shaped leaves which are thick and light green in colour. The average yield of mulberry leaves is 15,000-18,000 kg/acre. The leaves contain high moisture and nutrients.
V-1:The variety is released in 1997. This variety has oval and broad shaped leaves which are dark green in colour. The average yield of mulberry leaves is 20,000-24,000 kg/acre.

Time of sowing Mulberry is mainly planted in the month of July - August. The nursery for plantation is well prepared in the month of June - July.

Distance The distance between the plants is 90 cm. x 90 cm keep.

Seed Depth 90 cm in the pit Sowing should be done at a depth of

Seed quantity Use seed rate of 4 kg/acre.

Seed Treatment Firstly the seeds are stored for 90 days in a cool place. After 90 days of storage the seeds are soaked in water for 4 days instead of water after 2 days. The seeds are then placed in a paper towel to remain moist. When seeds are observed to germinate, they are sown in nursery beds.

Earth Loamy soil and loamy soil are more suitable for mulberry cultivation. pH of soil 6.5 to 7.0 is more favorable. Lime is added if the soil is acidic. If the soil is alkaline then gypsum is added.

Farm Preparation Plow the field properly and make the field friable, level and weed free.

Favorable Climate Temperature - 24-28 C
Rainfall - 600-2500 mm
Sowing temperature - 35-40 C
Harvesting temperature - 35-45 C

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers Use well decomposed cow dung at the rate of 25 metric ton/ha/per year. Apply NPK @ 350:140:140/ha/per year from second year onwards.

Weed Control Do a light digging one month after planting to eliminate weeds and air circulation. Do two more light digging and weeding at an interval of one month.

Irrigation Irrigation is given once a week @ 80-120 mm. Drip irrigation is required whenever there is a shortage of water in that area. Drip irrigation saves 40% of water.

Harvest Period The first crop should be taken six months after planting once the mulberries are well established.

Harvest Time Harvesting is mainly done when the fruit turns dark red in colour. It is preferred that harvesting is done in the early morning hours. Harvesting is mainly done by hand lifting method or shaking method. In the shivering method, the tree is shaken by placing a cotton or plastic sheet under the tree. Almost all ripe mulberries will rain on cotton or plastic sheets. Fully ripe fruits are used for processing.

Production Capability Normally the expected annual yield is 40-50 tonnes of leaves per acre.
