Bay Leaf

Bay Leaf- If you are interested in the cultivation of bay leaf, then you can very easily cultivate it and earn better. It is called 'Bay Leaf' in English. It is a kind of dry and fragrant leaf. Its fresh and dried leaves are mostly used as spices to make food fragrant and tasty. It is being produced for years.

Its main producing countries are India, Russia, Central America, Italy, France, North America and Belgium etc. It is also rich in medicinal properties, people use bay leaves and cinnamon in tea for weight loss. Most of the production of bay leaves in our country is done in Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, Kerala, Karnataka besides hilly regions of North East India.

If eaten whole, bay leaves are pungent and have a sharp, bitter taste. As with many spices and flavourings, the fragrance of the bay leaf is more noticeable than its taste. When dried the aroma is herbal, slightly floral, and somewhat similar to oregano and thyme. Myrcene, a component of many essential oils used in perfumery, can be extracted from the bay leaf. They also contain eugenol.

The Canadian government requires that the bay leaves contain no more than 4.5% total ash material with a maximum of 0.5% of which is insoluble in hydrochloric acid. To be considered dried it has to contain 7% moisture or less. The oil content cannot be less than 1 milliliter per 100 grams of the spice.


Time of sowing Tejpatta is sown in the month of June-July.

Sowing method Its sowing can be done by direct seed and by adopting cutting method.

Planting Method Plants prepared by seeds in nursery and vegetative propagation methods (pen method) are planted in the selected field at 50 cm. Digging deep pits and filling them with a mixture of surface soil and manure or compost. The pit is 3x3 m. are dug at a distance. Then in the month of June-July, seedlings are planted in these pits from nursery or branches.

Favorable climate For this it can be grown successfully in hot climate or mild frosty areas.

Land Selection It is mainly grown on barren and rocky lands. By the way, now it is grown in all types of soil, whose pH value is between 6-8. The soil should be dry and rich in organic matter.

Farm preparation Before planting, the soil should be made friable and level by deep plowing of the field, the field should be weed free and proper drainage system should be kept. After that the pit should be dug.

Fertilizers and chemical Fertilizers For proper growth of the plant, manure and fertilizer should be given, 20 kg of cow dung, 20 grams of nitrogen, 18 grams of phosphorus and 25 grams of potash should be given per plant. After distributing the amount of fertilizer in equal parts, one part should be given in the month of September-October and the other part with full quantity of cow dung in the month of May-June. The amount of manure and fertilizer should be increased every year as per the requirement.

Weed control To control weeds, weeding should be done as per the requirement.

Irrigation Irrigate immediately after planting. And after that irrigation should be done as per the requirement.

Harvesting sorting Its timely pruning leads to complete development of trees. Therefore, pruning is necessary from time to time. By the way, the need for sorting it separately is rarely required.

Leaf plucking The bay leaf plant is an evergreen plant, in which leaves keep coming out throughout the year, after harvesting the leaves, they can be dried and used.

Production The yield of bay leaves depends on their plants, about 6 tons of leaves can be obtained from plants planted in one hectare.
