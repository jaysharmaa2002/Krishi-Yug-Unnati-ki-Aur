Guava

Guava- Guava (Jamphal) is one of the common but important commercial fruit crop in India. It is a rich source of calcium and phosphorus as well as vitamin C and pectin. It is the fourth most important crop after mango, banana and citrus. It can be grown all over India. Guava is mainly cultivated in these states of Bihar, Uttar Pradesh, Maharashtra, Karnataka, Orissa, West Bengal, Andhra Pradesh and Tamil Nadu.

Guava is a common tropical fruit cultivated in many tropical and subtropical regions.[2] Psidium guajava (common guava, lemon guava) is a small tree in the myrtle family (Myrtaceae), native to Mexico, Central America, the Caribbean and northern South America.Although related species may also be called guavas, they belong to other species or genera, such as the pineapple guava, Feijoa sellowiana. In 2019, 55 million tonnes of guavas were produced worldwide, led by India with 45% of the total. Botanically, guavas are berries.

Guava was adopted as a crop in subtropical and tropical Asia, parts of the United States (from Tennessee and North Carolina, southward, as well as the west and Hawaii), tropical Africa, South Asia, Southeast Asia, and Oceania.Guavas were introduced to Florida, US in the 19th century and are grown there as far north as Sarasota, Chipley, Waldo and Fort Pierce. However, they are a primary host of the Caribbean fruit fly and must be protected against infestation in areas of Florida where this pest is present.Guavas are of interest to home growers in subtropical areas as one of the few tropical fruits that can grow to fruiting size in pots indoors. When grown from seed, guava trees can bear fruit in two years, and can continue to do so for forty years.


Crop Variety
Allahabad Safeda: Dwarf variety with rounded crown and spreading branches, fruit smooth, round and flesh is white in color with pleasant taste. TSS ranges from 10-12%. Gives an average yield of 145 kg per tree.

Arka Amulya: Dwarf variety with compact, rounded crown with dense foliage, fruit is large, smooth, round and white flesh. TSS ranges from 9.3 to 10.1%. Gives an average yield of 144 kg per tree.

Sardar: Also known as L-49. Dwarf variety with spreading branches, large fruit with rough surface. Flesh is creamy white, smooth, juicy with rich taste. TSS ranges from 10-12%. Gives an average yield of 130-155 kg per tree.

Punjab Safeda: It has creamy and white flesh. The fruit has a sugar content of 13.4% and is 0.62% sour.

Shweta: It has creamy white flesh. The fruit has a sucrose content of 10.5-11.0%. It gives an average yield of 151 kg per tree.

Nigsky: It gives an average yield of 80 kg per tree.

Punjab Sheetal: It gives an average yield of 85 kg per tree.

Allahabad Surkha: Seedless variety, large fruit with uniform pink flesh.

Apple Guava: Pink colored medium sized fruit, with good quality keeping, the fruit has a sweet taste.

Spotted: A popular variety of Uttar Pradesh, the fruits are similar to the Allahabad Safeda variety except these fruits have red spots on the skin. Its TSS content is higher than that of Allahabad Safeda and L 49 varieties.

Time of sowing The months of February-March or August-September are the most favorable time for planting guava.

Distance Keep a distance of 6x5 meters for planting saplings. If the plants are planted in a square manner, then keep the distance of the plants 7 meters. 132 saplings are planted per acre.

Seed Depth 25 cm to the roots. should be sown at a depth of

Sowing Method By direct sowing, planting in the field, planting cuttings, planting cheese.

Earth All types of soils are suitable for the production of guava, ranging from light to heavy and low-draining soils. It can also be grown in soil having pH of 6.5 to 7.5.

Farm Preparation Plow the field properly and make the field friable, level and weed free. Prepare the field in such a way that water does not stand in it.

Favorable Climate 1. The temperature required is around 15-30C.
2. Rainfall required is 100 cm.
3. The optimum sowing temperature is 15-20C, 25-30C.
4. The optimum temperature for harvesting is 20-25C, 18-22C.

Fertilizer (kg/acre)
-When the crop is 1-3 years old, apply well prepared cow dung @ 10-25 kg per tree with Urea @ 155-200 g, SSP @ 500-1600 g and Potash 100-400 g per tree.
- For 4-6 year old crop, apply cowdung @ 25-40 kg, Urea @ 300-600 gm, SSP @ 1500-2000 gm, Potash @ 600 gm-1000 gm per tree.

-When the crop is 7-10 years old, apply cow dung @ 40-50 kg, urea @ 750-1000 g, SSP @ 2000-2500 g and MOP @ 1100-1500 g per tree.
-When the crop age is more than 10 years, apply cow dung @ 50 kg per tree, Urea @ 1000 g, SSP @ 2500 g and MOP @ 1500 g per tree. Apply half dose of
-Urea, SSP and MOP and full dose of cowdung in the month of May, June and remaining half dose in September-October.

Weed control Weed control is essential for good production in guava cultivation. To check weed growth, apply paraquat dichloride 24% sl 6 ml in the month of March, July and September. per liter of water and spray it. After weed germination, take spray of Glyphosate 1.6 Ltr in 200 Ltr of water (before weeds flower and reach 15 to 20 cm in height) per acre.

Irrigation After planting, irrigate the crop immediately, then on the third day, irrigate later depending on soil type and climate. Irrigation is not required for well established orchards. Young seedlings need irrigation at weekly intervals during summer month and 2-3 irrigations in winter month. Avoid excessive irrigation during flowering stage as it prevents excessive flowering.

Harvest period In guava cultivation, grafted plants give effect at the age of 3 years and the peak harvesting period is August-September for rainy season crop and January-February. For a winter season crop, guavas develop the best flavor and aroma only when they are ripe on the tree.

Harvest time Fruit bearing occurs within 2-3 years after planting. Harvesting should be done when the fruit is mature. When ripe, the fruit changes color from dark green to greenish yellow. Harvest at proper time and avoid over ripening of fruits as it deteriorates the quality and testiness.

Production Capability In Guava, the yield from grafted plants is 350 kg and that of seed plants is 90 kg per tree. Yields are low in the initial years, i.e., a two-year-old guava plant yields 4 or 5 kg. In high density planting, the yield is 75 kg per tree.

Cleaning and Drying After harvesting, perform cleaning, grading and packing operations. Since guava is a short-lived fruit, its crop should be brought to the market immediately after harvesting. Use CFB, corrugated fiber box or different size bamboo baskets for packing.
