Almonds

Almonds-The almond tree, Prunus dulcis, is a deciduous tree in the family Rosaceae that is grown for its edible seeds (nuts). The tree has gray or brown bark and is either a variegated or a growth habit depending on the variety. The trunk can reach 30 cm (12 in) in diameter. The leaves of the almond are 7.5-13 cm (3-5 in) long with serrated edges, and grow alternately on the branches. The tree produces white to pinkish flowers and hairy green fruits that are oblong in shape. The fruit is a drunkard,Contains only one seed. The seed is protected by a hard brown shell. At maturity, the flesh of the fruit becomes leathery and splits to reveal the nut inside. Nuts typically measure 3.5 to 6 cm (1.4-2.4 in) in length. Almond trees can reach heights between 4 and 10 meters (13-33 ft) and have a commercial lifespan of between 30 and 40 years.Almonds can be referred to by variety and include bitter almonds. Almonds are usually tall and may also be. Known as sweet or bitter almonds depending on variety and hardiness, from wild species found in Central and Southwest Asia.


Improved varieties of Almonds California Pepper Sail, Non Peril, Drake, Thinold, IXL, Nipples Ultra, are predominantly almond varieties.

Seed Treatment If you are growing your almond tree from a seed (which is the almonds surrounded by their protective husks), start by germinating the seeds in a controlled environment. Once it starts, you can plant it in a pot or in the ground. First, collect your seeds in a large bowl (the more you use, the better - some may not germinate or may be prone to mold). Then, germinate them according to the following steps:

  • Add water and let the seeds soak overnight.
  • The next day, use a nutcracker to slightly crack the skin of the almonds.

Earth Light loamy soil with proper drainage is good for the plant. It yields quite well. Waterlogged land is not suitable for orange cultivation. The pH value of the land should be around 5 to 8.

Farm Preparation Do two to three good slant plowing in the field through cultivator. After plowing, make the field level by putting a pad. Almond plants, once planted, give yields for about 40 to 50 years. For this reason, the plowing of its fields is done only once in the beginning.

Climate Almonds grow best in Mediterranean climates with hot, dry summers and mild, wet winters. The optimum temperature for their growth is between 15 and 30 C (60-85 F) and the cool requirement of between 7.2 C (45 F) to 300 to 600 hours to break the tree's bud dormancy. it occurs.
Almond trees will grow best when planted in deep, well-drained loam, although they can withstand drought and grow in poor soil. Trees benefit from being planted in areas sheltered from frost and wind because the trees bloom early and are therefore susceptible to late frost damage. Trees usually develop after 3 to 4 years of fall with a crop of nuts.

Manures and Fertilizers The almond tree is a heavy feeder. For this, it requires and recommends a good amount of manure and fertilizers. It is best to apply well-rotted field yard manure (fim) at the rate of 20/25 kg per tree to each tree during winter season. Fertilizer should be applied based on leaf nutrient analysis and soil test. Generally, the following recommendation can be generally adopted depending on the age of the almond tree should be applied in 2 to 3 divided doses. The first half dose should be applied with DAP (complete) and MOP (complete) a fortnight before bloom is expected, a second dose (1/4th) of urea three weeks after fruit set and 3 doses of urea Should be implemented (1/4th) should be implemented in May-June. Spray of urea 1.5-2% urea can also be used for roaming after flowering in next stage season.

Disease and Pest Prevention Since almond is a kernel class crop, pest and disease attack on it is similar to that of peach.

Weed Control First weeding after 10 to 15 days, second weeding after 25 to 35 days, third weeding after 45 days, according to the weed density, 2-3 weeding should be done manually.

Irrigation Management In small almond orchards, irrigation should be given at an interval of 10 days in summer and at an interval of 20 to 25 days in winter. The drip method should be used in high and low rows. Irrigation is very necessary in summer for good results in fruit bearing plants. This eliminates the problem of drop of fruits and gives good results for the next year.Leaves, straw and other organic and inorganic materials can be used on the soil around the trees. Due to this transpiration of water is less, weeds come out less.
