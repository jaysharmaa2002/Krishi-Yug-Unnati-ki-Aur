Cotton

Cotton- Cotton cultivation is one of the most important fiber and cash crops of India. Cotton is grown almost all over the world. This cotton cultivation provides basic raw material to the textile industry. Cotton cultivation in India provides direct livelihood to about 6 million farmers and employs 40 to 5 million people in its trade or processing. Cotton is also a means of earning for many people.
Cotton cultivation in our country is also called white gold. There is a need for large scale cotton production in the country. Because the importance of cotton can be gauged from these works, it is made of cloth, its oil is extracted and its viola is widely used in animal feed without fiber.
Cotton is classified into three categories:-
If seen, cotton with long fiber is considered the best, whose length is 5 cm, it is included in high quality items. Cotton with a medium fiber length of 3.5 to 5 cm is called blended cotton. The third type of cotton is short fiber. Whose length is 3.5 cm.


Seed Quantity:- The amount of seed depends a lot on the type of seed, the area to be grown, irrigation etc. For American hybrid cotton, 1.5 - 2 kg per acre whereas for American cotton the seed quantity should be 3.5 - 4 kg per acre. The seed quantity should be 1.25 - 2 kg per acre for hybrid varieties of indigenous cotton and 3 - 4 kg per acre for indigenous varieties of cotton.

Seed Treatment :- To avoid seed borne disease, soak the seeds in 10 liters of water in a solution of one gram streptocycline or 2.5 grams of agrimycin and dry it for 8 to 10 hours, after that take it for sowing.
Where root rot disease is prevalent, treat Trichoma harzonium or Pseudomonas fluorescens @ 10 g per kg seed or chemical fungicide like Carboxin 70 WP, 3 g per kg seed or Carbendazim 50 WP 2 g per kg seed. Or treat Thiram at the rate of 3 grams per kg of seed.
Soak the seeds overnight in water to remove fiber naturally, then crush the seeds the next day with dung and wood shavings or ash. Then dry the seeds in shade before sowing.
The chemical method depends on the fiber of the seed. Mix pure sulfuric acid (industrial grade) at 400 g per 4 kg seed for American cotton and 300 g per 3 kg seed for indigenous cotton for 2-3 minutes, this will remove all the fiber from the seeds. Then add 10 liters of water to the pot containing the seeds and drain the water by shaking well. Wash the seeds thrice with plain water and then with lime water (sodium bicarbonate 50 g per 10 liters of water) for one minute. Then wash once again and dry in the shade.
Do not use metal or wooden utensils for the chemical method, but use plastic utensils or earthen pots. Make sure to use gloves while doing this action.
Treating one kilogram of non-fibre seed with 5 grams of imidacloprid 70 WS or 4 grams of thiomethoxam 70 WS can reduce leaf nectar sucking harmful insects and leaf twist virus.
For sowing cotton in unirrigated conditions, the yield is increased by treating the seed with 10 grams of Ejectobacter culture per kilogram of seed.

Time of sowing :- The appropriate time for sowing cotton varies from region to region. Sowing of this crop must be done in the month of April and May.

Method of sowing seeds:- For sowing of indigenous cotton, use seeding machine and for hybrid or Bt varieties, dig pit sowing. Square sowing is more beneficial than rectangular. Due to non-germination of some seeds and due to destruction, the distance increases in many places. It is necessary to close this gap. Destroy weak, diseased and affected plants two weeks after sowing.

Distance :- 75x15cm under irrigated condition for American cotton. and 60x30 cm in rainfed condition. Keep the distance. For Desi cotton, keep a distance of 60x30 in irrigated and rainfed condition.

Earth :- Sandy loam soil and black soil are considered most suitable for cotton cultivation. The yield of cotton is high in this type of soil. But today many types of hybrid varieties have come in the market. Due to which today cotton is being grown easily even in hilly and sandy places. For this the pH of the ground The value should be from 5.5 to 6. Cotton cultivation requires less water. For this reason its crop should be done in well drained soil.

Land preparation: After harvesting the Rabi crop, a deep plowing should be done with the soil turning plow. After this, the soil of cultivation should be made fine and friable by running the harrow two or three times. Thus the field becomes ready for sowing.

Temperature :- It is advisable to have at least 16 degree centigrade for the crop to grow and the ideal temperature for germination to be 32 to 34 degree centigrade. It needs a temperature of 21 to 27 degrees for its growth. At the time of fruiting, day temperature should be 25 to 30 degree centigrade and nights should be cool.

Rain :- At least 75 cm of rainfall is required for cotton. Rainfall of more than 125 cm is harmful.

Fertilizers and Fertilizers :- Before sowing of cotton, while preparing the field, 15-20 tonnes of well decomposed cow dung should be mixed well in the soil at the rate of 15-20 tonnes per hectare.American and Bt varieties require 75 kg of nitrogen and 35 kg of phosphorus per hectare and 50 kg of nitrogen and 25 kg of phosphorus per hectare for indigenous varieties. Give potash fertilizer on the basis of soil test, full amount of phosphorus and potash and half quantity of nitrogen should be given before sowing. The remaining half quantity of nitrogen should be given at the time of flower buds formation.

Harmful kits and diseases and their prevention :- :- Green mosquito- For its control, spray appropriate amount of Imidacloprid 17.8 SL or Monocrotophos 36 SL on the plants.

Oily - For its prevention, spray appropriate amount of Imidacloprid 17.8 SL or Thiomethacam 25 WG on the plants.

Thrips - Methyl demetan 25 EC 160 ml for the prevention of thrips. Buprofenazine 25% SC 350ml Take spray of any one of Fipronil 5% SC 200-300 ml, Imidacloprid 70 per cent WG 10-30 ml, Thymethoxam 25 per cent WC 30 gm in 200 liters of water per acre.

White fly - For its control, take Acetamiprid 4 gm or Acephate 75WP 800 gm per 200 ltr of water or Imidacloprid 40 ml. Spraying of M/s.@200Ltr of water or Thiamethoxam@40gm/acre in 200Ltr of water.

Mealy bug - Quinalphos 25 EC 5 ml is used for its prevention. or Chlorpyrifos 20 EC 3 ml. Spray at the rate of 1 liter of water.

Pied caterpillar - American caterpillar - Tobacco caterpillar - Pink caterpillar :- For its prevention, Quinalphos 25 EC, 2.0 ml per liter or Indoxacarb 14.5 SC, 1.0 ml per liter or Spinosad 45 SC, 0.33 ml per liter or Flubendiamide 480 S. c, Spray any one insecticide at the rate of 0.40 ml per liter of water.

Harmful diseases -
Scorch disease - For its prevention, spraying of camphor oxychloride should be done on the plant.

Plant blight disease - For its prevention, spray Entracal or Mancozeb by mixing it in 40 grams/15 liters of water.

For the prevention of Alternaria leaf spot disease - Tabuconazole 1 ml. Or take spray of Triflocitrobin + Tabuconazole 0.6 gm/Litre on 60th, 90th and 120th day after sowing. If disease is observed in field then take spray of Copper oxychloride or Captan@500gm/200Ltr of water per acre or Carbendazim 12% + Mancozeb 63%WP @25gm/10Ltr of water.

Root rot disease - For its prevention, it is necessary to treat the seed.

Weed control :- The first weeding should be done within 15 to 20 days of germination by running Kolpa or Dora. Weedicides include Pyretobrec sodium (750 g/ha) or Fluchlorin/Pendamethalin 1 kg. The active ingredient can be used before sowing.

Irrigation :- It is necessary to give 3-4 irrigations in cotton crop. The first irrigation should be done late. Cotton cultivation requires very little water. If there is a lot of rain then it does not need initial irrigation. But if the rain is not on time, its first irrigation is needed after about 45 to 50 days. Irrigation of the field must be done at the time of sycamore and flowering.

Cotton plucking :- The first harvesting of cotton should be done when the cotton buds are 40 to 60 percent in bloom. After that second harvesting is done after all the twigs bloom. Cotton picking is completed in 3-4 times.

Production :- If cultivated with the above improved method, 20 to 25 quintals of indigenous cotton, 25 to 32 of hybrid cotton and 30 to 50 quintals per hectare of Bt cotton can be obtained.
