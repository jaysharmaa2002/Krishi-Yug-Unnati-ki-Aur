Coconut

Coconut- Along with being an adorable fruit, coconut is also very useful in daily life. It has cultural as well as economic importance in India. It is associated with the life of small farmers of India. Every part of this tree is useful. Coconut fruit is useful for drinks, food and oil.

The peel of the fruit is useful in various industrial works and the leaves and wood are also useful. Due to these utilities, coconut is called Kalpavriksha. In India, it is cultivated in Kerala, Maharashtra, Goa, West Bengal, Orissa and coastal areas.


Varieties: - Coconut varieties are divided into two categories, known as tall and dwarf varieties. The improved variety from the version of these two varieties is known as hybrid variety.

In tall variety subspecies, West Coast tall species, East Coast tall species, Tiptur tall species, Andaman tall species, Andaman Giant and Lakshadweep ordinary are prominent.

Chavakkad Orange and Chavakkad Green are prevalent among the dwarf varieties. The main hybrid varieties are Lakshganga, Keraganga and Anand Ganga.coconut, edible fruit of the coconut palm (Cocos nucifera), a tree of the palm family (Arecaceae). Coconuts probably originated somewhere in Indo-Malaya and are one of the most important crops of the tropics. Coconut flesh is high in fat and can be dried or eaten fresh or processed into coconut milk or coconut oil. The liquid of the nut, known as coconut water, is used in beverages.


Planting time The suitable time for planting coconut is from June to September, but where irrigation is available, the plant can be planted at any time except during winter and heavy rains.

Planting Method While selecting quality plants, keep in mind that the middle age should be between 9-18 months and have at least 5-7 green leaves. The circumference of the neck part of the biceps is 10-12 cm. Yes and the plants are disease free. In the month of April-May, make pits of 1x1x1 meter size at a distance of 7.5x7.5 meters. The pit is kept open till the first rain, after the rain, mix manure or compost in the surface soil and fill it in such a way that 20 cm from the top. Keep the pit empty.

Plant Care Proper shade and irrigation system is necessary to protect the newly planted birches from heat and frost. After planting, keep the stem and the neck of the seednut open for 2 years. Irrigation as per requirement is very important to maintain the productivity of adult coconut trees.

Favorable climate The tropical and sub-tropical climate is essential for the good growth and fruiting of coconut plants, but it can also be grown successfully in areas where the lowest temperature is less than 10 C and the maximum temperature does not last more than 40 C for a long period. could.

Land selection Moist soil with good drainage capacity is useful for coconut crop cultivation, pH of the soil. be between 5.2 to 8.8. Except black and rocky soils, all such soils which do not have rock in the lower surface are suitable for coconut cultivation. The sandy loam soil is considered the best.

Farm preparation For coconut cultivation, pits of size 1 X 1 X 1 m are made at a distance of 7.5 X 7.5 m (25 X 25 ft) at the selected site by keeping the field weed free. The pit is kept open till the first rain, which is filled by mixing 30 kg of cow dung or compost and surface soil in such a way that 20 cm from the top. Keep the pit empty. After planting saplings from the remaining soil, a ridge is made around the pit so that rain water does not collect in the pit.

Fertilizers and chemical Fertilizers Regular application of fertilizers and fertilizers in recommended quantity is necessary for long period of high production. Use 30-40 kg of decomposed dung manure or compost in June every year at the time of first rainfall to adult coconut plants. Apart from this, use proper amount of urea, single super phosphate and muriate of potash.

Weed control Regular weeding and plowing are necessary to get more production and control of weeds in coconut garden. Due to these activities, air circulation is maintained in the roots of plants.

Irrigation Drip method is best and suitable for irrigation of coconut plants. Because through drip irrigation method the plant gets proper amount of water. Due to which the plant develops well and there is a difference in the yield. During the summer season, the plant must be watered at an interval of three days. Whereas in winter season one irrigation in a week is sufficient.

Mixed crop It is recommended to grow several mixed crops in a coconut orchard. Coconut growers get additional income from these crops. These crops should be selected keeping in mind the climatic condition and requirement of the area. Such crops as banana, pineapple, haw, chilli, sweet potato, papaya, lemon, turmeric, ginger, mosambi, tapioca, tejpat etc. can be cultivated.

Fruit picking Harvesting coconut fruits is the most difficult task. For this one has to climb to the top of the plant. It takes more than 15 months for the coconut fruit to fully mature. But even before that they are plucked. When the coconut is green in colour, it is harvested for coconut water. After the coconut is fully ripe, it turns yellow. It is right to break the coconut only after it is cooked properly. Once fully ripened, coconut is rich in fiber and oil.

Production Different varieties of coconut have different yields. In which dwarf species plants give more production. Because it starts bearing fruits only after 3 years. While the rest of the species start bearing fruit by 8 years. The average yield per hectare of different species is more than 50 quintals.
