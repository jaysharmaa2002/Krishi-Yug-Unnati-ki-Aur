Pomegranat

Pomegranat- Pomegranate is one such fruit, which is considered very beneficial in terms of health. Pomegranate seeds contain vitamin A, vitamin B-12, vitamin D, calcium, protein, potassium roughage, antioxidants named flavonoids. Eating it daily does not cause anemia, does not make cholesterol in the body, due to which the risk of heart attack is reduced, there is no stress, beneficial for the stomach and helps in preventing cancer.Pomegranate cultivation is mostly done in hot places like Maharashtra, Uttar Pradesh, Andhra Pradesh, Rajasthan, Haryana, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu and Gujarat. A pomegranate plant becomes a tree in 3-4 years and can bear fruit for 25 years.

The pomegranate (Punica granatum) is a fruit-bearing deciduous shrub in the family Lythraceae, subfamily Punicoideae, that grows between 5 and 10 m (16 and 33 ft) tall.

Young pomegranate in Side, Turkey The pomegranate was originally described throughout the Mediterranean region. It was introduced into Spanish America in the late 16th century and into California by Spanish settlers in 1769.

The fruit is typically in season in the Northern Hemisphere from October to February,[4][failed verification] and in the Southern Hemisphere from March to May. As intact sarcotestas or juice, pomegranates are used in baking, cooking, juice blends, meal garnishes, smoothies, and alcoholic beverages, such as cocktails and wine.

Pomegranates are widely cultivated throughout the Middle East and Caucasus region, north and tropical Africa, the Indian subcontinent, Central Asia, the drier parts of Southeast Asia, and the Mediterranean Basin.


Varieties:- Arkta Kandhari, Dholka Jalor Bedana, Jyoti Paper Sale, Bhagwa Ganesh, Ruby Mridula etc. are some of the major varieties of pomegranate. You can choose the variety of pomegranate according to your region for cultivation.

Time of sowing Planting its plants in February-March or August is considered appropriate.

Amount of Plants Per Hectare In the intensive planting method, planting can be done at 5 * 2 m (1,000 plants / ha), 5 * 3 m (666 saplings / ha), 4.5 * 3 (740 saplings / ha).

Sowing Method Pomegranate is sown by seed or by cuttings or by guni.

Planting Method For pomegranate cultivation, prepare pits about 60 cm long, 60 cm wide and 60 cm deep in the month of January-February or March, about 1 month before planting the saplings. The distance between 2 pits should be 4-5 meters.Leave these pits open for about 15 days and then prepare a powder of about 20 kg of cooked cow dung, 1 kg of single super phosphate, 0.50 g of chloro pyrifos and fill all the pits up to 15 cm. After 15 days, plant the sapling in it.

Favorable Climate Pomegranate is a plant of sub-tropical climate. It can be grown well in semi-arid climates. At the time of fruit growth and ripening, 40 degree centigrade temperature is suitable. Plant growth is adversely affected at temperatures below 11 degrees. Due to prolonged exposure to high temperature, the sweetness of fruits increases. It can be cultivated in places above 500 meters above sea level.

Land Selection Pomegranate can be cultivated in any type of soil. But sandy loam soil with good drainage is considered best for pomegranate cultivation.

Farm Preparation The field should be made loose and level by deep plowing of the field. After good plowing of the field, pits should be prepared for planting saplings.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers For good production of pomegranate, vermi compost or well-rotted manure should be added while preparing the pits. In pomegranate cultivation, fertilizer is given according to the age of the tree.
First year - 10 kg cow dung, 100 g Nitrogen, 50 g Phosphorous and 100 g Potash.
Second year - 20 kg cow dung, 200 g Nitrogen, 100 g Phosphorous and 200 g Potash.
3rd year - 30 kg of cow dung, 300 gm nitrogen, 150 gm phosphorus and 300 gm potash.
Fourth year - 40 kg cow dung, 400 g Nitrogen, 200 g Phosphorous and 400 g Potash.
Fifth year - 50 kg cow dung, 500 g Nitrogen, 250 g Phosphorous and 500 g Potash.
Apart from this, if needed, zinc sulphate and other tonic fertilizers can be used. Spraying of water soluble fertilizers has a good effect on the yield.

Disease and Prevention Pomegranate plants are prone to rotting insects, spray insecticides to avoid this. Keep cleanliness around the plants and keep sprinkling sulfuric acid to protect it from frost in winter.

Weed Control For the prevention of weeds in pomegranate cultivation, weeding is very important. And keep the crop weed free.

Irrigation For its high yield, it is necessary to irrigate it in the right amount. Plants should be irrigated after about 5 - 7 days in summer and about 10 - 12 days in winter. Drop-off irrigation is good for pomegranate. For drop-drop irrigation, irrigate by drip.

Fruit Picking When it is cooked properly then it should be broken. The fruits are ready for harvesting about 120 to 130 days after fruit set.

Production With its good care and advanced management, about 80 kg of fruits can be obtained from 1 tree. If the distance between 2 plants is less, then according to this, about 4800 quintals of fruits can be obtained per hectare. 8- 10 lakh rupees can be earned annually from 1 hectare.
