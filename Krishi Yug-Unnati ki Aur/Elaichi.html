Elaichi

Elaichi- Cardamom sometimes cardamon or cardamum,[1] is a spice made from the seeds of several plants in the genera Elettaria and Amomum in the family Zingiberaceae. Both genera are native to the Indian subcontinent and Indonesia. They are recognized by their small seed pods: triangular in cross-section and spindle-shaped, with a thin, papery outer shell and small, black seeds; Elettaria pods are light green and smaller, while Amomum pods are larger and dark brown.

Species used for cardamom are native throughout tropical and subtropical Asia. The first references to cardamom are found in Sumer, and in the Ayurvedic literatures of India. Nowadays it is also cultivated in Guatemala, Malaysia, and Tanzania.The German coffee planter Oscar Majus Kloffer introduced Indian cardamom to cultivation in Guatemala before World War I; by 2000, that country had become the biggest producer and exporter of cardamom in the world, followed by India.

Cardamom has a strong, unique taste, with an intensely aromatic, resinous fragrance. Black cardamom has a distinctly more smoky, though not bitter, aroma, with a coolness some consider similar to mint.Green cardamom is one of the most expensive spices by weight but little is needed to impart flavour.

It is best stored in the pod, as exposed or ground seeds quickly lose their flavour. Grinding the pods and seeds together lowers both the quality and the price. For recipes requiring whole cardamom pods, a generally accepted equivalent is 10 pods equals 1/2 teaspoons of ground cardamom.


Crop Name: Jujube (Plum)

Crop Variety: Elaichi (Cardamom) Late ripening (fourth week of March-mid April)

Sowing rate: Seeds of suitable root stock should be sown at 2 cm depth per sack. Vacancy: Rainfed - 6 x 6 m, Irrigated - 8 x 8 m, Pits of size 60 x 60 x 60 cm

Seed Treatment: Soaking the seeds in water for 24 hours increases the germination rate.

Time of sowing: The best line for sowing seeds is in tropical and subtropical climates in summer. Transplanting is done in February-March or August-September. Define the plant while growing from nursery for the purpose of transplanting.

Favorable Climate:
-The favorable temperature is 15-40 degree C.
-Rainfall requirement is 300-400 mm.
-It grows from MSL to an altitude of 1000 m.
-It is an early maturing variety in mid-November. Flowering and fruiting probably depend on the length of summer and the time when the monsoon sets in.
-The duration of flowering and fruit maturity varies from region to region. In northern India, growth begins from late June to early July with the arrival of the monsoon and matures during February to April. Flower buds are mostly produced on the current growth. Flowers start from the first week of September to mid-November.

Irrigation: Established trees usually do not require frequent irrigation. When the plant is in a dormant state, irrigation is not required. Irrigation is essential during fruit development stage. Irrigate at this stage with an interval of 3-4 weeks depending on the weather condition. Stop irrigation in the second fortnight of March.

Fertilizers and Manures:
rain based
Fertilizer (gram) - 100: 50: 50 (n2: p2o5: k2o)
Manure (Kg) - 30 FYM

Irrigated - Fertilizer (gram) - 500: 400: 400 (n2: P2o5: k20) Manure (Kg) - 60 FYM

Harvesting Time: The first harvesting is done within 2-3 years after planting. Harvesting should be done at the right stage of maturity. Avoid high ripening stage, as this reduces the quality and taste of the fruit. Harvesting is done when they are of normal size and give ripe color depending on the variety.

Yield rate: Gives an average yield of 200 kg per tree.

Cleaning and sorting: After harvesting the plum fruits, grading should be done. Large size A-grade best fruit, B-grade medium sized good colored fruit, C-grade small sized good colored fruit and D-grade all sized poorly colored fruit and exclude outer part.
