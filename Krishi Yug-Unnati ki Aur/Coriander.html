Coriander

Coriander- Since ancient times, the country of India is known in the world as "Land of Spices". Coriander seeds and leaves are used to make food fragrant and tasty. Coriander occupies an important place among the spices crops. Due to the volatile oil found in its grains, it makes food items tasty and aromatic. In India, it is mainly cultivated in Rajasthan, Andhra Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, Tamil Nadu, Bihar, Uttar Pradesh and Karnataka.A large part of the total production of coriander is sent to Sri Lanka, Japan, Singapore, Britain, America and Malaysia, which earns foreign exchange.
Coriander cultivation in Madhya Pradesh is 1,16,607 ha. which produces about 1,84,702 tonnes. Average yield is 428 kg/ha. Is. Guna, Mandsaur, Shajapur, Rajgarh, Vidisha, Chhindwara etc. are the major coriander producing districts of MP.


Improved varieties of coriander Improved varieties should be selected to get maximum production of coriander. The following varieties of coriander - Hissar Sugandha, RCR41, Kumbharaj, RCR 435, RCR 436, RCR 446, GC 2 (Gujarat Coriander 2), RCR 684, Pant Haritma, Simpo S 33, JD-1, ACR1, C S6, RCR 480, RC R728 .

Time of sowing Coriander crop is sown in Rabi season. The most suitable time for sowing coriander is 15 October to 15 November. Occasional sowing of coriander is beneficial. The suitable time for sowing coriander for grains is the first fortnight of November. For green leaf crop, the time of October to December is suitable for sowing. To avoid frost, it is appropriate to sow coriander in the second week of November.

Seed quantity 15-20 kg/ha under irrigated condition. 25-30 kg/ha in seed and unirrigated. Seeds are required.

Seed treatment For protection against soil and seed borne diseases, seed should be mixed with Carbendazim + Thiram (2:1)3 g/kg. Or Carboxin 37.5 percent + Thirum 37.5 percent 3 g/kg. and Trichoderma viridi 5g/kg. Treat at seed rate. To prevent seed borne diseases, it is beneficial to treat the seed with Streptomycin 500 ppm.

Sowing method Before sowing, after rubbing the coriander seeds lightly, break the seeds into two parts and make pulses. Sow coriander in rows with a seed drill, keep row-to-row distance of 30 cm and plant-to-plant distance 7 to 10 cm. In heavy land or more fertile land, the distance of rows should be kept 40 cm. It is more profitable to sow coriander in rows. The depth of the seed in the cod should be 2 to 4 cm. Sowing the seed at too deep a depth reduces the germination.

Land selection Good loamy land with good drainage is most suitable for irrigated crop of coriander and black heavy land is good for unirrigated crop. Coriander does not tolerate alkaline and saline soil. That is, loam or matiyar loam land with good drainage and fertility is suitable. The pH value of the soil should be 6.5 to 7.5.

Suitable Climate Dry and cool weather is favorable for the cultivation of coriander to get good production. 25 to 26 centigrade temperature is good for the germination of seeds. Coriander being a crop of temperate climate, frost free weather is required at the stage of flowering and grain formation. Coriander has a lot of damage due to frost. For high quality and more volatile oil of coriander seed, cold climate, strong sunlight for a long time, high altitude above sea and high land is required.

Farm Preparation In irrigated areas, if there is not enough water in the land at the time of plowing, then the preparation of the land should be done by palwa. Due to which lumps will not be formed in the land at the time of tillage and the weed seeds will be destroyed at the time of plowing after germination. For rainfed crop, after harvesting Kharif crop, two horizontal plowing should be done immediately after plowing.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers To get good yield of unirrigated coriander, apply well decomposed cow dung at 20 t/ha. 40 kg with Nitrogen, 30 kg. Phosphorus at the rate of 20 kg potash and 20 kg sulfur per hectare and 60 kg. Nitrogen, 40kg Sphur, 20 kg potash and 20 kg. Use sulfur at the rate of per hectare for irrigated crops. And other nutrients should be given on the basis of soil test.

Pest Control Mahu / Chepa (Aphid) - In coriander, there is mainly an outbreak of Mahu Chepa sucking insect. The light green color of this insect sucks the sap of tender parts like stems, flowers and seeds of the plant. Control- For prevention, spray Oxydemetan methyl 25 EC at 1.5 ml per liter of water or Dimethiot 35 EC 2 ml per liter of water or Imidacloprid 17.8 EC at the rate of 0.25 ml per liter of water.

Disease and Control Uktha/Ugra (Wilt)- Uktha disease is spread by Fusarium oxysporum and Fusarium coryandri fungi. Due to this disease the plants wither and dry up.
Control - Carbendazim 50 WP @ 2.0 g per liter of water or Hexaconazole 5 EC 2 ml per liter of water or Metalaxyl 35 percent at the rate of 1 g per liter of water or Metalaxyl Mancozeb - 72 MZ at the rate of 2 g per liter of water. Spray.

Strain / Stem Swelling / Stem Pitica (Stemgal)- This disease is spread by the fungus Protamyces macrosporus. The disease causes extensive damage to the crop. Swelling occurs on the stems of plants. Knots are formed on stems, flowering twigs and other parts. Distortion also occurs in the seeds.
Control- Spraying of streptomycin 0.04% @ 0.4 gm/Ltr of water at an interval of 20 days when symptoms of the disease are observed.

Churnil Asita / Bhabhutia- This disease is spread by Erysiphi polygonal fungus. Due to this, in the initial stage of fruit disease, the white powder layer on the leaves and branches gets deposited. Leaves turn yellow and dry up due to excessive impact. Spraying at an interval of 10 to 15 days with Carbendazim 2.0 ml per liter of water or Azoxystrobin 23 SC 1.0 g per liter of water or Hexaconazole 5 EC 2.0 ml per liter of water or Metalaxyl Mancozeb-72 MZ 2.0 g per liter of water do

Weed control Critical period of weed competition in coriander crop is 35 to 40 days. If weeds are not weeded during this period, the yield of coriander is reduced by 40 to 45 percent. If there is an abundance of weeds in coriander crop, weedicide can be controlled by spraying Pendimethalin 30 EC 3 liters in 600 to 700 water per hectare for 2 days after sowing.

Irrigation In coriander, first irrigation after 30 to 35 days (leaf formation stage), second irrigation after 50 to 60 days (branching stage), third irrigation after 70 to 80 days (flowering stage) and fourth irrigation after 90 to 100 days. (seed formation stage). In light soil, it is beneficial to do fifth irrigation after 105 to 110 days (the stage of grain ripening).

Frost Protection Coriander crop gets more damage due to frost. When there is a possibility of frost, a light irrigation should be given to avoid frost to the crop. Spraying of sulfuric acid 0.1% (1.0 ml per liter of water) on the crop in the evening if there is a possibility of frost. Whenever there is a possibility of frost, after midnight, smoke should be burnt around the field by burning garbage.

Harvest When the coriander crop is ready to ripen in 90-100 days and flowering stops and the color of the seed bunches from green to bright brown, then the crop is considered ready for harvesting. After harvesting, the crop should be dried in the shade in the barn, after drying completely, the grains are cleaned separately, after which the grains are dried and filled in sacks.The yield of coriander depends on the variety, season and care of the crop. But on cultivation with the above scientific techniques, yield of 6 to 10 quintal seeds and 80 to 100 quintal leaves is obtained from irrigated crop and 4 to 6 quintal yield per hectare of unirrigated crop.

Storage: Coriander seed should have 9-10 percent moisture at the time of storage. Coriander seeds should be stored in thin jute bags. The bags should not be kept on the ground and adjacent to the wall. The sacks should be placed on wooden stakes on the ground. No more than 4-5 sacks of seeds should be placed on top of each other. Seed sacks should not be thrown from the height. Do not keep the seed sacks directly on the ground or keep them attached to the wall. Can be kept in sacks.
