Muskmelon

Muskmelon- Melon is a member of the Cucumber family! It is cultivated in almost all the provinces in India. Melon is mainly cultivated in hot and dry regions of Uttar Pradesh, Punjab, Rajasthan, Haryana, Maharashtra, Bihar and Madhya Pradesh. Melon is widely cultivated in the north-west of our country.Its cultivation is mainly done on the banks of rivers. Melon is a delicious fruit, which gives radiance in summer and its seeds are used to decorate sweets.

Cucumis melo, also known as melon,is a species of Cucumis that has been developed into many cultivated varieties. The fruit is a pepo. The flesh is either sweet or bland, with or without a musky aroma, and the rind can be smooth (such as honeydew), ribbed (such as cantaloupe), wrinkled (such as casaba melon), or netted (such as muskmelon). In North America, the sweet-flesh varieties are often collectively called muskmelon, including the musky netted-rind varieties and the inodorous smooth-rind varieties, and cantaloupe usually means the former type. However, muskmelon in a narrow sense only refers to the musky netted-rind type, while the true cantaloupe is the European type with ribbed and often warty rind that is seldom grown in North America.

The origin of melons is not known. Research has revealed that seeds and rootstocks were among the goods traded along the caravan routes of the Ancient World. Some botanists consider melons native to the Levant and Egypt, while others place their origin in Iran,India or Central Asia. Still others support an African origin, and in modern times wild melons can still be found in some African countries


Time of sowing November to February is considered the appropriate time.

Sowing Method Melon cultivation is mainly done by bed or drain method. The drain method has been considered the best for the cultivation of melons scientifically. For this, 1/2 meter wide drain should be made at a distance of 2.0 meters in the prepared field. Seeds should be sown in these drains at a distance of 1.5 feet.

Nursery Preparation Nursery for musk can be prepared either with polythene bags of size 200 gauge, 10 diameter and 15 cm height or with protrusions under protected nursery. Use protease containing 98 cells for each transplant. Transplant about 12 days old seedlings to the main area.

Seed quantity Good hybrid seed - 1 kg per hectare 2-3 seeds should be sown at one place. Bad plants should be uprooted after 15-20 days of sowing.

Seed Treatment Before sowing treat seed with Captan or Thiram or Carbendazim@2gm/kg of seed. After chemical treatment treat seed with Trichoderma viride@4gm/kg of seed. Dry the seeds in shade and sow immediately.

Earth Sandy loamy soil rich in organic matter with good drainage and a pH range of 6.5-7.5 is considered suitable.

Farm Preparation The field is well plowed and channels 2.5 m apart are made long. After plowing the field 2-3 times with plow or cultivator, the soil should be friable and level.

Favorable Climate Musk is a hot and dry season crop. The seed germinates best at a temperature of 28 C - 30 C and grows well at 25 C - 35 C. It also requires a lot of sunlight. Frequent rain or high humidity will exacerbate the disease problem and reduce flowering and fruit setting.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers Apply 20 t/ha of good decomposed cow dung, NPK 40:60:30 kg/ha as basal and 30 days after sowing N @ 40 kg/ha, Azospirillum and Phosphobacteria @ 2 kg/ha and Pseudomonas@ Apply 2.5 kg/ha along with FYM 50 kg and Neem cake 100 kg before last plowing.

Weed control After irrigation, weeds start growing. It is very important to remove them from the crop, otherwise their effect falls on the yield. Simultaneously, more plants should be removed from the stem. Only 2 or 3 plants should be kept. In this way, 2 or 3 weedings should be done in the entire crop. If there are diseases and pests plants, then they should be removed from the crop so that other plants do not get infected with pests and diseases.

Irrigation
Irrigate the field before seeding and then once a week.
Drip Irrigation - Install drip system with main and sub-main pipes and inline lateral tubes at 1.5m intervals. Place drippers in the lateral tubules, with 4 cm and 3.5 LPH capacities at 60 cm and 50 cm intervals, respectively.

Harvesting time The best time to harvest quality cantaloupe for the fresh market is before the fruit reaches full maturity, which is about 80% ripe. They taste best in 2-3 days after harvest.

Production Capability 20 ton/per ha. Production capacity remains.
