Garlic

Garlic- You know that garlic is a famous crop grown in South Europe. It is used as a spice in many dishes. Apart from this, it contains ingredients used in many medicines. Sources like protein, phosphorus and potassium are found in it. It helps in digestion And reduces the amount of cholesterol in human blood. Garlic is cultivated on a large scale in Madhya Pradesh, Gujarat, Orissa, Uttar Pradesh, Maharashtra, Punjab and Haryana.
You can earn in lakhs by doing proper cultivation of garlic. As far as how to do it, you can get started by adopting only good knowledge and scientific method. If the cultivation of garlic is done in the manner prescribed by the agricultural scientist, then the farmers can get good benefits in very less cost.
If the land and crop are well taken care of, the farmers get 80 to 120 quintals of yield per hectare. We will tell you the information about what land should be for garlic cultivation by scientists, when and how much fertilizer should be given in the field, when should irrigate etc.


Time of sowing: The appropriate time for sowing is considered to be the last week of September to the first week of October.

Distance: At the time of sowing, row to row distance should be about 15 cm and plant to plant distance should be about 8 cm.

Seed Depth: The land should be dug 4 to 5 cm deep for sowing the seeds.

Sowing method: For good crop yield, garlic should be sown by doubling method. Sowing can be done by hand or by machine. Cover the garlic knots with soil and give light irrigation.

Seed quantity: Use seed rate of 225-250 kg per acre.

Seed Treatment: Fungicide Carbendazim 12%+Mancozeb 63% WP for Garlic Seed Treatment Or treat with Thiram or Captan at the rate of 2 g/kg seed. and Thymethoxam 30% FS for insecticide. Treat the seed at the rate of 2 ml/kg. For organic seed treatment, treat Trichoderma or Pseudomonas at the rate of 2 g/kg seed.

Earth: Garlic can be cultivated in any type of land, but for this fertile loamy soil containing organic matter and drainage is suitable. In heavy, clay soil, the size of the tuber is small and digging is difficult. It can also tolerate frost and salinity to some degree.

Farm Preparation: Plow the field 2-3 times till the soil becomes friable. Level the field well and divide it into beds and gaps.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers: For more production of garlic, put about 25 tons of decomposed cow dung according to one acre in the field. Apart from this, prepare 50kg earth power, 40kg micro fertility compost, 10kg micro earth power, 10kg super gold calcifert, 50kg castor cake and 20kg micro neem, by mixing them well and then scattering it in equal quantity in the field.Prepare the field by plowing, after that sow the seed, after about 25 days after sowing, make a solution of 1kg super gold manisium and 500 micro zime in 400ml water and spray it in the field by pump, after that every 15 to 20 days interval. Do second and third sprays.

Weed Control: To control weeds in the garlic field, weeding is done. And as a chemical weed killer, apply Pendimethalin 38.7% CS at the rate of 700 ml / acre after sowing and then irrigate. And for the destruction of narrow and broad leaf weeds in standing crop, spray Propaquizafap 5% + Oxyfluorophane 12% w/w EC at the rate of 1-1.5 ml/Lit of water.

Irrigation: Apply a light irrigation after sowing of garlic. Irrigate depending on climate and soil type. And depending on the need apply irrigation at the interval of 10-15 days as per requirement.

Digging: Garlic crop is ready in 3-4 months. When the garlic crop is ready, its leaves turn yellow and die. After the crop is ready, the tubers should be dug up along with the seedlings and then bundled and dried in the sun for 3 to 4 days.

Production: If the land and crop are well taken care of, the farmers get 80 to 120 quintals of yield per hectare.
