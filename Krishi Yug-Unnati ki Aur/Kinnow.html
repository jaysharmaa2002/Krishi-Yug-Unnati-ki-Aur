Kinnow

Kinnow- Kinnow is a hybrid variety of citrus fruits. Kinnow horticulture can be done successfully all over India. This species includes tangerine, orange, lemon and lemon etc. Apart from vitamin C, vitamin A, B and mineral elements are also found in good quantity in kinnow. It is the main fruit of Punjab.Kinnow crop is grown all over northern India. It is the third largest fruit in India after banana and mango. Punjab, Rajasthan, Haryana, Himachal Pradesh, Uttar Pradesh, Jammu and Kashmir are the main growing states of kinnow.
The 'Kinnow' is a high yield mandarin hybrid cultivated extensively in the wider Punjab region of India and Pakistan. It is a hybrid of two citrus cultivars -'King' (Citrus nobilis) X 'Willow Leaf' (Citrus X deliciosa) -first developed by Howard B. Frost,at the University of California Citrus Experiment Station. After evaluation, the 'Kinnow' was released as a new citrus hybrid for commercial cultivation in 1935


Time of sowing Its planting in the spring season from February to March and the time of July to October is suitable for planting saplings.

Distance Keep the spacing between plants 6x6m. 60X60X60 cm for new plants. Dig pits of the size off

Seed depth For germination of the plant 60X60X60 cm. Prepare pits of size.

Method of preparing nursery for plants Orange saplings are prepared in the nursery for the cultivation of kinnow. Sow the seeds in nursery on a seed bed of size 2 m x 1 m. In which keep a distance of 15 cm from line to line. When the height of the plants is 10 to 12 cm. Plant the pits prepared in advance for horticulture.

Seed quantity The density of 208 plants per acre should be maintained.

Earth Light loamy soil with proper drainage is good for the plant. It yields quite well. Waterlogged land is not suitable for orange cultivation. The pH value of the land should be around 6 to 8.

Farm Preparation For kinnow cultivation, in the beginning, remove the remnants of the old crops present in the field and do deep plowing of the field. After that do two to three good slant plowing in the field through cultivator. After plowing, make the field level by putting a pad.

Climate For the cultivation of kinnow, a maximum temperature of 16 to 20 and maximum 32 to 40 degree is required. In normal climate, vegetative growth occurs in the orange plant. Low temperature and cold/frost cause major damage to kinnow cultivation.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers
->10 kg in the first year Dung manure, 150 grams nitrogen, 50 grams phosphorus, 75 grams potash.
->20 kg in the second year. Dung manure, 300 g Nitrogen, 100 g Phosphorus, 150 g Potash.
->30 kg in the third year. Dung manure, 450 grams of nitrogen, 150 grams of phosphorus, 225 grams of potash.
->40 kg in the fourth year. Dung manure, 600 g Nitrogen, 200 g Phosphorus, 300 g Potash.
->Fifth year and above 50 kg. Dung manure, 750 grams nitrogen, 250 grams phosphorus, 375 grams potash.
Apart from this, farmers can use zinc sulphate and other tonic fertilizers as per the requirement. Spraying of water soluble fertilizers has a good effect on the yield. Keep in mind that chemical fertilizers should be used on the basis of soil test.

Weed Control Weeds in Kinnow field can be controlled by hand weeding or by chemicals, after weeding out - use glyphosate 4 liters or per quart 2 liters mixed with 500 to 600 liters of water per hectare. As far as possible, use weedicide before flowering. Weedicide should not be used on main plants.

Irrigation It requires frequent watering during the early growth years. Irrigate at weekly intervals in 3-4 years old crop. Irrigate the old tree at 2-3 weeks interval depending on soil type, weather condition and rainfall. Avoid flood irrigation as it causes disease like root rot, collar rot etc. Always apply light irrigation with high frequency for optimum yield. The important stage of irrigation is before germination and after fruit set.

Harvesting time On attaining proper size, shape with attractive color with acid ratio of TSS of 12:1, kinnow fruits are ready for harvest. Depending on the variety, the fruit is generally ready for harvesting from mid-January to mid-February. Harvest at the right time as harvesting too early or too late will result in poor quality.

Production Capability The average yield of kinnow is 21 mt./ha. and dual yield potential. The life span of kinnu tree is 15-35 years. It has become very popular among farmers due to high juice content and better price.

Cleaning and Drilling After harvesting, wash the fruits with clean water and subsequently submerge the fruits in chlorinated water, partially dry them, coating Citra Shine wax to maintain good quality as well as improve appearance. With foam, then these fruits are dried in shade and then packing. Fruits are packed in boxes.
