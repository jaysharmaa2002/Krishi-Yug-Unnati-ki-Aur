Wheat

Wheat- Wheat is a grass native to the Levant region of the Middle East, which is cultivated throughout the world. Worldwide, wheat is the second largest crop grown for food, after maize, and the yield of wheat is increasing steadily. This growth is happening due to improved varieties of wheat and scientific methods.
India is the second largest producer of wheat, except the states of Kerala, Manipur and Nagaland, it is cultivated in all the other states, Uttar Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh and Punjab are the largest wheat producing states.
It is a major source of protein, vitamins and carbohydrates and provides a balanced diet. India is the fourth largest producer of wheat in the world after Russia, USA and China. India contributes 8.7 per cent to the production of wheat in the world. The best wheat are produced in areas favoured with cool, moist weather during the major portion of the growing period followed by dry, warm weather to enable the grain to ripen properly. The optimum temperature range for ideal germination of wheat seed is 20-25 C though the seeds can germinate in the temperature range 3.5 to 35 c.
Rains just after sowing hamper germination and encourage seedling blight. Areas with a warm and damp climate are not suited for wheat growing.During the heading and flowering stages, excessively high or low temperatures and drought are harmful to wheat. Cloudy weather, with high humidity and low temperatures is conducive for rust attack. Wheat plant requires about 14-15 c optimum average temperature at the time of ripening . The temperature conditions at the time of grain filling and development are very crucial for yield. Temperatures above 250c during this period tend to depress grain weight.When temperatures are high, too much energy I lost through the process of transpiration by the plants and the reduced residual energy results in poorer grain formation and lower yields.


Sowing time, method and Seed quantity-
Unirrigated:The sowing time for unirrigated wheat is from October 15 to October 31, in this period sowing is possible only when there is sufficient rainfall in the month of September. This maintains the necessary moisture in the soil. If the weight of 1000 grains (1000 grains) of sown seed is 38 grams, then use 100 kg seed per hectare. If the weight of thousand grains is more than 38 grams, increase the amount of seed per gram by 2 kg per hectare.

Irrigated:Timely sowing in which first fortnight of November is best, sowing of irrigated and time-consuming species should be made necessary by 15-25 November. At the time of sowing the seed 2-3 cm. It should be sown in depth so that sufficient moisture is available for germination. Row to row distance is 20 cms. Should be kept For this, the average seed quantity is 100 kg/ha. Should be kept or according to the size of the seed, determine its quantity and the distance from row to row should be 18 cm. Keep it

For irrigated and late sowing:Late sowing in which fortnight of December is best. Late sowing must be completed from 15th to 20th December. On average, 125 kg seed per hectare in late sowing. It will be appropriate to sow according to this (for late sowing, increase the quantity of seed of each variety by 25 percent) and the row distance is 18 cm. Keep it

Earth
Mattyar loam soil is the best for good production of wheat, but if sufficient fertilizer is given to the plants in balanced quantity and irrigation etc. Alkaline and saline lands are not good for wheat cultivation. Wheat should not be cultivated even in the land which is filled with water.

Seed treatment
Before sowing the seed should be treated with Tebuconazole 2% DS. Or Thiram should be treated at the rate of 2 gm/kg seed.

Farm preparation
After harvesting the previous crop, the field should be well plowed with the help of tractor. The field is usually plowed with tractors and then plowed twice or thrice. Deep plowing should be done to make the soil fine and friable. Plowing of the field should be done in the evening and the transplanted land should be left open overnight to absorb the moisture from the dew drops.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers
In wheat cultivation, use of vermicompost or green manure while preparing the field before sowing increases the production. In chemical fertilizer, urea 110 kg, D.A.P. 55 kg, potash 20 kg. Gram. per acre can be used. Keep in mind that chemical fertilizers should be used only on the basis of soil test.

Weed control
Chemical weedicide Clodinafop Propargyl 15% + Metsulfuron Methyl 1% WP should be sprayed at 160 g/acre to control narrow and broad leaf weeds in wheat crop. Or 2,4-D 250 ml for broadleaf weed control. Use it by dissolving it in 150 liters of water.

Irrigation
30-35 hectare cm of dwarf varieties of wheat. and indigenous varieties to 15-20 hectare cm. Total water requirement. According to the available water, irrigation should be done in wheat by making beds. On average 5 cm in first irrigation. and 7.5 cm in subsequent irrigations. Water should be given. The number of irrigations and the amount of water depends on the type of soil, atmospheric temperature and the variety sown. Irrigation is necessary in dwarf varieties at certain critical stages of the cropping period.

Irrigation in Wheat cultivation
First irrigation should be given 3 to 4 weeks after sowing.
Second irrigation should be done 40 to 45 days after sowing.
3 irrigations 60 to 65 days after sowing.
Give 4 irrigations after 80 to 85 days of sowing.
Do 5th irrigation after 100 to 105 days of sowing.
Do 6th irrigation 105 to 120 days after sowing.

Harvesting
When the wheat plants turn yellow and the ears dry up, the crop should be harvested. When there is 15 to 20 percent moisture in the grains, then it is the right time to harvest. After harvesting the crop should be dried for 3 to 4 days. After this wheat can be threshed by modern machines like tractor driven thresher or oxen.

Produce and Storage
On cultivation with advanced technology, in addition to 50-60 quintal grain, 80-90 quintal straw/ha is obtained from dwarf varieties of wheat under irrigated condition. Whereas native tall varieties yield about half of its yield. In non-irrigated condition from indigenous variety 15-20 quintals/ha yield is obtained. The grains should not contain more than 10-12% moisture for safe storage.
Clean all storage rooms and rooms and sprinkle Malathion 50% solution on the walls and floors at the rate of 3 liters per 100 sqm. After keeping the grain in Bukhari, cell or room, it should be closed by keeping two tablets of aluminum phosphide 3 grams per ton.
