Turmeric

Turmeric- Turmeric is an important spice crop, which is used as a spice, medicine, coloring matter and cosmetics and in religious rituals. India ranks first in the world in the cultivation and export of turmeric. This crop is full of qualities, turmeric can be cultivated easily and by adopting low cost technology, it can be made a good source of income. In India this crop is grown in Andhra Pradesh, Orissa, West Bengal, Karnataka and Kerala.

Turmeric, (Curcuma longa), perennial herbaceous plant of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae), the tuberous rhizomes, or underground stems, of which have been used from antiquity as a condiment, a textile dye, and medically as an aromatic stimulant. Native to southern India and Indonesia, turmeric is widely cultivated on the mainland and in the islands of the Indian Ocean.

In ancient times it was used as a perfume as well as a spice. The rhizome has a pepperlike aroma and a somewhat bitter warm taste and has a strong staining orange-yellow colour. It is the ingredient that colours and flavours prepared mustard and is used in curry powder, relishes, pickles, and spiced butters for vegetables, in fish and egg dishes, and with poultry, rice, and pork.

In parts of Asia turmeric water is applied as a cosmetic to lend a golden glow to the complexion. Reputed to have anti-inflammatory properties, turmeric is sometimes consumed as a tea or in pill form for a variety of ailments, including arthritis and intestinal problems.


Famous varieties The spice variety, Poona, Sonia, Gautam, Rashim, Suroma, Roma, Krishna, Guntur, Megha, Halda1, Sukarna, Sugandan and Co.1 etc. are the major castes that farmers can choose from. Once a small amount is mixed, then your own seed can be prepared.

Time of sowing It can be sown between 15th May to 15th June according to the climate, variety and irrigation facility.

Sowing method For sowing, tubers with well developed knots of turmeric are used, sowing is done by making bunds.

Distance The optimal distance between the mand and the furrow is 45-60 cm. and 25 cm between rows and plants. distance should be kept.

Seed quantity 20-25 quintals/ha bales are required for sowing turmeric.

Seed treatment Tuber rot disease is not found by treating its tubers with 0.3% solution of Dithane M-45 before planting.

Climate Turmeric is a plant of warmer climate, but turmeric can be cultivated even in places up to an altitude of about 1500m above sea level. When the temperature of the atmosphere is 20 C.G. If it is less than this, then there is a great effect on the growth of turmeric plant.

Earth Turmeric is successfully cultivated in sandy loam or clay loam soil. There should be proper drainage system. If the soil is slightly acidic then turmeric cultivation can be done successfully in it.

Farm preparation For the cultivation of turmeric, the land needs to be plowed well 2-3 times with a plow or cultivator, as it is inside the ground, due to which it is necessary to make the land well pulverized.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers In turmeric cultivation, at the time of plowing the land or after transplanting, dung manure and compost 40 ton / ha as base manure. The seeds should be used by covering them by spreading them in the beds at the rate of Rs. 60 kg nitrogen, 50 kg. gram phosphorus and 120 kg potash per hectare. Fertilizer should be used in between, and half quantity of nitrogen should be applied after 40 days of sowing and remaining half quantity in the field after 90 days.

Major Diseases and Controls Leaf blotch:- Leaf blotch is caused by Tryphina maculans and appears as small, elliptical, oblong or irregular almond colored spots on both sides of the leaves, which immediately become pale yellow or dark brown in color. changes, the leaves also turn yellow. In the acute stage of the disease, the plants appear shriveled and the yield of rhizomes is also reduced. The disease can be controlled by spraying of Mancozeb 0.2%.
Rhizome rot:-This disease is caused by Pyium graminicolum. The black parts of the temporary stems become soft and wet, resulting in plant fall and rotting of the rhizomes. The disease can be prevented by treating the seed rhizomes in Mancozeb-0.3% solution for 30 minutes before storage and transplanting. When this disease is observed in the field, the beds should be treated with Mancozeb 0.3%.

Key Kits & Controls
Stem borer:-Stem borer (Conogales punctiferalis) is the most damaging pest of turmeric. Their gnats live in the temporary stems and feed on the inner tissues. Frass in the holes and the central part of the withered shoots are obvious signs of pest infestation. Its adult moths are medium sized, with a wingspan of 20 mm. Broad and on these wings there are small black dots of orange-yellow color. Fully developed burrows are light brown in color with hairs.Spraying of Malathion - 0.1% or Monocrotophos - 0.075% or Dipel - 0.3% (from Bacillus turrensis) at an interval of 21 days during July-October can control these pests. Spraying can be started when the first signs of insect infestation are seen in the inner leaves of the floating stems.
Rhizome scale:- Rhizome scale (Aspidella harti) infects rhizomes in fields (at the time of harvest) and in storage. The adult (female) scale is circular (approximately 1 mm in diameter) and pale brown to brown in color and appears as a crust on the rhizomes. They suck the juice from the rhizomes and when the rhizomes are more infected, they shrink and dry up.Due to which germination is also affected. Seed material should be treated with Quinalphos - 0.075% for 20 - 30 minutes before storage and also before sowing (if infestation persists). Do not store heavily infected plants and should be discarded.
Small Insects: Larvae and adults of small insects such as Lima sp. They eat leaves, especially during monsoon, they make long parallel marks on the leaves. Spraying of Malthion - 0.10.3% is sufficient to control shoot borers. Leaves infected with lacewing insects (Stephanatis typicus) turn yellow and dry up. The infestation of these insects is more during the post monsoon period, especially in dry areas of the country.
Turmeric thrips (Panketo thrips) infested by insects cause twisting of leaves of turmeric plants and turn yellow and gradually dry up. The infestation of these insects is more during the post monsoon period, especially in dry areas of the country, these insects can be controlled by spraying of dimethoate (0.05%).

Weed control For a good crop of turmeric, it becomes necessary to do 2-3 weedings. First weeding should be done 80-90 days after sowing and second weeding after one month but if weeds are already there and it seems that crop is getting affected then a weeding can be done even before this. Along with this, weeding should also be done from time to time so that air circulation can be good.

Irrigation Turmeric does not require much irrigation, but if the crop is sown in summer, 4-5 irrigations are required before the onset of rains. In this, one or two irrigations are required before the rains and two irrigations after the rains.

Drying The knots of turmeric should be separated by 5-7 cm. It should be dried in the sun by laying a mat or on a dry ground. Drying on thin sheets is not suitable as it adversely affects the color of turmeric dried in this way. At night, the rhizomes should be covered with something that does not obstruct the circulation of air. It may take up to 10-15 days for it to dry completely.

To Polish To polish it by hand, it is rubbed on a hard ground and to modify it by a new method, on the central axis of the hand operated machine with veils / casks with a mesh of iron on both sides. Turmeric becomes shiny and smooth by rubbing against the surface of the mesh and rubbing the turmeric knots inside the cask when the cassettes filled with turmeric are rotated. Turmeric is also brightened by electric piping. After polishing, there is a change of 15-25% in turmeric.

Dyeing The color of modified turmeric affects its value. To make the produce attractive, turmeric powder should be sprinkled with a little water in the last round of polishing.

Storage of Seed Rhizomes The rhizomes kept for seed material in turmeric cultivation should be covered with turmeric leaves and kept in well ventilated rooms. Seed rhizomes can also be kept in pits filled with wood sawdust, sand, Glycosmis pentaphylla (Panal) leaves etc. These pits should be covered with wooden planks with one or two holes for ventilation. If infestation of scale scales is seen, then the rhizomes are exposed to 0.075% for 15 minutes dip in quinalphos solution and in 0.3% mancozeb to avoid mold damage in storage.
