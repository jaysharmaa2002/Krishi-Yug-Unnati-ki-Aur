Taramira

Taramira- You know Taramira is a crop of the mustard family, Toria, Brown mustard, Yellow mustard and Raya come in the group of Taramira crops. The length of the taramira crop is 2 to 3 feet. It is similar to the mustard species but its grains or pods are red. Cultivated in all regions, this taramira can be grown in fertile and unusable land.

The amount of oil in it is found to be about 35 to 37 percent. Its oil is edible. Maximum taramira is produced in Hanumangarh and Sri Ganganagar districts of Rajasthan. Apart from this, sufficient production is also done in Nagaur, Jodhpur, Tonk, Jaipur, Bharatpur, Alwar.

Taramira is an important oilseed crop of drier regions of north-western India. The oil is not directly eaten, although it is mixed with mustard oil to increase the pungency of the later. It originated in Mediterranean region. It has 2n-22 chromosomes which are very small. Genetic improvement is limited in this crop, although some varieties are available. Very recently a variety RTM-314 has been released for the general cultivation. Taramira has good traits particularly conferring disease resistance which can be transferred toBrassica campestris and B. juncea both are important crops. Some efforts are underway in this regard with limited success.

Taramira seeds are oil seeds and mostly used in cattle feed. It is also used in hair oil and skin care products. Eurca sativa oil has some properties which protect our eyes from uv rays as well as high power blue light.


Time of sowing Depending on the availability of moisture, it should be sown from 15th September to 15th October.

Distance Keep row to row distance 40 cms.

Depth Sow seeds 5 cm deep in rows.

Sowing method Taramira is sown by direct seed propagation method or by seed drill.

Seed quantity 5 kg seed per hectare is sufficient for the cultivation of Taramira.

Seed treatment Before sowing, treat the seed with Mancozeb at the rate of 1.5 gm per kg of seed.

Favorable Climate Taramira is a rabi season crop, which requires cool dry weather and bright sunny days. The places with high rainfall are not suitable for its cultivation.

Land selection Taramira can be grown in fertile and unusable land, but light loamy soil is more suitable for Taramira. Acidic and highly alkaline soils are not at all useful for this.

Farm preparation Taramira can be cultivated mostly in rainfed areas, where other crops cannot be grown successfully. After harvesting Kharif fodder, urad, moong, chawla, maize, jowar etc., if there is moisture, then it can be sown successfully by doing a light plowing. As far as possible, the field should not be left vacant for sowing of Taramira in the rainy season.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers For good growth of Taramira, at the time of field preparation, 30 kg nitrogen and 15 kg phosphorus per hectare should be given with cow dung and at the time of sowing.

Weed control At the time of weed control, weeding should be done as per requirement.

Irrigation To get good yield, where suitable irrigation system is available, first irrigation should be done in taramira crop in 40 to 50 days before flowering, then second irrigation should be done at the time of grain formation if required.

Harvest When the leaves of Taramira crop fall and the pods start turning yellow, then the crop should be harvested, otherwise there is a possibility of the grain falling in the field due to delay in harvesting.

Storage Select a moisture-free place for storing taramira seeds.
Production Yield up to 12 to 15 quintals per hectare is obtained under the above techniques and favorable conditions.
