Tamarind

Tamarind- As you know tamarind is a fruit tree. It is one of the special fruit trees found throughout India, mostly grown in rainfed conditions, especially in Tamil Nadu, Maharashtra, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh and Orissa. It is one of the most popular tree which, apart from providing shade, also gives useful fruits and wood. Tamarind is believed to be native to tropical Africa, but is now cultivated throughout Southeast Asia, Australia, America.Tamarind is commonly used as a flavoring spice in regional cuisines in Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka and Andhra Pradesh. Tamarind is especially used while making Rasam, Sambar, Vata Kuzumbu, Puliogare etc. and any Indian chaat is incomplete without tamarind chutney. Even tamarind flowers are used to make delicious dishes.

Tamarind (Tamarindus indica) is a leguminous tree bearing edible fruits that is indigenous to tropical Africa. The genus Tamarindus is monotypic, meaning that it contains only this species. It belongs to family of Fabaceae. The tamarind tree produces brown, pod-like fruits that contain a sweet, tangy pulp, which is used in cuisines around the world. The pulp is also used in traditional medicine and as a metal polish. The tree's wood can be used for woodworking and tamarind seed oil can be extracted from the seeds. Tamarind's tender young leaves are used in Indian and Filipino cuisine. Because tamarind has multiple uses, it is cultivated around the world in tropical and subtropical zones.

Tamarindus indica is probably indigenous to tropical Africa, but has been cultivated for so long on the Indian subcontinent that it is sometimes reported to be indigenous there.It grows wild in Africa in locales as diverse as Sudan, Cameroon, Nigeria, Kenya, Zambia, Somalia, Tanzania and Malawi. In Arabia, it is found growing wild in Oman, especially Dhofar, where it grows on the sea-facing slopes of mountains.


Time of sowing The appropriate time for transplanting tamarind plants is June-July.

Distance Planting distance can range from 8 X 8 to 10 X 10 m depending on the type of soil.

Sowing method Sowing of tamarind is done by direct seed or through seed preparation in nursery.

Planting Method Planting should be done in pits of size 1 x 1 x 1 m to provide good growth, deep root development and proper medium for the plant. The pits should be dug during the summer. Also these pits should be filled with a mixture of 2 kg single superphosphate, well decomposed farm yard manure and top soil. To avoid termite problems, add insecticide powder to the soil mix.

Favorable climate Tropical climate is best for tamarind tree. It is able to tolerate the heat and hot winds of warm climates, while its growth is adversely affected by frost in cold climates.

Land selection Tamarind tree can be grown successfully in almost all types of land, for this, loamy, loamy, sandy and saline soils are more suitable. Appointed deep loamy soil and deep alluvial soil are also suitable for good yield of tamarind trees.

Farm preparation Before planting, make the land weed free, then dig pits on the selected land on the basis of proper size and land. In the form of plantation, tamarind plants can also be planted on the meadows of the field.

Fertilizers and chemical Fertilizers For good growth of the plant, well-rotted manure or vermicompost should be mixed with the soil in the pits at the time of planting. Chemical fertilizers should be given on the basis of soil test.

Weed control To control weeds, weeding should be done from time to time as needed.

Irrigation Light irrigation should be done immediately after planting. Irrigation is not required during the rainy season, but in summer, plants should be watered at special intervals and moisture should be maintained in the soil, keeping in mind that there should be no problem of water logging in the field.

Harvest Plants propagated by seed will start bearing fruit after 7-8 years of planting and by grafted or cutting method, 4-5 years after planting. The fruits are harvested in the months of January-April.

Production Tamarind fruit production varies with soil type, climate, propagation method and management practices. A well managed tree yields 300-500 kg of ripe pods.
