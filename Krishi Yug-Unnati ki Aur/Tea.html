TEA

TEA- Tea is one of the most popular and important beverages around the world. It is an evergreen shrub-like plant which is a species of tree called Thienensis. Tea contains a substance called thein which gives energy to the human body. It's made from leaves of tea plants. China is the largest producer of tea in the world. India is the second largest producer of tea in the world.
Tea is an aromatic beverage prepared by pouring hot or boiling water over cured or fresh leaves of Camellia sinensis, an evergreen shrub native to China and East Asia. After water, it is the most widely consumed drink in the world. There are many different types of tea; some, like Chinese greens and Darjeeling, have a cooling, slightly bitter, and astringent flavour, while others have vastly different profiles that include sweet, nutty, floral, or grassy notes. Tea has a stimulating effect in humans primarily due to its caffeine content Its largest exporting country is Sri Lanka. In India, Darjeeling, Assam, Kolukkumalai, Palampur, Munnar, Nilgiris are popular for tea cultivation.


Time of sowing The best time for planting saplings is in the months of October and November.

Sowing method Tea seedlings are prepared by seed and cutting method. It takes a lot of effort and time to prepare seedlings from seed. Therefore its seedlings are prepared through the pen. For cultivation through seeds, seedlings are first prepared in the nursery from its seeds.

Planting Method Tea plants are planted in pits prepared in the field. For this, a small pit is prepared in the middle of the pits already prepared in the field. After that, after removing the polythene of the plant, put it in the prepared pit and press it well by putting soil around it. Its plants need shade to grow. For this, one shade tree should be planted on four to five plants in each row.

Distance In the field, prepare pits keeping a distance of 4-5 meters in rows.

Favorable climate Hot humid climate is best for tea cultivation. And it gives good yield in 10 to 35 degree temperature.

Land selection A slightly acidic soil rich in organic matter is best for good tea production. There should be good drainage system in tea gardens. Light acidic soil with a pH of 4.5 - 5.0 is considered good for its cultivation.

Farm preparation Before planting, the field is well plowed and left open for a few days. After that, make the soil friable by running rotavator in the field. After making the soil friable, level the land by putting a pat in the field. Its plants are prepared in the fields and planted in them.

Fertilizers and chemical Fertilizers For good growth and high yield of tea, more fertilizers and fertilizers are required. While preparing the pit for this, each plant should be given about 20 kg of well decomposed cow dung or vermicompost. NP in chemical fertilizer Of. And necessary nutrients should be given on the basis of soil test.

Weed control To control weeds, weeding should be done as per the requirement.

Irrigation Irrigation in tea cultivation is done through rain. Irrigation should be done by sprinkler method every day when there is little or no rain.

Harvest The leaves are ready for harvesting about a year after planting. Farmers can get the crop by plucking it 3 times in a year.

Production About 1800 to 2500 kg per hectare of tea can be obtained from plantation per hectare.
