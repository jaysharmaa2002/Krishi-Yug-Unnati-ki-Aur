Ginger

GINGER-(Zingiber officinale) (Group-Zingiberaceae) is a shrubby perennial plant whose rhizomes are used as a spice. India produces 60% of the world's total production. Kerala is the most cultivated crop of this crop in India, where 70% of India's total production is produced from here.
Apart from this, it is also cultivated in Himachal Pradesh, Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu, Maharashtra, Karnataka, Orissa, Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, etc.India, ginger is known by various names such as Aadu (Gujarati), Ale (Marathi), Ada (Bengali), Ilam (Tamil), Allayu (Telugu), Alla (Kannada) and Ginger (Hindi, Hindi). Punjabi) etc.Ginger (Zingiber officinale) is a flowering plant whose rhizome, ginger root or ginger, is widely used as a spice and a folk medicine. It is a herbaceous perennial which grows annual pseudostems (false stems made of the rolled bases of leaves) about one meter tall bearing narrow leaf blades.


Time of sowing :- Ginger is sown in the first week of May-June.

Distance:- The distance between the rows should be kept 20-25 cm. The pieces of seed rhizome should be dug in a light pit and then paralleled by adding compost (FYM) and soil.

Seed depth:- Seed depth 3-4 cm. should be close to.

Sowing Method:- Ginger can be sown directly and by transplanting.

Seed Quantity:- Use fresh and disease free bales for sowing. Seed rate of 15 to 20 quintal rhizome per hectare is suitable for ginger cultivation.

Seed treatment:- After treating the seed rhizome with 0.3% (3g/Litre water) Mancozeb for 30 minutes, drying it in a shady place for 3-4 hours and sowing it at a distance of 20-25 cm.
After sowing, cover the crop with green leaves at 50 qtl/acre. Cover the crop again with green leaves at 20 qtl/acre after each fertilization.

Earth :- Cultivation of Ginger: The sandy loam in which the amount of fossil or organic matter is high, that land is most suitable. The pH value of the soil is 5.6 to 6.5 and the soil with good drainage is the best, yields more than the ginger crop. Do not grow ginger crop in the field where ginger crop was grown last time. Do not plant ginger crop on the same land every year.

Climate:- Ginger can be cultivated well in hot and humid climate. Apart from this, there should be 50 to 60 cm of annual rainfall at the time of crop development, as well as the land should also be such where water cannot stagnate for a long time and adequate shade should also be maintained.

Field preparation: For the cultivation of ginger, before the monsoon, the field should be well plowed four or five times and the soil should be tilled and leveled. For sowing ginger 15 cm. Make high and 1 meter wide beds. 50 cm between two beds. Keep the distance. There should be good drainage system.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers:- Ginger is a long duration crop. Which requires more nutrients. Fertilizers should be used after soil testing. Before sowing of ginger, while preparing the field, well decomposed cow dung at the rate of 10-15 tonnes of manure/acre should be mixed well in the soil. And in chemical fertilizers Nitrogen 25 kg (55 kg urea), Phosphorus 10 kg (60 kg single super phosphate) and 10 kg of potash. Apply (16 kg murrate of potash) per acre. Apply full dose of Potash and Phosphorus at the time of sowing. Divide the amount of nitrogen into two equal parts. Apply first part after 75 days of sowing and rest part after 3 months of sowing.

Harmful pests and diseases and their prevention:- Ginger mite and turmeric tuber scale - Chlorpyriphos (5%) dust 25 kg/ha for its prevention. Apply it in the field at the time of sowing.
Niger scale - For its prevention, treat the tubers with Quinalphos dust for 20 minutes before sowing and storage.
Thrips - Dimethoate 2 ml / l for its prevention. Spray with water.
Stem borer and leaf rot - Dimethoate 2 ml / l for its prevention. or quinalphos 2 ml / l. Spray with water.

Foliage - For its prevention, Mancojave 2 ml. Dissolve in one liter of water and sprinkle.
Ukatha or Jaundice and Tuber rot - Mancojave + Metalaxyl 3 ml for its prevention. Treat the tubers by dissolving them in per liter of water and do sowing or drenching in the standing crop.
Bacterial uptake - For its prevention, treat the tubers with a solution of Streppocycline 200 p.p.n.
Anthracnose - Hexaconazole 10 ml for its prevention. Or Mancozeb 75 WP 25 gm per 10 ltr of water +10 ml. Spray stickers.

Weed control :- Germination of ginger rhizome takes place in 15 to 25 days, so weeding and hoeing should be done in the field as per requirement. Apply soil to the roots for the development of roots. After 50-60 days of sowing, apply soil to the roots for the first time and after 40 days apply soil again.

Irrigation :- Irrigate ginger crop on the basis of intensity and frequency of rainfall. Irrigation and water management It is very important to maintain equal moisture in ginger cultivation. That is why the first irrigation in its cultivation should be done immediately after sowing. In better irrigation techniques for ginger cultivation, if drip method or drip irrigation is used, it can yield very good results.

Excavation :- The crop is ready for harvesting after eight months of sowing when the leaves turn yellow and gradually dry up. The plants are carefully uprooted with the help of shovel or spade and separate the rhizomes from the root and soil. To use ginger as a vegetable, it should be dug out in the sixth month itself. The rhizomes should be washed thoroughly and dried in sunlight for at least a day and used.

Storage :- To make good seed, the rhizome is stored in pits made in shady places. When the crop is in green stage of 6-8 months, healthy plants are selected in the field. The seed rhizome is dried in a shady place after 30 minutes treatment in a solution of 0.075% quinalphos and 0.3% mancozeb. Seed rhizomes are conveniently stored by making pits. The walls of these pits are coated with cow dung.
A layer of rhizomes is then placed in a layer of 2 cm sand/sawdust in the pits. Enough space is left inside the pits to get enough air. These pits are covered with wooden frames. One or two holes are made in these boards to make them ventilated. The seed rhizome should be seen at an interval of about 21 days.
