Gooseberry

Gooseberry- Amla is an important fruit of Indian origin. Amla fruit rich in medicinal properties and nutrients is an unprecedented gift of nature. Its botanical name is Emblica officinalis. Vitamin 'C' (500 to 700 mg per 100 grams) and calcium, phosphorus, potassium and sugar are found in abundance in amla fruits. Amla is generally known for its abundance of Vitamin 'C'.

Its fruits are used to prepare various medicines. Anemia, diarrhea, toothache, fever and wounds are treated with medicines made from amla. Various types of shampoos, hair oils, dyes, dental powders, and oral creams are prepared from amla. In India, Uttar Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, Punjab, Haryana, Karnataka, Uttarakhand and Himachal Pradesh are the main producing states of Amla.

Gooseberries are small, nutritious fruits that offer many health benefits.The fruits of the gooseberry bush are small, weighing around 0.1-0.2 ounces (3-6 grams) each. They vary in color and can be green, yellow-white, pink, red, or dark purple. Their flavor ranges from tart to sweet

The goose in gooseberry has been mistakenly seen as a corruption of either the Dutch word kruisbes or the allied German Krausbeere,[4] or of the earlier forms of the French groseille. Alternatively, the word has been connected to the Middle High German krus ('curl, crisped'), in Latin as grossularia.Black bears, various birds and small mammals eat the berries, while game animals, coyotes, foxes and raccoons browse the foliage.


Time of sowing The most suitable time for planting Amla plants in the fields is after the month of June till the month of September.

Distance In the month of May-June, plant the grafted plants at a distance of 4.5 X 4.5 m.

Seed depth Dig a square pit 1 meter deep and leave it exposed to sunlight for 15-20 days.

Sowing method Amla plants are planted through both seed and cutting medium, but it is best to plant through cuttings.

Seed quantity For good yield, apply seed at 200 gm per acre.

Seed treatment To protect the crop from soil borne diseases and pests and for good germination, treat the seeds with Gibberellic acid 200-500 ppm. Air dry the seeds after chemical treatment.

Favorable Climate The temperate climate is suitable for the cultivation of Amla, Amla plants need normal temperature in the beginning, while its full grown plant can tolerate any temperature from 0 degree to 45 degree, but the minimum temperature for a longer time. Plants are harmed if persisted.

Land selection Gooseberry can be grown in any soil, but black alluvial soil is considered suitable for it. Soil pH should be 6.5-9.5 for Amla cultivation.

Farm Preparation Well cultivated and organic soil is required for Amla cultivation. Plow the land before sowing to make the soil well friable. Add organic manure such as cow dung to the soil. Then dig pits in the land at a distance of 7.5 to 9.5 meters and the size of 1.25 to 1.50 meters should be dug.

Fertilizers and chemical Fertilizers For good production of Amla, mix well-rotted cow dung in the soil at the rate of 10 kg per plant. Apply Nitrogen 100 gm, Phosphorus 50 gm and Potash 100 gm per plant in the field. Apply this manure to one year old plant and keep increasing the amount of manure for 10 years. Full amount of phosphorus and half of potash and nitrogen should be introduced in January-February.Enter as a dose. Put the rest in the month of August. Apply 100-150 grams of boron and zinc sulphate, depending on the age and health of the plant, in soil with high sodium content.

Weed control To control weeds, weeding should be done as per the requirement.

Irrigation Irrigation is required immediately after planting. In summer, apply irrigation at 15 days interval and in winter in the month of October-December, apply 25-30 liters per tree every day by flat irrigation. Irrigation is not required during the rainy season. Do not irrigate at the time of flowering.

Fruit picking and sorting Amla plants start yielding about three to four years after planting in the field. Its fruits are ready to ripen about 5 to 6 months after flowering. Its fruits appear green in the beginning. But after ripening, their color starts to appear light yellow. Its fruits should be harvested during this time. After harvesting its fruits, they should be washed with cold water and dried in shady places. After drying the fruits, they should be sorted and sent to sell in the market.

Production One fully grown Amla tree gives 100 to 120 kg of fruits. Whereas about 150 to 180 saplings can be planted in one acre.
