Mustard

Mustard- Mustard farming is mainly done in all the regions of India. Mustard farming is a major crop of Haryana, Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, Uttar Pradesh and Maharashtra. It is a major oilseed crop. Mustard Farming The special thing is that it can be grown in both irrigated and rainfed conditions. It is the third most important crop in the world after soybean and palm oil. Mustard seeds and its oil are mainly used in the kitchen. Mustard leaves are used to make vegetables. Mustard cake is also made which is used to feed milch animals.
It is being produced in India since time immemorial. It is cultivated in about 66.34 lakh hectares of land in India, giving about 75 to 80 lakh production. If mustard is cultivated with scientific technology, then growers can get maximum yield from its crop. Complete information about how to do advanced cultivation of mustard is mentioned in this article.


Time of sowing The right time for sowing mustard is September to October.

Distance Row to row spacing of 30 cm for mustard sowing. And the plant to plant distance is 10 to 15 cm. Keep it For mustard sowing, the spacing of rows is 45 cm. And the plant to plant distance is 10 cm. Keep it

Seed depth Seed 4-5 cm Must be sown deep.

Sowing method Use only sowing machine for sowing.

Seed quantity The quantity of seed required is 1.5 kg per acre. After 3 weeks of sowing, destroy weak plants and leave healthy plants in the field.

Seed treatment To protect the seed from soil pests and diseases, treat the seeds with Thiram @ 3 gm/kg of seed.

Land Selection Mustard can be grown in sandy to heavy clay soils. But sandy loam soil is most suitable. This crop can tolerate mild alkalinity. But the soil should not be acidic.

Suitable climate Mustard farming is done in winter season in India. This crop requires 18 to 25 Celsius temperature. Rain, high humidity and cloudy weather in the atmosphere are not good for mustard crop during flowering. If this type of weather happens, then there is more chance of Mahu or Chapa coming on the crop.

Farm Preparation For mustard cultivation, plow the land with desi plow or cultivator two or three times and after each plowing, ploughing. The beds should be soft, wet and flat for the seeds to germinate evenly. The crop sown on seed bed germinates well.

Manures and Fertilizers At the time of preparation of field for mustard cultivation, well decomposed cow dung at the rate of 7-12 t/acre should be mixed well in the soil. Get the soil tested for proper use of fertilizers. Apply 40 kg Nitrogen (90 kg Urea), 12 kg Phosphorus (75 kg Single Super Phosphate) and 6 kg Potassium (10 kg Murate of Potash) per acre to mustard.In areas with low rainfall, apply all the fertilizers before sowing. For mustard, apply half the amount of manure before sowing and half at the time of first watering. Keep in mind that chemical fertilizers should be used only on the basis of soil test.

Weed control For weed control in mustard cultivation, do 2-3 weedings in 15 days interval.

Irrigation Sowing the crop after irrigation. After sowing, three irrigations are required at an interval of three weeks to get a good crop. Use more organic fertilizers to conserve soil moisture.

Harvesting and Threshing The more ripe the crop, the greater the risk of pods cracking. Therefore, the crop should be harvested when the plants turn yellow and the beans turn brown. The grain is separated by drying the crop by beating it with thresher or sticks. After drying the seeds, put them in sacks or in drums. And store it in a moisture free place.

Production On cultivation by the above advanced techniques of mustard, a grain yield of 15 to 20 quintals is obtained in unirrigated areas and 20 to 30 quintals per hectare in irrigated areas.
