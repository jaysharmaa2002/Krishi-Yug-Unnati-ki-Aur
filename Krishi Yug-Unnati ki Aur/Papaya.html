Papaya

Papaya- Papaya has an important place among fruits. Papaya fruit is spherical and elongated. Its flesh is yellow and there are black seeds between the anus. It is an evergreen sweet fruit, papaya is delicious and tasty and is also useful for health. Papaya is called Papaya in English.Apart from the vitamins A and C found in it, potassium, calcium and iron are found in abundance. It is cultivated in different states of the country namely Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu, Bihar, Assam, Maharashtra, Gujarat, Uttar Pradesh, Punjab, Haryana, Delhi, Jammu and Kashmir, Uttaranchal and Mizoram. Now it is being cultivated all over India.

The papaya, papaw, or pawpaw is the plant Carica papaya, one of the 22 accepted species in the genus Carica of the family Caricaceae. Its origin is in the tropics of the Americas, perhaps from Central America and southern Mexico.


Time of sowing In India, it is grown for twelve months, but the appropriate time for its cultivation is considered to be between February and March and October, because the growth of papaya grown in these months is very good.

Seed quantity 500 grams of seed is sufficient for one hectare of land. Select seeds that are fully ripe, well drained and not older than 6 months.

Sowing Method Seeds are sown between July to September and February-March, take seeds from good quality and healthy fruits. Prepare good quality seeds in the nursery. For sowing seeds, the bed which should be high above the ground, apart from this, you can also sow in a big pot or wooden box.Keep the beds high above the ground and cover the sown beds with dry grass or straw and water in the morning and evening. Seeds set in about 15-20 days after sowing, 4-5 leaves and 25 cm. Planting should be done in the field after 2 months of reaching height. In northern India, sowing should be done in nursery in March-April, June-August.

Method of Planting In the month of May, make pits of size 50 * 50 * 50 cm at a distance of 2 * 2 meters in a well prepared field. And leave them open for 15 days so that the pits get good sunlight and the harmful insects - insects and germs etc. are destroyed. Then later plant the pits, after planting, fill the pit with 50 grams of Aldrin soil and cow dung manure in such a way that it remains 10-15 cm high from the ground.Irrigate after filling the pit. Generally, plants are planted in June-July or February-March, but in areas with high rainfall and winter, they should be planted in September or February-March. Light irrigation should be given daily in the afternoon till the plants germinate well.

Seed treatment Before sowing seeds, the seed should be treated with 3 grams of captan and one kg of seed with Mancozeb.

Favorable Climate Papaya can be cultivated well in hot humid climate. It can be grown at maximum temperature of 38 C to 44 C, minimum should not be less than 5 C, heat and frost cause great damage to papaya.

Land Selection Light loamy soil is good for successful production of papaya, whose pH is between 6.5 to 7.5. There should be a good drainage system in the field because due to waterlogging, the stem of the plant starts rotting.

Farm Preparation The field should be well plowed 2-3 times well before planting the saplings. The field should be kept smooth, flat, well drained and weed free.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers Papaya starts bearing fruit early. So it needs more fertile land. For good harvest, 20-25 kg of rotted manure, vermi compost, one kg of bone meal and one kg of neem cake thrice a year should be given per plant in March-April, July-August and October months.Apart from this, an average of 200 grams of nitrogen, 250 grams of phosphorus and 500 grams of potash per plant is beneficial in chemical fertilizers. Keep in mind that the amount of Nitrogen should be divided into 6 parts and applied at an interval of 2 months and Phosphorus and Potash should be given in 2 times. Spread the fertilizers around the plant at a distance of 25-30 cm from the stem and mix it into the soil.

Harmful diseases and kits and their control measures Mainly no insects cause harm, but viruses help in spreading diseases. To control harmful diseases and pests, do the treatment according to the advice of the agricultural expert.

Weed control In papaya cultivation, weeding and hoeing should be done according to the need for weed control and protection of nutrients. Due to continuous irrigation, the surface of the soil becomes hard, which affects the growth of the plant, so after 2-3 irrigations, weeding should be done.

Irrigation Irrigation is essential for good production of papaya. Irrigation should be given at an interval of 6-7 days in summer and 10-12 days in winter. In the rainy season, when there is no rain for a long time, irrigation is required. The modern method of irrigation can be adopted by drip technology.

Fruit Picking Fully ripe papaya fruits should be harvested along with the stalk when the top part of the fruit starts to turn yellow. After harvesting healthy, uniform sized fruits should be separated and rotten fruits should be removed separately.

Production Generally, the improved varieties of papaya give 35-50 kg per plant, while the new variety gives 2-3 times more yield. One hectare crop of papaya with healthy trees gives a yield of 250-400 quintals.
