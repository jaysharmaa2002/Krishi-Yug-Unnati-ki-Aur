Banana

Banana- Banana is a major popular nutritious food fruit around the world. Ripe bananas are used as fruits and raw bananas are used for making vegetables and flour or chips. The first evidence of banana was found 4000 years ago in Malaysia. India became the largest producer of bananas in the world. In India, banana cultivation is done the most in states like Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu, Gujarat, Madhya Pradesh, Andhra Pradesh, Bihar, Karnataka and Assam.

A banana is an elongated, edible fruit- botanically a berry - produced by several kinds of large herbaceous flowering plants in the genus Musa.In some countries, bananas used for cooking may be called "plantains", distinguishing them from dessert bananas. The fruit is variable in size, color, and firmness, but is usually elongated and curved, with soft flesh rich in starch covered with a rind, which may be green, yellow, red, purple, or brown when ripe. The fruits grow in clusters hanging from the top of the plant. Almost all modern edible seedless (parthenocarp) bananas come from two wild species - Musa acuminata and Musa balbisiana. The scientific names of most cultivated bananas are Musa acuminata, Musa balbisiana, and Musa X paradisiaca for the hybrid Musa acuminata X M. balbisiana, depending on their genomic constitution. The old scientific name for this hybrid, Musa sapientum, is no longer used.

Musa species are native to tropical Indomalaya and Australia, and are likely to have been first domesticated in Papua New Guinea.They are grown in 135 countries,primarily for their fruit, and to a lesser extent to make fiber, banana wine, and banana beer and as ornamental plants. The world's largest producers of bananas in 2017 were India and China, which together accounted for approximately 38% of total production


Crop Variety Robusta, Dwarf Cavendish, Grand Nine, Rusthali, Vial Vazhai, Poovan, Nendran, Red Banana, Karpoorvalli, Matti, Sannchenkadali, Udayam and Nepovanov.

Time of sowing Planting is done from February to May whereas in North India, it is done during July-August. In South-India, it can be done at any time of the year except in summer.

Distance In North India, coastal belt and areas with high humidity and low temperature i.e. below 5-7 C, planting distance should not be less than 1.8m x 1.8m.

Seed Depth Cut the roots of the banana to 45x 45x45 cm. or 60x60x60 cm Plant in pits of size. Leave the pits open in the sun, it will kill the harmful insects. Fill the pits with 10 kg of compost or decomposed cow dung, 250 gm of neem cake and 20 gm of carbofuron. Plant the roots in the middle of the pit and press it well around the soil. Do not print deeply.

Sowing Method For sowing, transplanting method is used.

Seed quantity If the distance is taken as 1.8 X 1.5 m, then plant 1452 saplings per acre. If the distance is 2 m X 2.5 m, it is recommended to plant 800 saplings in one acre.

Seed Treatment For planting, use healthy and infection-free roots or rhizomes. Before planting, wash the roots and then dip in solution of Chlorpyrifos 20EC @ 2.5 ml/Lit of water. To protect the crop from rhizome rhizomes and knots from nematodes, before transplanting, dip the roots in Carbofuron 3% CG @ 50 g and then dry in shade for 72 hours.To prevent Fusarium drought, immerse the roots in Carbendazim@2gm/Ltr of water for 15-20 minutes.

Earth Soil depth and drainage are the two most important factors in selecting soil for banana cultivation. The soil in the field should be 0.5 to 1 meter deep for its good production. Soil should have a pH range of 6.5 to 7.5 with moisture retention, abundance of fertile organic matter.

Farm Preparation For banana cultivation, in the beginning, remove the remnants of old crops present in the field and do deep plowing of the field. After that do two to three good slant plowing in the field through cultivator. After plowing, level the field by putting a pad.

Climate Banana plants do best in protected areas, as they are susceptible to wind damage. An average temperature of 27 C (81 F) and full sun is also beneficial for optimum plant growth and yield. The optimum conditions for ripening bananas are at a temperature of 20-21 C (68-70 F) and 90% relative humidity.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers The rapid growth rate of the banana plant makes it a heavy feeder. At the time of last plowing, mix 10 tonnes of well decomposed cow dung or decomposed cow dung into the soil. Young plants may need pound of fertilizer per month. A balanced fertilizer 8-10-8 (NPK) is recommended. When planting a banana tree add aged manure to the hole, adding enough compost so that you have a 50:50 mixture of soil and compost.

Weed Control Remove weeds before transplanting by deep ploughing and cross harrowing. To control weeds, weeding should be done. And apply mulching to control weeds.

Irrigation The total water requirement of banana plants for their entire life cycle is about 900-1200 mm and this can be met through both natural rainfall (rain) and supplementary irrigation. Moisture should always be maintained in the banana field.

Harvesting Time The sown crop is ready for harvest within 11-12 months of planting. The first planting crop will be ready 8-10 months after harvesting the main crop and 8-9 months after the second crop.

Cleaning and Drying Bananas should be washed in clean water in the wash tank. Extended periods of washing should also be avoided as this can lead to the absorption of water from the banana. After that they should be dried and packed like going to the market.
