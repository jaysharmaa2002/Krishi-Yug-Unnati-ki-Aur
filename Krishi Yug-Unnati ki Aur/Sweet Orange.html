Sweet Orange- Improved cultivation of oranges is done on a large scale in the country. Orange is a citrus fruit. Orange is the most grown in India after banana and mango. Among the citrus fruits grown in India, Mandarin Orange is the most common. It accounts for about 40% of the total area under cultivation of oranges in India.Orange is mostly grown in Maharashtra, Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, Tamil Nadu, Assam, Orissa, West Bengal, Nagaland, Mizoram, Arunachal Pradesh. Oranges are rich in vitamins C, A, B and phosphorus. It is mainly used for eating and drinking after extracting juice. There are many beneficial benefits of drinking it.

The sweet orange is not a wild fruit, having arisen in domestication from a cross between a non-pure mandarin orange and a hybrid pomelo that had a substantial mandarin component. Since its chloroplast DNA is that of pomelo, it was likely the hybrid pomelo, perhaps a BC1 pomelo backcross, that was the maternal parent of the first orange.Based on genomic analysis, the relative proportions of the ancestral species in the sweet orange is approximately 42% pomelo and 58% mandarin. All varieties of the sweet orange descend from this original cross, differing only by mutations selected for during agricultural propagation.

Time of sowing Its planting in the spring season from February to March and the time of July to October is suitable for planting saplings.

Distance Keep a distance of 6x6 m for sweet oranges. Dig a 1x1x1 meter pit and leave it in the sun for a few days. Put 15-20 kg of cow dung, 500 gm single super phosphate in each pit.

Seed depth For germination of plant 75x75x75 cm. Prepare pits of size.

Method of preparing nursery for plants For orange cultivation, orange saplings are prepared in the nursery. Sow the seeds in nursery on a seed bed of size 2 m x 1 m. In which keep a distance of 15 cm from line to line. When the height of the plants is 10 to 12 cm. Plant the pits already prepared for horticulture.Keep one thing in mind that only healthy and similar sized plants should be used for transplanting. Remove small and weak plants beforehand. If necessary, before transplanting, prune the roots and treat the roots.

Seed quantity The density of 208 plants per acre should be maintained.

Earth Light loamy soil with proper drainage is good for the plant. It yields quite well. Waterlogged land is not suitable for orange cultivation. The pH value of the land should be around 5.5 to 6.5.

Farm Preparation For orange cultivation, in the beginning, remove the remains of old crops present in the field and do deep plowing of the field. After that do two to three good slant plowing in the field through cultivator. After plowing, level the field by putting a pad.

Climate Subtropical climate with an altitude of 500-500 m above MSL, requires approximately 150 cm to 250 cm of rainfall. Winter should be mild and there should be no strong, hot winds during summer. Season: November - December

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers
10 kg in the first year Dung manure, 150 grams nitrogen, 50 grams phosphorus, 75 grams potash.
20 kg in the second year. Dung manure, 300 g Nitrogen, 100 g Phosphorus, 150 g Potash.
30 kg in the third year. Dung manure, 450 grams of nitrogen, 150 grams of phosphorus, 225 grams of potash.
40 kg in the fourth year. Dung manure, 600 g Nitrogen, 200 g Phosphorus, 300 g Potash.
Fifth year and above 50 kg. Dung manure, 750 grams nitrogen, 250 grams phosphorus, 375 grams potash.
Apart from this, farmers can use zinc sulphate and other tonic fertilizers as per the requirement. Spraying of water soluble fertilizers has a good effect on the yield. Keep in mind that chemical fertilizers should be used on the basis of soil test.

Weed control Weeds in the orange field can be controlled by hand weeding or by chemicals, after weeding - use glyphosate 4 liters or per quart 2 liters per hectare by mixing 500 to 600 liters of water. As far as possible, use weedicide before flowering. Weedicide should not be used on main plants.

Irrigation Irrigation has to be taken care of for good production in orange cultivation. Irrigation is given at an interval of 10 to 12 days from March to June, whereas irrigation is not done in the rainy season, from September to December, irrigation should be done at an interval of 20 days.

Harvest Period Mandarin flowers appear in March and April and are highly fragrant, attracting many bees. Fruit set is in May and requires six to eight months to fully ripen. Growing trees need watering at least once a week or twice during heat waves or in dry areas.

Harvesting The fruits are harvested when they attain full size. When the color of the fruits appear yellow and attractive. Then they should be cut and separated along with the stalk. Due to which the fruit remains fresh for a long time. Fruits should not be harvested in wet weather or during rains. After harvesting the oranges, take the tail with a clean damp cloth and dry it in a shady place. After this, the fruits are stuffed in a ventilated box with dry grass. Now you can close the box and send it to the market.

Cleaning and Drying Fruits that are oblong, immature, puffy (swollen), bloated (with spots on the surface of the fruit), deformed, (not having a natural shape), dark green, fruit (dark fruit) and clean due to disease are removed during

After Harvesting Harvested fruits are usually washed with chlorine (1000 ppm) and after removing surface water they are coated with Stayfresh High Shine Wax (2.5%) containing Bavistin (4000 ppm) and finally Dries at 500-550C in tunnel dryer.The fruits are usually packed in wooden boxes for distant markets, while local marketing baskets of bamboo and mulberries are used for division. Shredded straw and hay are mostly used for padding. The fruit should be lightly cleaned and polished with a piece of cloth before wrapping it in tissue paper or newspaper. Use of ventilated corrugated fiber board cartons in place of wooden boxes is highly beneficial.

Transportation Mandarins are usually transported by rail or road as simple cargo without refrigeration.
