Sorghum

Sorghum- Jowar is the second important coarse cereal crop in India after millet. Whose cereal crops are fifth after rice, wheat, maize and millet. Due to high temperature and drought tolerance, this crop is easily grown in high temperature and low fertility soils of rainfed areas.Due to the deep root system, jowar absorbs water from the lower layers of the soil, due to this ability it can be grown easily in areas with low rainfall. Jowar crop is mainly grown in Maharashtra, Karnataka, Madhya Pradesh, Andhra Pradesh, Haryana and Rajasthan.

Sorghum is truly a versatile crop that can be grown as a grain, forage or sweet crop. Sorghum is one of the top five cereal crops in the world. Sorghum is among the most efficient crops in conversion of solar energy and use of water and is known as a high-energy, drought tolerant crop that is environmentally friendly.


Crop Variety :- For fodder production, most farmers in India plant local varieties of jowar, which are affected by pests and diseases along with giving less production.
Hybrid Varieties - Maharashtra - CSH 14, CSH 9, CSH 16, CSH 18, CSV 13, CSV 15, SPV 699, Karnataka - CSH 14, CSH 17, C SH 16, C SH 13, C SH 18.
Package Varieties: V 13, CS V 15, G J 35, G J 38, G J 40, G J 39, G J 41.

Seed Quantity:- The quantity of jowar seed at the time of sowing is 15-20 kg per hectare. And to get fodder in summer, sowing is done in the month of March. For multi-harvesting, the seed quantity should be 30 -40 kg per hectare.

Time of sowing :- The best time for sowing jowar in northern India is the first week of July. It is not advisable to sow before the last week of June, as there is a possibility of more rain during flowering in the early crop of jowar. In the southern states of the country like Maharashtra, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh and Tamil Nadu, where jowar is grown during the Rabi season, it is best to sow it between September 15 and October 15.

Seed Treatment / Nursery :- To protect the crop from soil borne diseases, treat the seed with 300 mash 4 g of sulfur powder and Azotobacter at 25 g per kg before sowing.

Earth :- Loamy and sandy soils are considered suitable for the cultivation of jowar. It can be sown in heavy soil with proper drainage.pH of the land values 6.5-7 are suitable.

Favorable Climate :- Jowar is a crop of warm climate, cultivation of jowar can be done easily in areas up to 1500 meters above sea level. The minimum temperature for jowar germination is 9 to 10 degree Celsius. The optimum average temperature for plant growth has been found to be 26 to 30C. The temperature of more than 30 degree Celsius at the time of maize emergence in the crop is harmful for the crop.

Land preparation: After plowing the field thoroughly with country plow or cultivator two-three times, run the pada. After plowing, 100 q/ha of rotted manure of cow dung in the field. Add it to the field and mix it well. There should be proper drainage system.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers :- Crop plants require nutrients for proper growth. Therefore, while preparing the land before sowing, good decomposed manure or compost at the rate of 10-15 tonnes per hectare should be used for jowar crop. Apart from this, 40 kg phosphorus should be used in chemical fertilizers by spraying full quantity and 80 kg nitrogen half quantity at the time of sowing and remaining quantity after 30 days of sowing. Use half the amount of chemical fertilizers in places with heavy rainfall.

Harmful pests and diseases and their prevention:- There is an outbreak of stem fly and stem borer in jowar crop. For its prevention, at the time of sowing 15 kg per hectare with phorate 10% or carbofuran 3 g. Dana should be put. For the prevention of fungal diseases, Mancozeb 0.2 percent solution should be sprayed.

Weed control :- Weeding should be done with weeder less culture at the stage of 25-30 days. Spraying of Atrazine (0.5-0.75 kg/ha, 600 liters of water) before deposition is effective for the crop.

Irrigation :- Jowar crops are generally rainfed crops. Moisture is the most important place for proper growth of jowar plants. For irrigated areas, when sufficient moisture is not received by rain, irrigation should be done from time to time. 3 - 4 irrigations are sufficient for jowar crop.Keep in mind that there should be moisture in the field at the time of grain formation. Due to this, the development of grain is good and the yield of grain and fodder increases. There are four critical stages of irrigation for jowar crop, which are as follows- the stage of early seedling, the stage before the emergence of the cob, the stage of emergence and the stage of grain formation. Lack of water in the above conditions adversely affects the yield of jowar.

Harvesting time :- Harvest when the hairs are mature, 35-40 days after flowering. After drying the corn thoroughly in the sun for 2 to 3 days, remove its grain from the corn and separate it. After harvesting, jowar cobs should be allowed to dry in the barn for at least one week. Coats are plucked by beating them with sticks, by oxen, or by shreshers. Immediately after threshing, the grains are separated from the straw by milling.

Production :- If the cultivation of jowar is done by adopting all the above advanced methods, then on average 35 to 45 quintal grain in hybrid sorghum irrigated condition and 100 to 120 quintal bitter and 20 to 30 quintal grain in unirrigated (rainfed) areas and 70 to 80 quintal bitter per hectare. is received.
