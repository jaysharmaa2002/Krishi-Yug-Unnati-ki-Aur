Chickpeas

Chickpea- Chickpea is a major pulse crop. Chana is called the king of pulses. In terms of nutritional value, an average of 11 grams of water, 21.1 grams of protein, 4.5 grams in 100 grams of gram grains. Fat, 61.5 g. Carbohydrates, 149 mg. Calcium, 7.2 mg. Iron, 0.14 mg. riboflavin and 2.3 mg. Niacin is found. The main producing countries of gram are India, Pakistan, Ethiopia, Burma and Turkey etc.
Its production is highest in the whole world in India followed by Pakistan. The main gram producing states in India are Madhya Pradesh, Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh, Haryana, Maharashtra and Punjab.The name chickpea comes from the Latin word cicer, referring to the plant family of legumes, Fabaceae. It is also known by its popular Spanish-derived name, the garbanzo bean. These plants produce edible seeds, called pulses, that have high nutritional value.


Gram classification
Chickpea Cultivation - Chickpea rich in many qualities is also known as CHICKPEA and BENGAL GRAM. Gram is classified into two castes-
Cicer arietinum: Known as native gram.
Sisar Kabulium: It is known as Kabuli Chana.

Improved Varieties of Chickpeas Green chickpeas No. 01, Gaurav (H 75-35), Radhe, Chafa, K. 4, K. 408, K. 850, Atul (Pusa 413), Ajay (Pusa 408), Amar (203), Girnar.

Seed quantity
For indigenous gram varieties-70-80 kg per hectare
For Kabuli gram species- 100-125 kg per hectare
Late sowing-90-100 kg per hectare

Seed treatment To prevent fungal diseases on gram crop, treatment should be done by mixing 250 grams of medicine in the quantity of 0.25 ie 100 kg seed of thyram and captan. Seed should be treated with Rhizobium culture for early germination.

Time of sowing In plains - 15 October to first week of November
In Terai areas - from 15th November to the whole month

Transfer and distance Sowing of gram gives good yield by keeping the distance from plant to plant at 30 to 45 cms.

Gram Sowing Method For sowing, farmers can take the help of dibbler, sow the seed at a depth of 5 to 8 cm on the mark which is formed through the dibbler.

Land Selection This type of soil is considered suitable for gram crop with proper drainage loam land which is flat, gram crop is done successfully in sandy loam, matiyar loam and black soil. For the development of its plants, the pH of the soil should be from 6.5 to 7.5, so that the crop yield is good.

Land Preparation After 1 ploughing with land-reversing plow, do 2-3 ploughings with indigenous plow or cultivator. After every plowing, the land should be made lump-free by rolling the pad in the field and making it flat.

Climate and Temperature Chana is one such crop which comes in cold and dry season crop. Apart from this, if we talk about the germination of gram, then it also requires high temperature. Areas with very low and moderate rainfall or 65 to 95 cm of rainfall are suitable for better growth of gram plant.

Manure and Fertilizers The gram plant contains bacteria called Rhizobium to absorb nitrogen from the atmosphere. After germination in gram, it takes 25-30 days for the formation of bacterial glands, in such a situation, 15 to 20 kg of nitrogen and 40 to 50 phosphorus and 40 to 60 kg of potash should be given per hectare.

Diseases and control of gram crop
Uktha disease - It is called fungal disease. This raised disease in the crop is caused by a fungus named Fusarium Arthosoras. The color of the leaves turns yellow. The stem turns black. This disease has a bad effect on the production of gram, the plant or leaf in which this disease occurs, then it stops the growth of the plant.
Prevention and Prevention: Seeds should be treated with thiram or captan before sowing. Sow gram in the last week of October only. For gram cultivation, the resistant varieties like Uktha resistant, BG0244, BG 266, ICCC 32, CG 588, GNG 146, etc. should be selected.

Gram Rust or Gerui - This disease is caused by a fungus called Uromyces Cicerisarietira. In severe outbreaks of the disease, the sick leaves start turning and drying up. Most of the outbreaks of this disease in gram crop occur in the states of Uttar Pradesh, Punjab. Prevention and prevention: Before sowing, treat the seeds with thiram or captan, apart from this, if you want, you can spray Dithane M 45 @ 0.2% after 10 days of sowing.

Gray mold disease of chickpea - This fungal disease is originated due to fungus named Botrytis cinerea. It lives as a survivor in a gram field. It adversely affects both the yield and the quality of the grains. Prevention and Prevention: Seeds should be treated with thiram or captan before sowing.

Chickpea blight disease-In this disease the plants turn yellow and dry up, its outbreak falls on the cultivation of gram sown in North-West India. Small brown spots appear on the stem, leaves and fruits of the affected plant. Prevention and Prevention: The blight resistant variety C 235 of gram should be grown. Seeds should be treated with Thiram or Captan @ 2.5 g/kg seed before sowing.

Weed control For weed control in gram crop, first weeding should be done after 25-30 days of sowing by hand or with a weeding wheel and if needed, second weeding after 60 days of sowing. For effective weed control, spray one acre before sowing by dissolving Pendimethalin in 1 liter per 200 liters of water, 3 days after sowing. If there is less damage, instead of weedicide, do weeding by hand or remove grass from somewhere. This keeps the soil airy.

Irrigation If there is proper arrangement of irrigation in gram crop, give water once before sowing. Due to this the seeds germinate well and the growth of the crop is also good. Apply second water before flowering and third time at the time of pod development. In case of early rains, irrigation should be done late and as needed. Unnecessary watering reduces crop growth and yields. This crop cannot tolerate excessive standing of water, for this, make arrangements for good drainage.

Gram harvesting About 120-130 days after sowing, when gram grains become hard. Harvest the crop with the help of a scythe and a scythe. On an average, it takes 150-170 days for the cultivation of gram to take all the agricultural operations together.

Chickpea yield Average yield from gram cultivation-
In unirrigated area - 15-20 quintals per hectare
In irrigated area - 25-30 quintals per hectare

Storage After drying, when 10-12 percent moisture remains in the grains, store them in the warehouse.
