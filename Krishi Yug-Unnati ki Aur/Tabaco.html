TABACCO

Tabacco- Tobacco crop is a low labor commercial and cash crop, the use of tobacco is injurious to health. It is also called slow poison. Tobacco is dried and used in smoke and smokeless intoxicants. Tobacco is used to make cigarettes, bidi, cigars, pan masalas, zarda and khaini and many more.
Almost all the states in India grow tobacco, but the important ones are Andhra Pradesh, Gujarat, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Bihar, West Bengal and Uttar Pradesh. India produces a wide variety of commercial types of tobacco.

Tobacco is the common name of several plants in the Nicotiana genus of the Solanaceae family, and the general term for any product prepared from the cured leaves of these plants. More than 70 species of tobacco are known, but the chief commercial crop is N. tabacum.


Time of sowing The proper time for planting tobacco is considered as 20 September to 10 October.

Sowing method Tobacco seeds are sown in nurseries. After the seedlings are ready in the nursery, they are planted in the field. The time for nursery preparation is from 15th August to 15th September.

Distance 90X75 cm for plantation. Distance is the best.

Seed quantity Seed quantity 1.2-2 kg/acre seed is sufficient for sowing depending on the variety. And the number of plants for plantation is about 6000 saplings/acre.

Seed treatment Before sowing seeds in nursery, treatment with Thiram or Mancozeb @ 2.5 g/kg of seed should be done.

Favorable climate Tobacco cultivation requires a cool and dry climate, with a maximum rainfall of 100 cm for its cultivation. Its plant needs temperatures around 15 degrees for germination, and around 20 degrees for growth.

Land Selection Red loam and light friable soils are best suited for tobacco cultivation, whose pH is higher.Values 6-8 are suitable.

Farm preparation The field should be plowed 2-3 times before sowing, at the time of last ploughing, the field should be leveled and friable. And the field should be kept weed free.

Fertilizers and chemical Fertilizers At the time of field preparation, 7-10 tonnes/acre of cow dung and NPK. Apply 40:40:25 kg/acre as basal. Also, the amount of calcium should be given at the rate of 50 kg / acre.

Weed control To control weeds, weeding should be done from time to time as required.

Irrigation The first irrigation of tobacco plants should be done immediately after planting, after the first irrigation, light irrigation should be given to its plants every 15 days. And irrigation should be continued as needed.

Pruning It is very important to take care of the tobacco plant, to get the maximum quantity of tobacco, the buds (flower buds) and side branches on the plants should be broken, but keep in mind that if it is cultivated to make seeds, then the buds should be broken. should not be broken.

Harvest Tobacco crop is ready for maturity in 120 to 130 days, after which it should be harvested when its lower leaves start drying and become hard, its plants should be cut from near the root.

After harvesting Tobacco is cut and dried in the field for two to three days, after which they are collected in one place, covered for a few days or buried in the soil, during drying, its plants keep turning, at the time of drying The more moisture and whiteness comes in its plants, the better the tobacco becomes. After drying, its plants are harvested and prepared.

Production The production of tobacco crop is about 2 to 2.5 quintals/acre. And in the southern black soil, its production is obtained from 5 to 7 quintals / acre.
