Soyabean

Soybean- [scientific name="Glycine max"] is a soybean crop. Soybean is an important crop in India. It is considered a crop of oilseeds instead of pulses. The production of soybean has been increasing continuously since 1985 and the consumption of soybean oil is the second highest after peanut and mustard oil. Soybeans are an important food source. Its main components are proteins, carbohydrates and fats. Soyabean contains 38-40 percent protein, 22 percent oil, 21 percent carbohydrate, 12 percent moisture and 5 percent ash. 60% of the world's soybeans are produced in America. Madhya Pradesh produces maximum soybean in India. There is a Soyabean Research Center at Indore in Madhya Pradesh.Soy beans contain significant amounts of phytic acid, dietary minerals and B vitamins. Soy vegetable oil, used in food and industrial applications, is another product of processing the soybean crop. Soybean is the most important protein source for feed farm animals (that in turn yields animal protein for human consumption).

Selection of suitable varieties:- Different varieties have been developed for different regions by National Soybean Research Center and various agricultural universities for the agro-climate and soil there and varieties have been supported. Accordingly, soybean varieties should be sown. By bringing a little seed of these varieties, it can be grown and used on its own.


Time of sowing :- Last week of June till first week of July is the most suitable time, for good germination at the time of sowing, the soil should have suitable moisture up to 10 cm depth. After the first week of July, the seed rate of sowing should be increased by 5-10%.

Seed Quantity:-

  • Small grain varieties-70 kg per hectare
  • Medium grain variety-80 kg per hectare
  • Large grain varieties-100 kg per hectare.

Seed Treatment:- Treat soybean seeds before sowing. Treat seed with Carbendizim or Thiram 2 g/kg seed 24hrs or use 4 g/kg seed with talc formulation of Trichoderma (or) Pseudomonas fluorescens 10 g/kg seed.

Soil Testing :- The main elements in the soil for balanced fertilizer and soil health - nitrogen, phosphorous, potash, secondary nutrients like sulphur, calcium, magnesium and micronutrients like zinc, copper, iron, manganese, molybdenum, boron as well as pH, E .C. And get the organic matter tested.

Earth:- Soybean cultivation can be done successfully in all types of land except more light sandy and light land, but smooth loam land with water drainage is more suitable for soybean. Do not take soybeans wherever water stagnates in the field.

Climate :- Soybean crop requires dry hot climate, its seed set in 4 days at 25 degree C, whereas it takes 8 to 10 days for seed setting at lower temperature. Therefore, 60-65 cm for soybean cultivation. Rainy places are considered suitable.

Field Plowing:- After the onset of rain, the field should be prepared by using bakhar and pata 2 or 3 times. This will destroy all the stages of harmful insects. As far as possible, do the last bakhrani and pata in time so that the germinated weeds can be destroyed.

Fertilizers :- While preparing the field before sowing soyabean, mix well decomposed manure (compost) 5 ton per hectare in the field at the time of last harvest and 20 kg Nitrogen, 60 kg Phosphorous, 20 kg Potash and 20 kg Sulfur at the time of sowing. per hectare. This quantity can be increased or decreased on the basis of soil test. Zinc sulphate at the rate of 50 kg per hectare in deep black soils and 25 kg per hectare in shallow soils should be applied after taking 5 to 6 crops.

Harmful Pests and Prevention :- Whitefly - For control of whitefly, take spray of Thiamethoxam@40gm or Trizophos@300ml/acre. If required, do second spray after 10 days of first spray.

Tobacco caterpillar -If infestation of this pest is observed, take spray of Acephate 57SP@800gm or Chlorpyriphos 20EC@1.5Ltr of water per acre. If needed, do second spray after 10 days of first spray.

Hairy caterpillar -When the attack of hairy caterpillar is less, eliminate it by lifting it with hands or putting it in kerosene. If its attack is more, then Quinalphos 300 ml. Or take spray of Dichlorvas@200ml/acre.

Yellow Pied Disease -It is spread by whitefly. This causes irregular yellow-green spots to appear on the leaves. Pods do not develop on affected plants. For its prevention, use resistant varieties of yellow spot disease. To control white fly, take spray of Thiamethoxam@40gm or Trizophos@400ml/acre. If required, do second spray after 10 days of first spray.

Fungus Control :- Carbendazim 50 WP or Thiophanate Methyl 70 WP 0.05% to 1 gram per liter of water should be sprayed for the control of various speckled fungal diseases on the leaves of soybean. First spray should be done at 30-35 days stage and second spray at 40-45 days stage.

Control of a disease called bacterial pestilence -200 ppm of streptocycline or kasugamycin. 200 mg; Spraying of mixture of solution of medicine per liter of water and solution of copper oxychloride 0.2 (2 grams per liter) of water should be done.

Virus-borne yellow mosaic virus disease and wad vlight disease are often spread by aphid, whitefly, thrips etc. Therefore, only disease free healthy seeds should be used. and thiomethazone 70 w.v. for disease-carrying insects. Treat at the rate of 3 grams per kilogram and repeat at 30 days interval. Remove diseased plants from the field. Ethofenprax 10 EC 1 liter per hectare thiomethazem 25 wg, 1000 g per hectare.

Weed control:- Weed control is very important for the first 30 to 40 days of the crop. Control weeds by running dora or kulfa after the arrival of goats and do the second weeding after 30 and 45 days after germination. In standing crop of 15 to 20 days, to destroy the weeds of the grass family, Kuzelophop Ethyl one liter per hectare or for the grass group and some broad-leafed weeds, Imagthafire 750 ml. Spraying is recommended at the rate of liters per hectare.

Irrigation :- Generally, soybean does not require irrigation as it is a Kharif season crop. At the time of seed filling of beans, i.e. in the month of September, if there is not enough moisture in the field, then one or two light irrigations as per the requirement are beneficial for getting abundant production of soybean. If there is no rain, suitable irrigation can be done at the right time according to the moisture.

Harvesting of Crop :- The crop is ready for harvesting when the soybean pods dry up and the leaves turn yellow and the leaves drop. Harvest by hand or with a rake. After harvesting, the crop should be dried well in the sun for 2-3 days. Threshers, tractors, vines and other agricultural tools should be used to remove the seeds from the plants after drying.

Seed Storage :- After drying the soybean seeds thoroughly in the sun, clean the seeds thoroughly. Remove small seeds, affected seeds and stalks. Select a dry place to store the seed and keep it away from moisture.
