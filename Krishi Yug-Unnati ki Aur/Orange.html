Orange

Orange- Improved cultivation of oranges is done on a large scale in the country. Orange is a citrus fruit. Orange is the most grown in India after banana and mango. It is mainly used for eating and drinking after extracting juice. There are many beneficial benefits of drinking it. It protects us from many diseases.Its juice provides coolness to the body and removes fatigue and stress. In India, Rajasthan, Himachal Pradesh, Punjab, Haryana and Uttar Pradesh are the major orange growing states. The Nagpur orange variety is prevalent in Madhya Pradesh.

The orange is the fruit of various citrus species in the family Rutaceae (see list of plants known as orange); it primarily refers to Citrus X sinensis, which is also called sweet orange, to distinguish it from the related Citrus X aurantium, referred to as bitter orange. The sweet orange reproduces asexually (apomixis through nucellar embryony); varieties of sweet orange arise through mutations.

The orange is a hybrid between pomelo (Citrus maxima) and mandarin (Citrus reticulata).The chloroplast genome, and therefore the maternal line, is that of pomelo. The sweet orange has had its full genome sequenced.

The orange originated in a region encompassing Southern China, Northeast India, and Myanmar,and the earliest mention of the sweet orange was in Chinese literature in 314 BC. As of 1987, orange trees were found to be the most cultivated fruit tree in the world. Orange trees are widely grown in tropical and subtropical climates for their sweet fruit. The fruit of the orange tree can be eaten fresh, or processed for its juice or fragrant peel. As of 2012, sweet oranges accounted for approximately 70% of citrus production. In 2019, 79 million tonnes of oranges were grown worldwide, with Brazil producing 22% of the total, followed by China and India.


Time of sowing Its planting is the spring season from February to March and the time of July to October is suitable for planting saplings.

Distance Keep a distance of 5x5m for sweet oranges. Dig a 1x1x1 meter pit and leave it in the sun for a few days. Put 15-20 kg of cow dung, 500 gm single super phosphate in each pit.

Seed Depth For germination of the plant 60X60X60 cm. Prepare pits of size.

Method of preparing nursery for plants For orange cultivation, orange saplings are prepared in the nursery. Sow the seeds in nursery on a seed bed of size 2 m x 1 m. In which keep a distance of 15 cm from line to line. When the height of the plants is 10 to 12 cm. Plant the pits prepared in advance for horticulture. Keep one thing in mind that only healthy and similar sized plants should be used for transplanting.Remove small and weak plants beforehand. If necessary, before transplanting, prune the roots and treat the roots.

Seed quantity The density of 208 plants per acre should be maintained.

Earth Light loamy soil with proper drainage is good for the plant. It yields quite well. Waterlogged land is not suitable for orange cultivation. The pH value of the land should be around 6 to 8.

Farm Preparation For orange cultivation, in the beginning, remove the remains of old crops present in the field and do deep plowing of the field. After that do two to three good slant plowing in the field through cultivator. After plowing, level the field by putting a pad.

Climate For orange gardening, maximum temperature of 16 to 20 and maximum 32 to 40 degree is required. In normal climate, vegetative growth occurs in the orange plant. Low temperature and cold/frost cause major damage to the cultivation of oranges.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers
10 kg in the first year Dung manure, 150 grams nitrogen, 50 grams phosphorus, 75 grams potash.
20 kg in the second year. Dung manure, 300 g Nitrogen, 100 g Phosphorus, 150 g Potash.
30 kg in the third year. Dung manure, 450 g Nitrogen, 150 g Phosphorus, 225 g Potash.
40 kg in the fourth year. Dung manure, 600 g Nitrogen, 200 g Phosphorus, 300 g Potash.
Fifth year and above 50 kg. Dung manure, 750 grams nitrogen, 250 grams phosphorus, 375 grams potash.
Apart from this, farmers can use zinc sulphate and other tonic fertilizers as per the requirement. Spraying of water soluble fertilizers has a good effect on the yield. Keep in mind that chemical fertilizers should be used on the basis of soil test.

Weed control Weeds in the orange field can be controlled by hand weeding or by chemicals, after weeding - use glyphosate 4 liters or per quart 2 liters per hectare by mixing 500 to 600 liters of water. As far as possible, use weedicide before flowering. Weedicide should not be used on main plants.

Irrigation Irrigation has to be taken care of for good production in orange cultivation. Irrigation is given at an interval of 10 to 12 days from March to June, whereas irrigation is not done in the rainy season, from September to December, irrigation should be done at an interval of 20 days.

Harvesting The fruits are harvested when they attain full size. When the color of the fruits appear yellow and attractive. Then they should be cut and separated along with the stalk. Due to which the fruit remains fresh for a long time. Fruits should not be harvested in wet weather or during rains. After harvesting the oranges, take the tail with a clean damp cloth and dry it in a shady place. After this, the fruits are stuffed in a ventilated box with dry grass. Now you can close the box and send it to the market.

After Harvesting Harvested fruits are usually rinsed with chlorine (1000 ppm) and after removing surface water they are coated with Stayfresh High Shine Wax (2.5%) containing Bavistin (4000 ppm) and finally Dries at 500-550C in tunnel dryer.
