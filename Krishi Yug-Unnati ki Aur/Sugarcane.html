Sugarcane

Sugarcane- It is a major cash crop of the world, from which sugar, jaggery, liquor etc. are manufactured. Sugarcane is the largest producer in Brazil and India ranks second in the world in terms of sugarcane productivity. Sugarcane is cultivated in India on about 32 lakh hectares of land, giving a yield of 1800 lakh tonnes of sugarcane.

Thus the average yield of sugarcane is about 57 tonnes per hectare which is much less than the production potential. Maharashtra has the highest share of sugar making in India which is 34 percent and Uttar Pradesh comes second.Sugarcane or sugar cane refers to several species and hybrids of tall perennial grass in the genus Saccharum, tribe Andropogoneae, that are used for sugar production. The plants are 2-6 m (6-20 ft) tall with stout, jointed, fibrous stalks that are rich in sucrose, which accumulates in the stalk internodes.


Time of sowing
Sugarcane is sown twice a year.
- Autumn sowing and spring sowing.
-Sugarcane is sown in October-November autumn and February-March in spring.
-Autumn sugarcane gives 25 to 30 percent more yield than spring-sown sugarcane and 30 to 40 percent more than summer sugarcane.

Distance 35 to 45 quintals of sugarcane seed per acre (at the rate of 12 eyes per meter) for sowing at 90 cm distance.

Seed depth Cut sugarcane by 3-4 cm. Sow at a depth of 1 and cover it with soil.

Seed Treatment / Nursery For seed borne disease and pest control, make a solution of Carbendazim 2 gm per liter of water and Chlorpyriphos 5 ml per hectare and treat the required seed for 15 to 20 minutes.

Soil treatment Trichoderma virdi 5 kg per hectare mixed with 150 kg vermi compost, keeping it moist for a day or two, sprinkle it in the litter before sowing or at the time of first hoeing, it provides relief from fungal diseases.

Land selection Deep soil with good drainage for sugarcane crop in which the water level is 1.5-2 cm. Yes, and the land that holds the water in the dam is beneficial. This crop tolerates salt and salinity. If the pH of the soil is less than 5, then add bud in the soil and if the pH is more than 9.5 then add gypsum to the soil.

Land preparation For sugarcane cultivation, the field should be plowed twice. First tillage 20-25 cm Should be Make the pebbles level by mechanically breaking them well.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers Before plowing, 20 tons of rotted cow dung or compost should be mixed evenly in the field. Apart from this, 300 kg Nitrogen (650 kg Urea), 85 kg Sphur (500 kg Single Super Phosphate) and 60 kg Potash (100 kg Murate Appotash) should be applied per hectare. Keep in mind that chemical fertilizers should be used only on the basis of soil test.

Pest Control Advance borer- Damage of this insect is possible for 15 to 100 days, one larvy damages many stems by going underground. Due to this a dead heart is formed and the infected plant cannot be saved and many buds emerge from the side.

Weed control Weed control is necessary for about 4 months after sowing. For this you should condemn 3-4 times. For chemical control, spray atrazine at 160 g/acre in 325 liters of water before germination. Later, for weeds, spray 2-4 D sodium salt dissolved in 400 grams per acre in 325 liters of water. It is necessary to have moisture in the field at the time of spraying.

Irrigation Irrigate the soil with heavy soil at an interval of 8 to 10 days during summer and 15 days in winter. In light soils, irrigation should be given at an interval of 5 to 7 days during summer and with an interval of 10 days in winter. Irrigation Sugarcane crop requires more water. There is a need to have moisture in the soil at the time of crop formation, flowering and growth.

Harvesting time If the main crop is harvested in February-March, due to the low temperature, due to low temperature, there will be less swelling and less buds will be received in the tree crop. When harvesting sugarcane should be cut close to the surface of the ground. This will result in healthier and more buds.

After harvesting Sugarcane is used to extract juice. Apart from this, sugar, jaggery and molasses are obtained from sugarcane juice.

Production Capability About 1000 to 1500 quintals per hectare of sugarcane can be obtained by using the above advanced scientific techniques in sugarcane production.
