Jackfruit

Jackfruit- Jackfruit is the national fruit of Bangladesh and Sri Lanka, while in the Indian states of Kerala and Tamil Nadu it has also been given the status of a state fruit. The fruit of jackfruit is used both raw and ripe, but its importance is more in the vegetable. Jackfruit fruit is also used for pickles.Jackfruit plant is an evergreen, 8-15 m tall. Growing high, spreading and having a dense area. Improved varieties of jackfruit being a fruit tree and mainly propagated by seed.

The scientific name for jackfruit is Artocarpus heterophyllus. Jackfruit is a tropical tree fruit grown in Asia, Africa and South America. Under its thick, bumpy rind is a stringy flesh that you can eat raw or cooked in a variety of dishes. Jackfruit is the largest tree-borne fruit in the world, weighing up to 40 pounds or more. Luckily, you don't need to throw this massive fruit into your shopping cart. Some health food stores carry peeled and cut jackfruit portions in pouches or cans, ready to cook or eat.


Famous Varieties Khajwa, Swaran Manohar, NJ-1, NJ-2, NJ-15 This is Swarna Purti (for vegetables), the variety is mainly used for vegetables in India.

Time of sowing The suitable time for transplanting jackfruit plants in India is from July to September. The varieties to be taken in July are a no. 1, Pleasant varieties are planted in July. The rainy season is considered the best for jackfruit cultivation.

Sowing Method While transplanting jackfruit plants, first prepare the plant by taking out the seeds from the jackfruit. After that, prepare pits of 1 meter diameter and 1 meter depth at a distance of 10 to 12 meters on flat land. In all these pits, 20 to 25 kg of cow dung should be filled with compost or compost, 250 g single super phosphate, 500 muriate of potash, 1 kg neem cake and 10 g thyme by mixing it well in the soil.

Climate Jackfruit is a tropical fruit, so it can be grown in both dry and moist climates. And its horticulture is done successfully in both dry and temperate climates.

Earth Any type of soil is suitable for jackfruit cultivation, but often its production is better in loam and sandy loam, so it is cultivated in this soil.

Farm Preparation For the cultivation of jackfruit, the land should be flat and friable. Prepare the field in such a way that there is no stagnation and accumulation of water anywhere in the field.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers Plants should be given adequate amount of manure and fertilizers for good yield. 20-25 kg to each plant. Decomposed manure of cow dung, 100 g. Urea, 200 g. single super phosphate and 100g. Murate of potash should be given every year in the month of July. After that, with the growth of the plant, the amount of manure should be increased.When the plant becomes 10 years old, then 80-100 kg. Dung manure, 1 kg Urea, 2 kg single super phosphate and 1 kg. Murate of potash should be given every year.

Weed Control Prevention of weeds in jackfruit cultivation, weeding, hoeing and applying soil on the roots. And mixed farming can be done in mango field. Mixed farming helps in controlling weeds from the crop. Cultivation of pulse crops such as moong, urad, lentil and gram etc. can be done as a mixed farming. Crops like onion, tomato, radish, legumes, cauliflower and cabbage can also be used for mixed farming. Do not use millet, maize and sugarcane for mixed farming.

Irrigation Jackfruit cultivation does not require much water, so if it is the rainy season, do not water the plant. If there is no rain, the plants should be watered as per the requirement. In the beginning, the plants will have to be watered. Irrigation should be continued every week in summer season and 15 days in winter season for the first few years.

Harvest period Jackfruit tree is considered an evergreen tree. But it takes 5 to 6 years for a tree to grow.

Harvesting time Jackfruit starts harvesting mainly in the month of January to March in our India. The fruits continue to develop from January-February to June-July. During this time, seeds, cocoons etc. develop inside the fruit and finally the fruits start ripening in June-July.

Production Capability Fruiting starts in 7-8 years in Biju plant of jackfruit whereas fruits start getting fruit in 4-5 years in Kamali plants. After 15 years of planting, the plant becomes fully developed, about 150 to 250 kg from a fully grown tree. Fruit is obtained every year. The fruit of jackfruit plant is low in the beginning and falls after fruiting.

Cleaning and Drying Jackfruit plants should be kept clean after weeding. Orchards of large trees should be plowed twice a year. Water should not freeze at all in the jackfruit orchard. After planting, the plants should be well taken care of for one year. From time to time weeding should be done by removing weeds in the plant plates.
