Moong

MoongIn India, moong is a major short-ripening pulse crop of both summer and kharif seasons. Its grain is mainly used for pulses, which contains 24-26% protein, 55-60% carbohydrate and 1.3% fat.Being a leguminous crop, lumps are found in its roots, which is the fixation of atmospheric nitrogen in the soil (38-40 kg nitrogen per hectare) and 1.5 tonnes of organic matter per hectare in the form of roots and leaves after harvesting from the crop field. are released into the soil, which maintains organic carbon in the soil and increases the fertility of the soil.
Green gram also known as moong is one of the main pulse crop of India. It is a rich source of Protein along with fibre and iron. It can be cultivated as Kharif as well as summer crop. In Punjab, near about 5.2 thousand hectares of area is under moong cultivation with total production of 4.5 thousand tones


Seed Rate and Seed Treatment :- Moong 20 kg/ha for kharif sowing by row method. is sufficient. 25-30 kg/ha for spring or summer sowing. Seeds are required. Before sowing, treat the seed with Carbendazim + Captan (1+2) at the rate of 3 grams per kg of seed.

Sowing Method:- In the rainy season, to get good production from these crops, it is appropriate to sow in rows or rows behind the plow. Row to row distance for Kharif crop is 30-45 cms. and 20-22.5 cm for spring (summer). Plant to plant distance is kept 10-15 cm. Keeping 4 cm should be sown at a depth off.

Suitable climate for the cultivation of Moong :- Moong requires moist and warm climate. It can be cultivated in rainy season. The temperature of 25-32 degree Celsius has been found favorable for its growth and development. 75-90 cm for moong. Areas with annual rainfall have been found suitable. There should be clear weather and 60% humidity at the time of ripening. Excess rainfall during ripening is harmful.

The above land for Mung cultivation :- For the cultivation of mung, loam to sandy loam lands whose Ph. 7.0 to 7.5 is best for this. There should be good drainage in the field.

Land preparation: For kharif crop, a deep plowing should be done with a soil-reversing plow and after making weed-free plowing 2-3 times with indigenous plow or cultivator, level the field by running a pad. To prevent termites, chlorpyriphos 1.5% powder should be mixed with 20-25 kg/ha in the soil at the time of field preparation.
For summer moong cultivation, immediately after harvesting of rabi crops, the field should be plowed immediately after leaving 4-5 days. After ploughing, make the field flat and fine-grained by applying 2-3 ploughings with native plow or cultivator. Due to this, moisture is conserved in it and the seeds get good germination.

Fertilizers and Fertilizers :- For good production in mung cultivation, before sowing, while preparing the field, well decomposed cow dung should be mixed in the soil at the rate of 15 - 20 ton / acre. The amount of chemical fertilizers and fertilizers is kg / ha. Should contain, Nitrogen 20, Phosphorus 20, Potash 20, Sulfur 20, Zinc 20, At the time of sowing the full amount of Nitrogen, Phosphorous and Potash fertilizers is 5-10 cm. Give as a base manure in deep litter.

Harmful pests and diseases and their prevention Pest Control :-
In the crop of moong, there is mainly outbreak of pod beetle, green hopper, mahu and blanket pest. For the control of leaf eater insects, 1.5 liters of Quinalphos or 750 ml of Monocrotophos. and dimethoate 1000 ml for nectar insect pests like green hopper, mahu and white fly. per 600 liters of water or imidacloprid 17.8 sl. 125 ml per 600 liters of water. Spraying per hectare according to the drug is beneficial.
Disease Control :-
Most of the yellow diseases, foliage and brown diseases come mainly in moong. For the prevention of these diseases, disease resistant varieties Hum-1, Pant Moong-1, Pant Moong-2, TJM-3, JM-721 etc. should be used. Yellow disease is spread by white fly, for its control, use Metasystox 25 EC 750 to 1000 ml. 2 times per hectare by dissolving in 600 liters of water.Do it at day intervals. For the control of fungal diseases (Alternaria/Cercospora/Myrotheceus) prepare dithane M. 45, 2.5 g/L or mixed medicine of Carbendazim, Diethane M. 45 and dissolve it in 2.0 g/L of water in open season except rainy days. Spray in Repeat spraying as needed after 12-15 days.

Major diseases and control of Moong :-
Yellow Pit (Mosaic) Disease-
Disease resistant or tolerant varieties like TJM-3, K-851, Pant Moong-2, Pusa Vishal, HUM. Select -1. Use certified and healthy seeds. Sow the seeds in rows by the first week of July, uproot and destroy the diseased plants in the initial stage. This disease is caused by virus whose carrier is whitefly insect.To control which triazophos 40 EC, 2 ml per liter or thiomethoxam 25 WG. 2 g/L or Dimethate 30 EC, 1 ml/L. Make a solution in water and spray it 2 or 3 times at an interval of 10 days as needed.
Cercospora Foliage Stain
Use disease free healthy seeds. Plants should not be dense in the field. 10 cm of plants. Sparse by distance. Mancozeb 75 W when symptoms of the disease appear. 2.5 g liter of P. or Carbendazim 50 w. 1 g/L of P. Make a solution of the drug and spray it 2-3 times.
Antracnose -
Select certified and healthy seeds. Fungicides such as Mancozeb 75 W. p. 2.5 g/l. or 1 g/L of Carbendazim 50 WD. Spraying should be done after 40 and 55 days of sowing.
Thawing charcoal
Seed treatment Carbendazim 50 WG. Apply at the rate of 1 gram per kg of seed. Adopt crop rotation of 2-3 years and include jowar, bajra crops in the crop cycle.
Bhabhutia (powdery mildew) disease
Select disease resistant varieties. Sow on time. Carathan or Sulfur powder 2.5 gm/Lit when symptoms of the disease are observed. Spray with water.

Weed control :- Due to not doing sleep control in moong crop at the right time, there can be a reduction of up to 40-60 percent in the crop yield. Crops in Kharif season include narrow-leaf weeds such as: Sava (Ecainacloclova kolacanum/Ku sageli), Doob grass (Cyanodon dactylon) and broad-leafed stone mat (Tryanthima monogyna), Kanakwa (Comelina vegalensis), Mahkua (Azieratum konijwadis), White rooster (Silosia argensia), Hazardana (Phyllanthus niruri) and Weeds of Lasua (Digera arsis) and Motha (Cyprus rotundus, Cyprus aria) etc., are found in abundance. The critical stage of competition between crops and weeds lasts for the first 30 to 35 days in moong. Therefore, first weeding should be done on 15-20 days and second weeding should be done on 35-40 days. Weed killer Pendimethylene 700 g/ha can be sprayed up to 0-3 days after sowing, Quzalofap 40-50 gm can be sprayed 15-20 days after sowing.

Irrigation and Drainage :- Generally, the moong crop does not require irrigation during the rainy season, yet in this season, a light irrigation is necessary during the formation of legumes when there is a long gap between one rain and the next rainfall or due to lack of moisture. In spring and summer, irrigation is required at an interval of 10-15 days.
Irrigation should be stopped 15 days before the harvest. During the rainy season, if there is excessive rainfall or when there is water logging in the field, the surplus water should be removed from the field so that air circulation remains in the soil.

Harvesting :- When the moong pods start turning black and dry up, then the crop should be harvested. There is a fear of cracking the pods if it dries too much. The seeds are removed from the pods either by a thresher or by a pole.

After harvesting and threshing, after drying the grains thoroughly in the sun, when the moisture content in it is 8 to 10 percent, then it remains fit for storage. Use a storage cotton sack and keep it in a moisture-free place.
