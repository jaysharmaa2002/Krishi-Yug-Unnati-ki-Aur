Jujube

Jujube- Crop Name: Jujube (Plum) (Zizyphus mauritiana (Zizyphus mauritiana)) The plum fruit belongs to the genus Ziziphus. The fruit probably originated in southern China, India and Malaya. There are about 50 species under this genus, of which about 18-20 are found in India.Some species are useful from the commercial point of view. Ziziphus mauritiana is used commercially for horticulture in our country and Ziziphus jujube in China.

Jujube fruit, also known as red or Chinese date, is native to Southern Asia but has become popular around the world. These small round fruits with a seed-containing pit grow on large flowering shrubs or trees (Ziziphus jujuba). When ripe, they're dark red or purple and may appear slightly wrinkled. Due to their sweet taste and chewy texture, they're often dried and used in candies and desserts in parts of Asia where they commonly grow. In alternative medicine, they're widely utilized to improve sleep and decrease anxiety. This article tells you everything you need to know about jujube fruit, including its nutrition, benefits, and uses.

In addition, jujube fruits contain carbs in the form of natural sugars, which provide your body energy. However, dried jujubes, which are more commonly eaten and used in cooking in many parts of the world, are much higher in sugar and calories than the fresh fruit. During drying, the sugars in the fruit become concentrated, and additional sugar may be added during processing.


Time of sowing The best line for sowing seeds is in summer in tropical and subtropical climates. Transplanting is done in February-March or August-September. Define the plant while growing from nursery for the purpose of transplanting.

Sowing method Seeds of suitable root stock should be sown at a depth of 2 cm per sack. Vacancy: Rainfed - 6 x 6 m, Irrigated - 8 x 8 m, Pits of size 60 x 60 x 60 cm

Seed Treatment Soaking the seed in water for 24 hours increases the rate of germination.

Favorable Climate The favorable temperature is 15-40 C.
Rainfall requirement is 300-400 mm.
It grows from MSL to an altitude of 1000 m.

Earth Plum cultivation can be done easily in almost all types of soils. But for good yield, loamy loam soil in which the amount of organic matter is high is considered best for this. She goes.

Farm Preparation For berry cultivation, in the beginning, remove the remains of old crops present in the field and do deep plowing of the field. After that do two to three good slant plowing in the field through cultivator. After plowing, level the field by putting a pad.

Fertilizers and Manures
rain based
Fertilizer (gram) - 100: 50: 50 (n2: p2o5: k2o)
Manure (Kg) - 30 FYM
irrigated -
Fertilizer (gram) - 500: 400: 400 (n2: P2o5: k20)
Manure (Kg) - 60 FYM

Weed control Weeds in the field can be controlled by hand weeding or by chemicals, after weeding, use glyphosate 4 liters or 2 liters per hectare by mixing 500 to 600 liters of water. As far as possible, use weedicide before flowering. Weedicide should not be used on main plants.

Irrigation Established trees usually do not require frequent irrigation. When the plant is in a dormant state, irrigation is not required. Irrigation is essential during fruit development stage. At this stage, depending on the weather condition, apply irrigation at an interval of 3-4 weeks. Stop irrigation in the second fortnight of March.

Harvest Period It is an early maturing variety in mid-November. Flowering and fruiting probably depend on the length of summer and the time when the monsoon sets in. The duration of flowering and fruit maturity varies from region to region. In northern India, growth begins from late June to early July with the arrival of the monsoon and matures during February to April. Flower buds are mostly produced on the current growth. Flowers start from the first week of September to mid-November.

Harvest Time The first harvesting is done within 2-3 years after planting. Harvesting should be done at the right stage of maturity. Avoid high ripening stage, as this reduces the quality and taste of the fruit. Harvesting is done when they are of normal size and give ripe color depending on the variety.

Yield Rate Gives an average yield of 121 kg per tree.

Cleaning and Sorting After harvesting the plum fruits, grading should be done. Large size A-grade best fruit, B-grade medium sized good colored fruit, C-grade small sized good colored fruit and D-grade all sized poorly colored fruit and exclude outer part.
