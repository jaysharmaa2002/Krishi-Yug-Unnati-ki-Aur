Arhar

Arhar-India ranks first in the area and production of pulse crops in the world. Arhar is the main crop of Kharif pulses. It is an important crop and a source of protein. This crop is grown in tropical and sub-tropical regions. In India, Arhar is known as Arhar, Tur, Red Gram, and Pigeon Pea. Scientists consider India and South Africa to be the birth place of tur.It is a matter of great pride for us Indians. Arhar, famous for its main source of protein in every household, is the plant of our country. Maharashtra, Uttar Pradesh, Gujarat, Madhya Pradesh, Karnataka and Andhra Pradesh are the major tur producing states of the country. The cultivation of the pigeon pea dates back to at least 3,500 years ago. The centre of origin is probably peninsular India, where the closest wild relatives (Cajanus cajanifolia) occur in tropical deciduous woodlands.Archaeological finds of pigeon pea dating to about 3,400 years ago (14th century BC) have been found at Neolithic sites in Kalaburagi, Karnataka (Sanganakallu)

Time of sowing :-
Sowing of Arhar should be done with the onset of rains. Generally sow from last week of June to first week of July. 50 cm for sowing. in rows and 25 cm. Keep the distance between the plants.

Seed Quantity:- Seed quantity of 6 to 8 kg per acre is suitable for early varieties and 8 -10 kg for late varieties also.

Seed Treatment :- Before sowing, treat with fungicide @ 2 gm Thiram or 1 gm Carbendazim or Vitavex @ 2 gm/kg of seed. In biological treatment, treat Trichoderma at 4 g / kg seed and Rhizobium culture at 10 g per kg seed and then sow.

Earth :- For good production of tur, sandy loam or loamy soil containing biomass is good. Proper drainage and mild sloping fields are best for tur. This crop grows well up to 6.5-7.5 pH. Saline and alkaline lands are not conducive for the cultivation of tur, tur cultivation can be done successfully in black soil also.

Land preparation: For the cultivation of tur, before sowing, the field should be plowed well 2-3 times with a native plow or cultivator, so that the soil becomes friable, then after this, prepare the field for sowing by running the pad.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers:- While preparing the field before sowing in the cultivation of tur, well decomposed cow dung at the rate of 15-20 ton / acre should be mixed well in the soil so that the production increases. In chemical fertilizers apply Nitrogen 6 kg (13 kg Urea), 16 kg Phosphorus (100 kg Single Super Phosphate) and Potash 12 kg (20 kg MOP) per acre. Keep in mind that chemical fertilizers should be used only on the basis of soil test.

Harmful Pests:- Fly and pod bug - for the control of these pests, use Dimethoate 30 EC. or 1000 ml of propanophos-50. Dissolve the quantity in 500 liters of water and sprinkle.
Indoxicarb 14.5 EC for the control of borer worms and plume moth and blister beetles - these pests. 500 ml Or Quinalphas 25 BC. 1000 ml or Acephate 75 wp. Spray 500 grams per hectare by dissolving it in 500 liters of water.
Harmful diseases: Leaf spots - Light and dark brown spots appear on the leaves. In severe disease, it attacks the petiole and stem. To prevent this, the seed should be disease free and treat the seed with Thiram 3 gm or Mancozeb 2 gm/kg.
Ukta disease - It is spread by a fungus called Fusarium. Symptoms of the disease usually appear at the flowering stage of the crop. This disease can be seen between the months of September to January. The plant turns yellow and dries up. In this, the roots become dark in color and after removing the bark, black stripes are found from the root to the height of the stem.
For the prevention of this disease, it is necessary to do good deep plowing of the field during summer. Due to deep plowing in summer and intercropping of sorghum with tur, the infection of this disease is less. And sow after treating the seeds.

Infertility virus disease- This disease occurs with the iriophyd insect. Flowering does not form due to its attack and leaves become light in color, to prevent this, apply Phenazacin 10% EC 300 ml. 200 lt per acre Mix it with water and sprinkle.

Phytophthora blight disease- The diseased plant turns yellow and dries up. In this, unlimited growth is seen on the stem on the top of the ground and the plant breaks from here when the wind blows etc. For its prevention, treat 3 grams of Metalaxyl fungicide at the rate of 3 grams per kg of seed.

Weed control :- For control of Arhar weeds, do first weeding in 20-25 days and second weeding before flowering. Weed killer Pendimethalin 2 L. per acre. 150-200 liters Put in water after 2 days of sowing. After the use of weedicide, it is beneficial to do a sleep at the stage of about 30 to 40 days. Quijalophap 5 EC is used for the control of narrow leaf or grass total weeds. Use Tergasuper 800-1000 ml 15-20 days after sowing.

Irrigation :- Where irrigation facilities are available, one irrigation at flowering stage and the other at the stage of pod formation gives good yield.

Harvesting :- When 80 percent of the beans are ripe, the crop can be harvested by 10 cm with a sieve or with a sieve. should be done at a height of After that, to dry the crop, make bundles and bring the crop to the barn. Then after drying for four to five days, pullman separates the grains from the straw by thresher or by beating on a wooden log.

Storage :- The moisture percentage for storage should not exceed 10-11 percent. Use 2 tablets per ton of aluminum phosphide for protection from insects in storage.
