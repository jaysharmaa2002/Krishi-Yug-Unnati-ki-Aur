Barley

Barley- Barley is an important rabi crop of India, mainly sown in North India, which requires less water than wheat. It is used for making feed, animal feed, fodder and many industrial uses (products like wine, bakery, paper, fiber paper, fiber board).Barley is mainly cultivated in the country in Uttar Pradesh, Punjab, Haryana, Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, Bihar, Himachal Pradesh, Gujarat and Jammu and Kashmir.

Barley is a cereal grain. People often eat the grain as food. Some people also use the grain to make medicine. Barley is most commonly used for heart disease and high cholesterol.In manufacturing, barley is used as a food grain, natural sweetener, and as an ingredient for brewing beer and making alcoholic beverages.


Sowing time Sowing time from 1st November to 25th November

Seed quantity 35 to 45 kg per acre.

Seed Treatment For seed treatment, treat seed with Betawax or Carbendazim at 2 gm/kg of seed, or use Thiram and Betavax or Carbendazim in the ratio of 1:1 for 2:5 gm/kg of seed. Chlorpyriphos 20 E.C. to prevent termites. of 4.5 ml.

Sowing Method Sowing of barley is done by tractor driven seed drill or pelva.

Distance Row to row distance for sowing is 22.5 cm. Should be. If sowing is done late, then 18-20 cm. Keep the distance

Seed depth The depth in irrigated areas is 3-5 cm. Keep and keep 5-8 cm in rainfed areas. Keep it

Land Selection Barley crop can be sown in different types of soil, but medium, loamy soil is more suitable. The field should be flat and well drained.

Fertilizers and chemical Fertilizers While preparing the field before sowing for the cultivation of barley, compost or organic manure or well decomposed cow dung should be mixed well in the soil at the rate of 5-10 tonnes/acre. In chemical fertilizers, ammonium sulphate and sodium nitrate should be given one month before sowing and at first irrigation. In irrigated land, use of fertilizer should be done. Keep in mind that the use of chemical fertilizers should be given only on the basis of soil test.

Harmful pests and diseases and their prevention Rust and scorch - Both these diseases spread very fast in favorable weather, use of resistant species should be given priority to prevent these diseases.
1. Mahu Pest - For its control, apply Rozor and 2 ml. Spray / liter or imidachloroprid 20 gm active ingredients per hectare mixed with 1000 liters of water, if the infestation of the kit is very high, then spray again after 15 days.
2. Termite kit - For the control of termites, treat 4.5 ml of Chlorpyriphos drug per kg of seed. To prevent termites in standing crop, mix 3 liters of Chloropyriphos drug in 20 kg of sand or sand by irrigating the affected field. Scatter before.

Weed control Early weed control is very important for a good crop and good production. Broad and narrow leaf weeds are serious pests of this crop. To control broad-leafed weeds, apply 2,4-D@250gm/100Ltr of water after 30-35 days of sowing after the germination of weeds.
To control weeds like narrow leaf, spray Isoprotiuron 75%WP@500gm per 100Ltr of water or Pendimethalin 30%EC@1.4Ltr per 100Ltr of water per acre within 2 days after sowing.

Irrigation For good yield of barley crop, 3 to 4 irrigations are required. Lack of water affects the yield at the time of earing. Apply first water after 20-25 days at the time of sowing. When the earrings come, apply another water.

Harvest time Harvesting is done in March-April.

Cleaning and Drying The ripe crop should be harvested in March-April, when the stalks of the plants are completely dry and break easily on bending. Then tie them in bundles and bury them soon, because these days there is more fear of storm and rain.

Storage After harvesting the crop, the grain should be separated from the straw by thresher by drying it thoroughly and after drying it thoroughly and cleaning it in sacks and stored in a safe place. A moisture-free place should be selected for storage.

Production Capability 15 to 20 quintals per bigha.
