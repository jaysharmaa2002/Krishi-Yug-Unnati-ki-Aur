Giloy

Giloy- Giloy is a divine medicine, which has been used by millions of people to get rid of many diseases. Giloy is almost like a betel leaf in appearance. It grows and grows in the form of a creeper. The leaves of Giloy are thin, green and heart shaped. There is a story behind Giloy which is as follows: When the ocean was churned, many things came out of it, out of which nectar was very valuable which the gods got.

But the demons started running away after getting the nectar by deceit. Wherever the drops of nectar fell on the earth in the course of escape, there Giloy appeared in the form of a plant, hence it is also called Amrit Valli.

Giloy might be one to consider. It's a climbing shrub and an essential herb in Ayurvedic medicine. People take it to support general wellness and treat a wide range of conditions, including fever, infections, and diabetes.

Giloy (Tinospora cordifolia) is a climbing shrub that grows on other trees, from the botanical family Menispermaceae. The plant is native to India but also found in China and tropical areas of Australia and Africa.

It's considered an essential herbal plant in Ayurvedic and folk medicine, where people use it as a treatment for a wide range of health conditions . All parts of the plant are used in Ayurvedic medicine. However, the stem is thought to have the most beneficial compounds. The Ayurvedic Pharmacopoeia of India has approved the plant's stem for use in medicine.


Crop Varieties: Amritabalali VII, Giloysattva, Satgilo, Senthil Kodi.

Time of sowing The 6-8-inch cuttings are planted upright with two knots. In June-July, the stems obtained from the main plant are directly planted in the field within 24 hours.

Sowing Stem cuttings are the best propagation material of this plant and are mainly done in the month of June-July. Seeds are also used for propagation of this plant, before sowing the seeds are soaked in water for 24 hours for germination. Hydroprimed seeds are sown in polybags during May-July.

Sowing method For the cultivation of Giloy, we should take the support of a fence or a large plant in the field or the creeper should be easy to walk in the field. 15- 20 cm from good vigorous and fast growing plants. Use pieces of branches slightly thicker than a finger with 4-5 eyes of length, these pieces should be planted in the month of May-June and prepare for nursery.When planting a pen in it, a raised bed or a polythene bag should be used, the lower part of the pen should be immersed in the solution of Rutex powder for 15-20 minutes, it is necessary to keep the nursery in the shade, apart from this, except one day in the nursery. The day should be irrigated after 30-45 days, the plants become transferable, while cultivating in cultivable land, two plants and 120-150 cm in row. should keep the difference this crop does not need to be given additional manure, but after 20-25 days of transfer, giving 15-20 grams of nitrogen per plant increases the growth of the plant.

Seed treatment Giloy is a climber that you can find on large trees like Mango, Neem etc. You have to cut a stem straight down and put it in the soil near a tree (which it can climb) with some water as it grows from its stem part and its stem is the most beneficial part.

Favorable climate This plant is very hardy and can be grown in almost all climates, but it prefers warm climates.

Soil Giloy can be grown in any type of land, the wetness of the soil is more beneficial for this crop, the humid or moist climate is beneficial for this creeper.

Farm preparation The land should be well plowed, and weed free.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers Plants should be grown without the use of chemical fertilizers and pesticides. Organic manures like farm yard manure (FYM), vermicompost, green manure can be used as per the requirement of the species.

Weed control About two to three hoeing is required for good growth. The distance between the plants in the rows should be kept free from weeds by weeding and hoeing frequently.

Irrigation For this 50 ml to 100 ml of water is required. It is a type of climbing different types of big trees like mango, neem etc. from where one can cut the stem and put it in the soil near a tree along with some water.

Harvest period The nursery period of these plants is up to one and a half months.

Harvesting time Seeds take almost more than twice as long to mature and yield the same amount. The plant needs support to grow, which can be provided by raising wooden stakes or trellis.

Yield rate The yield rate of stem is about 0.8-1 t/ha.

Cleaning and Drying After cooking Giloy leaves, they should be washed with water and if you want to make powder of Giloy then you can dry it and take out this powder. Over and over again it can also be used for many purposes.
