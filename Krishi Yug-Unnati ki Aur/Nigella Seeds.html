Nigella Seeds

Nigella Seeds- Kalonji (Nigella sativa) also called "Black Cumin" Kalonji, small black Kalonji seeds are on the bushes. Which is widely grown in Central India. Kalonji is a shrub plant of Ranunculaceae family, whose botanical name is "Nigella sativa", Kalonji can be grown successfully in different parts of the country. It is mainly grown for its seeds, which are used as a spice in pickles, the seeds and the oil prepared from them are used in Ayurvedic medicines and also in the perfumery industry.

Also known as black cumin, nigella or by its scientific name Nigella sativa, kalonji belongs to the buttercup family of flowering plants. It grows up to 12 inches (30 cm) tall and produces a fruit with seeds that are used as a flavorful spice in many cuisines. In addition to its culinary use, kalonji is known for its medicinal properties. In fact, its use can be traced back several centuries as a natural remedy for everything from bronchitis to diarrhea.

Nigella sativa (black caraway, also known as black cumin, nigella or kalonji) is an annual flowering plant in the family Ranunculaceae, native to eastern Europe (Bulgaria, Cyprus and Romania) and western Asia (Turkey, Iran and Iraq), but naturalized over a much wider area, including parts of Europe, northern Africa and east to Myanmar.

N. sativa grows to 20-30 cm (7.9-11.8 in) tall, with finely divided, linear (but not thread-like) leaves. The flowers are delicate, and usually coloured pale blue and white, with five to ten petals. The fruit is a large and inflated capsule composed of three to seven united follicles, each containing numerous seeds which are used as spice, sometimes as a replacement for black cumin (Bunium bulbocastanum).


Improved variety AZAD KALONJI: This is an important variety of 1, N.R.C.S.S.A.N.1. Apart from this N.S 44- is also a popular variety of Kalonji. It becomes ready for harvesting in about 150-160 days. The production is around 4.5-5.5 t/ha.

Time of sowing It is advisable to sow Kalonji in the month of October.

Seed quantity Seed quantity 7-8 kg per hectare.

Sowing method Sowing of fennel seeds is done by the sprinkler method or in lines, but sowing in lines is more suitable. If the moisture content in the field is less at the time of sowing, then light irrigation can be done after sowing.

Distance Sow by keeping a distance of 30 cm between Kalonji plants. After 15 to 20 days after germination, make the distance of the plants 15 cm.

Depth Sow the seed no deeper than 1.5 cm. Otherwise the seed germination will be adversely affected.

Seed treatment Seed should be treated with Kaptan or Thiram or Mancozeb fungicide at the rate of 2.5 g/kg seed to prevent seed borne disease root rot.

Climate Kalonji is a cool climate crop. It is mainly grown in Rabi in the winter season in northern India, it needs a mild cool climate at the time of sowing and growing and a mild warm climate at the time of ripening.

Earth Kalonji can be grown in all types of soils with good drainage of bacteria. Loamy and sandy land is more suitable for the production of Kalonji crop. This crop can be grown even in heavy land with proper drainage management.

Farm Preparation For abundant production it is necessary to prepare the land well. For this, first plowing should be done with soil turning plow and after 2-3 plowing with cultivator, the field should be made fine. After this, level the field by making the soil finely by applying a pat. It is necessary to have proper moisture in the field before sowing for good germination. Therefore, it is good to sow the field after getting it washed.

Fertilizers and chemical Fertilizers The amount of manure and fertilizer should be given only after getting the soil tested. For good yield of Kalonji, 10 tonnes of cow dung or compost should be applied, in addition to this 40:20:15 NPK is used as an element per hectare for land with normal fertility. The amount of nitrogen should be applied in two equal parts with irrigation in standing crop at 30 and 60 days interval from sowing.

Crop protection: The main pests and diseases are as follows-- Katwa Worm:- This caterpillar damages the plant by cutting it near the ground. For its prevention, spray Chlorpyrifos 20 EC by mixing it with 2.5 ml/Lit of water. Root rot: - The main problem of Kalonji is to use disease free seeds, treat the seeds and sow them.

Weed control Two or three weedings are required to take Kalonji crop and keep the field free from weeds. Weeding should be done at an interval of about every 30 days. At the time of first weeding, remove the extra plants.

Irrigation Irrigation should be done as per the requirement of the crop.

Harvesting Kalonji crop is ready to ripen in about 120-140 days, the crop ready for harvesting is uprooted from the whole plant and kept in the barn to dry for 5-6 days. After drying, the seeds should be separated by beating them with a stick.

Yield An average yield of 8-10 quintals can be obtained from one hectare.
