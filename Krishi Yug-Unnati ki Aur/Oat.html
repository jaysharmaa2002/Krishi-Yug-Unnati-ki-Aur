Oat

Oat- Complete information about improved cultivation of oats: It is an important cereal crop as well as a fodder crop. The cultivation of oats is similar to that of wheat. It is mainly grown in temperate and subtropical climates. It can also grow well in high altitude tropics. They are very popular due to their health benefits. Oatmeal is a very famous food.Oats are rich in protein and fiber. They also help in weight loss, controlling blood pressure and building a strong immune system.

Oats (Avena sativa) are a cereal commonly eaten in the form of oatmeal or rolled oats. ... They are mainly eaten as porridge, as an ingredient in breakfast cereals, and in baked goods (oatcakes, oat cookies, and oat bread). Over the past few decades, oats have become a very popular "health food."


Geographical Distribution: Oat area and production in the world are 27 m ha and 40 m tonnes respectively.
The countries that cultivate oats are Russian Federation, USA, Canada, Poland, China, France and Australia.
In India, limited areas in Punjab, Haryana, UP and MP, Orissa, Bihar, West Bengal are oat growing states.

Varieties of oats Kent, Algerian, Bunker 10, Coachman, HFO 114, UPO 50

Time of sowing The best time for sowing oats is mid-October to mid-November. October 15 is the optimum time for fodder production.

Distance Row Spacing of 20-23 cm is optimal for folder adn 23-25 cm for grain production.

Seed depth Seed depth should be 3-4 cm.

Sowing method For sowing drill sowing is better than broadcast.

Seed quantity The seed recommendation is 100 kg/ha.

Seed treatment Before sowing, treat the seeds with Kaptan or Thiram@3gm/kg of seed. This protects the seeds from fungal diseases and bacterial viruses.

Crop System:
*sorghum-oat-maize
*maize-oats-maize

Land Selection A wide range of soils with good water holding capacity are more suitable for the cultivation of oats. Soil having 5-6.6 pH is good for the cultivation of oats.

Climate Grows best in cool and moist climates.
Also, cotton is best suited for belts.
Cool weather is important during grain filling for high yield.

Land Preparation Selected well drained loamy soil with good irrigation potential for growing oats. Waterlogged areas should be avoided. The land should be well plowed three to four times and leveled properly to make the soil well friable and level. Seeds should be sown when there is sufficient moisture in the soil. If the soil is dry, pre-sowing light irrigation should be given.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers At the time of field preparation, apply well decomposed cow dung manure. 80:40:0 kg NPK/ha is the recommended dosage of fertilizers. Apply half dose of Nitrogen and full dose of Phosphorus at the time of sowing. Remaining nitrogen should be applied after 30-40 days of sowing.

Weed control Weeding is done to control weeds. Weed control is not needed if the oat plants are properly erected. Weeds are found less in oats crop.

Irrigation It requires more water than wheat. 4-5 irrigations provide good yield. Generally, irrigation is mandatory immediately after each cutting. Tilling is the crucial stage for irrigation of oats.

Harvesting It takes 120-150 days to mature. Common practice is 2 or 3 cuttings for the forage and then allowing the grain.

Production The yield of green fodder is 200-250 quintals and grain is 15-20 quintals per acre.
