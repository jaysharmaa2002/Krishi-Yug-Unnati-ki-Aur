Plum

Plum- Plum is an ancient popular fruit of India and China. Ber, also known as the fruit of the poor, is commonly cultivated in arid areas. It is rich in Vitamin 'C' and 'A'. Apart from vitamins, minerals like calcium, phosphorus and iron are also found in abundance in jube.It is mainly cultivated in the states of Madhya Pradesh, Bihar, Uttar Pradesh, Punjab, Haryana, Rajasthan, Gujarat, Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu and Andhra Pradesh. Plum is a versatile and nutritious fruit for us.

A plum is a fruit of some species in Prunus subg. Prunus. Mature plum fruits may have a dusty-white waxy coating that gives them a glaucous appearance. This is an epicuticular wax coating and is known as "wax bloom". Dried plums are called prunes, which have a dark, wrinkled appearance.


Famous Varieties Kaithali, Umran, Gola, Banarasi and Goma are the cultivars of Keerthi ber.

Time of sowing Destroying these seedlings at ground level in the month of May helps in giving new shoots by July. Patching these seedlings in July helps to replant it to the variety of choice. In northern India, planting is done in February-March or July-September at an interval of 7-8 months.

Sowing Plum plants for planting are usually raised from nursery with complete root system with adequate soil. Plum plants can also be planted bare rooted during December-January. For this the entire root system is carefully dug out of 'water' soil.

Distance Keep a distance of 7.5 x 7.5 meters for planting saplings.

Seed Depth 60x60x60cm before planting. Dig the pit and leave it open in the sun for 15 days. After this fill these pits with soil and cow dung, after that plant the plants in these pits.

Seed Treatment From the present study of seed germination in ber, it is concluded that ber seeds can be sown after one year of storage for improved germination and quick availability of good quality grafted plants within one year of seed sowing. can.

Cutting and Pruning Thorough cutting and pruning of the plant is necessary every year. It starts at nursery time. Make sure the nursery has a single stem plant. At the time of planting in the field, the upper end of the plant should be clean and 30-45 cm. Long 4-5 strong twigs. Cut the twigs of the plant so that the twigs do not spread on the ground. Cut off dry, broken and diseased branches of the plant. Prune the plant in the second fortnight of May when the plant is not growing.

Earth Plum cultivation can be done easily in almost all types of soils. can. But for good yield, loamy loam soil in which the amount of organic matter is high is considered best for this.

Farm Preparation For berry cultivation, in the beginning, remove the remains of old crops present in the field and do deep plowing of the field. After that do two to three good slant plowing in the field through cultivator. After plowing, level the field by putting a pad.

Climate The plum grows in varying climatic conditions at an altitude of up to 1,000 m. Above m.s.l. It can withstand extremely hot conditions but is susceptible to frost. High atmospheric humidity is not suitable for its cultivation.

Fertilizers and Manures Application of 10 kg of FYM and 15 kg of ammonium sulphate per 4-5 year old tree is recommended. A full grown tree should give 20-30 kg FYM and half kg nitrogen to get good yield.

Weed Control Weeds in the field can be controlled by hand weeding or by chemicals, after weeding, use glyphosate 4 liters or 2 liters per hectare by mixing 500 to 600 liters of water. As far as possible, use weedicide before flowering. Weedicide should not be used on main plants.

Irrigation Irrigation in ber fruit cultivation:- Generally, ber trees are cultivated under rainfed conditions. However, in case of irrigated crop, watering should be done based on soil moisture status and weather conditions. In hot long dry season, irrigate the field twice a month.

Harvesting time The first harvesting of fruits should be done when the plant is 2 to 3 years old. Harvesting of fruits should be done only after ripening. The fruit should not be allowed to over ripen, as it affects the quality and taste of the fruit. Harvesting should be done only after the change in color according to the size and variety of the fruit.

After Harvest The spoiled and unripe fruits should be separated, along with the fruits should be separated according to their size. After sorting the fruits should be packed in appropriate size cardboard boxes, wooden baskets or jute sacks.

Yield Rate The fruits of jujube mature about 150-175 days after flowering. In irrigated farming, about 75 to 100 kg from one berry plant. But in rain-fed farming, 30 to 60 kg fruits are available per plant.
