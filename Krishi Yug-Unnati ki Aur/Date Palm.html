Date Palm

Date Palm- The date palm is the oldest tree to grow on earth. They are an excellent source of calcium, sugar, iron and potassium. They are used in many social and religious festivals. Apart from this, they have many health benefits, such as reducing constipation, heart disease, controlling diarrhea and helping in pregnancy. It is also used to make various products such as chutneys, pickles, jams, juices and other bakery items.

Phoenix dactylifera, commonly known as date or date palm, is a flowering plant species in the palm family, Arecaceae, cultivated for its edible sweet fruit. The species is widely cultivated across Northern Africa, the Middle East, and South Asia, and is naturalized in many tropical and subtropical regions worldwide. P. dactylifera is the type species of genus Phoenix, which contains 12-19 species of wild date palms, and is the major source of commercial production.

Dates have been cultivated in the Middle East and the Indus Valley for thousands of years. There is archaeological evidence of date cultivation in Arabia from the 6th millennium BCE. The total annual world production of dates amounts to 8.5 million metric tons, countries of the Middle East and North Africa being the largest producers.

Date trees typically reach about 21-23 metres (69-75 ft) in height,[7] growing singly or forming a clump with several stems from a single root system. Date fruits (dates) are oval-cylindrical, 3 to 7 centimetres (1 to 3 in) long, and about 2.5 centimetres (1 in) in diameter, with colour ranging from bright red to bright yellow, depending on variety. Containing 61-68 percent sugar by mass when dried,dates are very sweet and are enjoyed as desserts on their own or within confections.

Time of sowing Sowing is done in the month of February to March and in the month of August to September.

Distance For transplanting, dig pits of size 1 m x 1 m x 1 m at a distance of 6 m or 8 m.

Seed depth Dig pits of size 1 m x 1 m x 1 m for transplanting.

Sowing method The vegetative part is planted in the main field.

Seed quantity When row and plant is used for planting 6 meters apart, there are about 112 shoots in one acre. 8 meters to 8 meters gives 63 shoots per acre.

Seed treatment To encourage rooting, before transplanting into the pit, dip the sucker base in IBA @ 1000ppm and Chlorpyrifos @ 5ml per liter of water for two to five minutes.

Climate Date palms have very specific conditions for their specific cultivation i.e. long dry summers, moderate winters and almost free rainfall during fruit ripening (July-August).

Earth Dates can be cultivated in any type of soil whose pH should be between pH8 to pH10, the soil should have the ability to absorb moisture. Dates can also be grown in saline and alkaline soils.

Farm Preparation For date palm cultivation, make the field level and fine by plowing the field 2-3 times. After the soil is leveled, dig pits of size 1 m x 1 m x 1 m in summer. Keep these pits open for two weeks. After that fill the pits with well decomposed cow dung and fertile soil. Chlorpyrifos 50ml Or put phorate 10 g, 200 g and captain 20-25 g each in the pit.

Fertilizers and Chemical Frtilizers In the month of September to October, manure @ 10-15 kg for young plants and 30-40 kg per tree for mature plants is required. Urea @ 4.4 kg is applied on mature tree to one year old plant. Application of urea is done in two equal divisions, first dose is given before flowering and remaining half dose is given after fruit set in April.

Weed Control To control weeds in the field, do weeding and keep the field clean.

Irrigation In summers, irrigation is given at an interval of 10-15 days and in winter at an interval of 30-40 days. Pre-irrigation is necessary after the plants are covered. Irrigation is given at regular intervals after fruit set.

Harvest Period It may take 4 to 8 years before a date palm plant begins to bear fruit, and between 7 and 10 years will begin to produce viable yields for a commercial crop.

Harvest Time Four to five years after planting, the date palm is ready for the first harvest. The fruits are harvested in three stages, the khal or doka stage (fresh fruits), the soft or ripe stage (pind) and the dry stage (chauhara). Complete the harvesting of fruits before the onset of monsoon season.

Production Capability It may take 4 to 8 years before a date palm plant begins to bear fruit, and will begin to produce fruit and yields viable for a commercial crop between 7 and 10 years.

Cleaning and Drying After harvesting in the doka stage, wash the fruits with clean water. For the purpose of making Chura, they are dried in the sun or in a dryer at a temperature of 40-45 C for 80 to 120 hours.
