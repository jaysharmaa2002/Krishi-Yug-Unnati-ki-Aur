Rubber

Rubber- Rubber is a beautiful and evergreen plant. Rubber is also known as rubber plant and rubber tree. Rubber trees are found in equatorial evergreen forests, rubber is prepared from its milk, which is called latex. Rubber was first produced in the country in northern Travancore (Kerala) on the Periyar coast.Sir Henry William brought rubber seeds to India from Para (Brazil) in 1876. Rubber production in India is mainly done in southern India especially in Kerala, Karnataka and Tamil Nadu. Kerala is the largest rubber producing state in India. Apart from this, rubber is also cultivated in Assam and Tripura in the Northeast.

Rubber, also called India rubber, latex, Amazonian rubber, caucho, or caoutchouc, as initially produced, consists of polymers of the organic compound isoprene, with minor impurities of other organic compounds. Thailand and Indonesia are two of the leading rubber producers


Time of sowing June-July is the best time for planting rubber plants.

Distance In hilly areas - 6.7 X 3.4 m flat areas - in square - 4.9 m X 4.9 m Triangular - 4.9m X 4.9m

Sowing method Plantation is done by preparing nursery through seeds and new plants are prepared by adopting cutting method.

Favorable climate A mild mild climate is required for the cultivation of rubber plants. The rubber plant is basically a product of the Mediterranean climate. The temperature for the rubber plant is 25 to 30 and 150 to 200 C. m. Rain is required.

Land selection Rubber plants grow well in well drained, fertile loamy soils. Soils with an acidic pH of 4.5 to 6.0 are more suitable for this.

Farm preparation Before planting, the field should be well plowed and leveled. Keep the selected area weed free. And the pits should be excavated according to the proper distance.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers At the time of sowing seeds in nursery, vermicompost or cow dung should be given for good growth. And according to the soil test at the time of plantation, use manure and fertilizer only on the basis of the advice of agricultural experts.

Weed control To control weeds, weeding should be done around the plants from time to time.

Irrigation Every plant should be watered immediately after planting. Later, to make the soil moisture, water should be given from time to time as needed.

Harvesting and Production Rubber yields increase year after year, reaching a peak 14 years after planting. In South India, the annual yield of rubber is 375 kg/ha from planted trees, while the yield of seeded plants is 800 - 1000 kg/ha.
