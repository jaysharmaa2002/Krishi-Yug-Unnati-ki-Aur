Sesame

Sesame Sesame seed is an important oilseed crop. Apart from Kharif season, it is also planted in summer and semi-winter season. This crop can be done successfully in the summer season in Gujarat and especially in Saurashtra. Important things to keep in mind for summer sesame cultivation are as follows.
According to the summer season, this variety is very suitable and it takes about two and a half months to 3 months for this variety to become ripe, the state, Maharashtra, Gujarat and Rajasthan have very favorable time for sesame crop.Sesame seed is one of the oldest oilseed crops known, domesticated well over 3000 years ago. Sesamum has many other species, most being wild and native to sub-Saharan Africa. S. indicum, the cultivated type, originated in India.It tolerates drought conditions well, growing where other crops fail.Sesame has one of the highest oil contents of any seed


Improved varieties of sesame:- Major improved varieties of sesame, such as- T-4, T-12, T- 13, T- 78, Rajasthan sesame-346, Madhavi, Shekhar, Kaniki white, Pragati, Pratap, Gujarat sesame-3, Haryana sesame, Tarun, Gujarat Sesame - 4, Punjab Sesame - 1, Brajeshwari (TLK- 4) etc.

Seed Quantity:- Seed requirement is 4 to 5 kg per acre.

Seed Treatment :- Seed should be treated with Kaptan or Thiram fungicide at the rate of 2.5 g/kg of seed to prevent seed borne disease root rot. Or treat at the rate of 4 grams of Trichoderma viridi per kg of seed.

Time of sowing :- Sow sesame seeds at the right time, after the first monsoon rains, sow in the first week of July. Delay in sowing leads to decrease in crop production. If the temperature is 25 to 27 degree centigrade at the time of sowing, then it is good for germination. The best time for sowing is in the month of March for Zayed season.

Sowing Method:- The method of sowing has a direct effect on the yield of sesame. Sesame seeds should be sown in straight lines. Line to line distance is 30 to 45 cm and plant to plant distance is 10 to 15 cm. m. Should keep Sesame seed is small in size, so it should not be sown deeply.

Earth :- Matiyar sandy land is suitable for the cultivation of sesame. The pH value of the soil is 5.5 to 8.0, acidic or alkaline soils are not suitable for sesame.

Climate :- Sesame requires moderate temperature, the temperature of Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, and Rajasthan is best for this crop.

Field preparation: After harvesting the previous crop, the soil should be made friable by sifting it. Before the onset of monsoon, the field should be leveled by plowing, and the field should be prepared after one or two ploughings.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers:- Before sowing of sesame, while preparing the field, well decomposed cow dung should be mixed well in the soil of 10-15 tons / acre. And 60 kg in chemical fertilizer. Nitrogen, 46 kg. phosphorus, and 15 kg. Sulfur should be given per hectare. Full amount of Phosphorus and half of Nitrogen and Sulfur should be applied at the time of sowing. And the remaining half quantity of Nitrogen and Sulfur should be given in the form of spray in the standing crop when the crop is 30-35 days old.

Harmful pests and diseases and their prevention

Harmful Pests:-
Cheek fly - Monocrotophos 36 W for its prevention. P. or cunalphas 25 EC. Spraying should be done at the rate of one liter/ha.
Pod and leaf borer - Monocrotophos 36 W for its prevention. Spray 50 percent soluble powder of P. or Carboryl on the crop.

Harmful diseases:
Root and stem rot - For its prevention, seed should be sown after treatment before sowing. Sesame crop should not be taken continuously in the field affected by this disease.
Scorching and blight - For its prevention, spray Mancozeb or Zineb at the rate of 1.5 kg or Captain 2 to 2.5 kg per hectare. Repeat spraying after 15 days.
Bhabhutia disease - For its prevention, sprinkle powdered sulfur. and mancozeb 40 g or mycobutanil 10% w. Spray with P., 10 gm/15 ltr of water.

Weed control :- Keeping the sesame crop free from weeds for the first 45 days helps in the growth and development of the plants. Immediately after sowing sesame seeds, take spray of Alcolor 1.5 L/ha (60 ml in 10 l of water) on the ground. Do hand weeding and hoeing as required. If there is shortage of labor then after 3 days of sowing, spray Quillazofop ethyl at the rate of 0.05 kg per hectare in the standing crop.

Irrigation :- The crop in Kharif season is rain-fed. Therefore, according to the condition of rainfall, the level of moisture in the soil, the type of land and the demand of the crop, irrigation is required to maintain sufficient moisture in the land. The wilting of leaves, drying and non-flowering of flowers in the fields during early morning indicate the need for irrigation in the crop.The critical stages of irrigation for good yield are before sowing or after sowing, flowering stage and pod formation stage, so irrigation must be done at these three stages.

Harvesting:- Sesame crop, which will be ready in two and a half months. Often all the pods of the crop do not ripen together, but most of the pods should be harvested when the color turns brownish yellow. Bundles of the crop should be kept. On drying, the mouth of the pods crack open, then they are turned upside down and the grains are separated by beating them with sticks. After this, the seeds should be dried and stored after remaining 9 percent moisture.

Yield :- Its production is about 5 to 6 quintals per acre, which is very good and this variety which is ready to ripen in a very short time, in 3 months this crop can be brought back from the farmers' sow and harvested. could.
