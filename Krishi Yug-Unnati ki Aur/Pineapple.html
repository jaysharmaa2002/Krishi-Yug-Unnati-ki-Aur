Pineapple

Pineapple- Pineapple Pineapple (Ananas comosus) is one of the commercially important fruit crops of India. The total annual world production is estimated at 14.6 million tonnes of fruit. India is the fifth largest producer of pineapple with an annual production of about 1.2 million tonnes. Other major producers are Thailand, Philippines, Brazil, China, Nigeria, Mexico, Indonesia, Colombia and USA.
The area under pineapple cultivation in India has increased by 35% from 57 thousand hectares. 77 thousand hectares in 1991-92. Whereas in 2001-02 the production increased by 54% to 12 lakh tonnes from 8 lakh tonnes. The states where pineapple is grown include Assam, Meghalaya, Tripura, Manipur, West Bengal, Kerala, Karnataka and Goa. Other states where it is grown on a small scale are Gujarat, Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh, Orissa, Bihar and Uttar Pradesh.
Pineapple fruit is also a good source of vitamins A, B, C and calcium, magnesium, potassium and iron. It is also a good source of bromelain, a digestive enzyme. It is consumed fresh or in the form of juice, jam, squash and syrup. In all forms, canned slices and juices are in great demand in India, accounting for about 70% of the production.

Time of sowing The ideal planting time is 12-15 months before the peak flowering season under natural conditions, which varies from December to March in different regions. Planting time also varies from place to place depending on the time of onset of monsoon and the intensity of its rainfall. In Assam, planting should be done during August-October, while in Kerala and Karnataka; The best time for planting is April-June. Planting is generally avoided during periods of heavy rain.The ideal planting time is October-November in northern part of West Bengal and June-July for other parts. Delay planting until the end of September, delaying the harvest by at least 7-9 months. The peak flowering period is from January to March.

Farming Method Pineapple plant is prepared from the branches formed on its plants. Those are known as side puttal (sucker), gooty puttal (slip) and crown. Sucker is prepared by removing the leaves of the ground-ready part of the plant. And the slip is prepared from the branches coming out of the ground part. Whereas the crown is prepared from the branches formed on the fruit.The seedlings should be cleaned and treated before planting in the field. Yellow leaves should be plucked and removed before treating the plant. After that the seeds should be treated with Cerasen solution or Thiram after drying in the sun for some time and then sown in the fields. It is good to plant plants prepared from its sucker. Because the plants planted with sucker start giving fruits after about 15 months after transplanting. Whereas the plants planted with slip and crown take 20 to 24 months to bear fruit after transplanting.

Planting Method Four different systems are followed for pineapple planting. These four systems are flat-bed, furrow, contour and trench. The exact system of planting varies according to the soil and rainfall. Terraced or contour planting system is implemented in slopes, which helps in preventing soil erosion.

Seed quantity High density cultivation is recommended for commercial viability. A density of 63,400 plants per hectare is ideal for subtropical and mildly humid conditions. Whereas a density of 53,300 plants per hectare for hot and humid conditions, 25 cm from plant to plant within a row, 60 cm from row to row and 90 cm from trench to trench provides high yields.But in high fertile, rainfed and mountainous areas, a somewhat lower potting density of 31,000 plants per hectare is recommended.

Seed treatment Planting material is dipped in Ceresan solution (4 g in 1 liter of water) or 0.2% Dithane M-45 before planting to prevent bud rot in the plants.

Favorable climate Pineapple is suitable for cultivation in moist tropics. The fruit grows well on the beach as well as indoors; Unless the temperature is extreme. The optimum temperature required for successful cultivation is 22-32 C. Higher temperatures at night are detrimental to plant growth and a difference of at least 40 C between day and night temperatures is desirable. It can be grown up to a thousand meters. Above sea level, if the area is frost free. Rainfall requirement is between 100-150 cm.

Land selection A sandy loamy soil with a pH between 5.0-6.0 is ideal for plant growth. Pineapple cultivation is confined to the high rainfall and humid coastal areas in peninsular India and the mountainous regions of the north-eastern region of the country. It can also be grown commercially in interior plains with moderate rainfall and supplemental irrigation.

Farm preparation The land is prepared for planting by plowing or digging. Depending on the nature of the land, trenches of convenient length, approximately 90 cm. width and 15-30 cm. Depth is created.

Fertilizers and chemical Fertilizers Necessary fertilizer is needed for good yield of pineapple. At the time of first plowing in pineapple, 200 to 250 quintals of decomposed cow dung should be mixed in the soil. Apart from this, 16 grams of nitrogen and 2.5 grams of potash should be given per plant per year, ammonium sulphate fertilizer is best for nitrogen. Mixing fertilizers is beneficial for pineapple crop. Fertilizers should be sprinkled around the plants and mixed in the soil. The first dose of fertilizers should be given 90 days after transplanting.

Mulching and Weed control
Mulching is very helpful for controlling weeds and retaining moisture in the soil. You can use dry leaves or straw as mulching material.
Mulching with black polythene and saw dust has been found to be very effective.
Ripe fruits can be covered with rice straw or pineapple leaves to reduce both sunburn and bird damage.
Weeds are a lot of trouble when growing any plant. They consume nutrients and water from the soil, and eventually the plants suffer. So do weeding regularly. And completely remove all weeds from the soil before fertilizing your plants.

Irrigation Generally, pineapple is cultivated mostly under rainfed conditions. But supplemental irrigation or watering helps in producing good sized fruits in areas with optimum rainfall. Irrigation also helps establish an off-season planting to maintain production throughout the year. In case of low rainfall and hot weather, irrigation can be done once in 20-25 days.

Crop care
Suckers increase with the appearance of the inflorescence, while the shoots begin to grow along with the developing fruit.
Fruit weight increases with increasing number of suckers/plant whereas fruit ripening is delayed due to increase in number of slips. The shape of the crown has no bearing on the weight or quality of the fruit.
So while the unloading can be delayed as much as possible, it is recommended to remove the slips immediately after they have attained the required size for planting. The crown does not need to be removed as this fruit affects its appeal and becomes difficult to handle.
Partial pinching of the crown 45 days after fruit set involving the removal of the innermost whorl of leaves as well as the growing tips is ideal to obtain fruits of better shape and size.

Inter-crop: The common inter-cropping practice is to plant pineapples under coconuts or papayas. Inter-cropping benefits can be significantly increased by supplying the fertilizer requirements of all crops.
Harvest Pineapple fruits should be harvested when the fruit is ripe. It takes about 18 to 20 months from sowing pineapple to fruit ripening. When the fruit is ripe, the plant starts turning yellow and the color of the fruit becomes reddish yellow. Fruits should be harvested at this time.
Post Harvest work There are a few things to do after the kiwi fruit is harvested. Common post-harvest operations are mentioned below.
Grading Pineapples are classified on the basis of their weight, size and color.
Storage Crowned fruits can be kept without damage for 10-15 days after harvesting. When fruits are transported over long distances or for several days, refrigerated transport is needed to slow the ripening process.
Pineapple can be stored well for a period of 20 days when refrigerated at 10-13 C. Best storage is at 7.2-8 C or 90% relative humidity.
Production capability On the basis of favorable climate, proper distance and scientific method, the average yield in pineapple cultivation is 50-80 tonnes per hectare.
Packing The fruits are packed in baskets woven from bamboo strips. For local markets, the fruits are arranged in baskets (each weighing 20-25 kg.) to stand on their stumps lined with paddy straw.
The second layer of fruits are arranged on the crowns of the first layer of fruits. For distant markets, the fruits are individually wrapped with paddy straw and then packaged.
Transportation Good transportation service helps in getting your products to market easily. Road transport by truck/lorry is the most popular mode of transport due to easy access to the market from the gardens.
Marketing Marketing is the most important part of a successful pineapple farming business. Because, if you can market your products properly, you will definitely make good profits.
Pineapple already has a good demand and price in the market. So, you will be able to easily sell your products locally or in any nearby market.
The growers usually sell their produce to middlemen at the farm gate. Most farmers sell their crops either through trading agents at the village level or through commission agents in the market.
