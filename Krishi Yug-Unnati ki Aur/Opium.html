Opium

Opium- India is the only country authorized by the United Nations Single Convention on Narcotic Drugs (1961) to produce gum opium. Eleven (11) other countries, namely, Australia, Austria, France, China, Hungary, the Netherlands, Poland, Slovenia, Spain, Turkey and the Czech Republic cultivate opium poppy, but do not extract the gum.

The opium/poppy/poppy plant is an important medicinal plant. Its products such as opium and codeine are important drugs that are used for pain relief and hypnotic. The useful part of the poppy plant is a white milky liquid that comes out of the fruit.Opium (or poppy tears, scientific name: Lachryma papaveris) is dried latex obtained from the seed capsules of the opium poppy Papaver somniferum.Opium is a highly addictive narcotic drug acquired in the dried latex form the opium poppy (Papaver somniferum) seed pod.


Famous Varieties A large number of Optimum races are followed in India by their local names. They usually differ in leaf vessels, flower vessels or capillary vessels. Telia, Dholia are some of the local species recommended for commercial cultivation. The varieties recommended for Madhya Pradesh are Jawahar Opium-16, Jawahar Opium-539 and Jawahar Opium-540 etc.

Seed quantity The seed quantity is 7-8 kg/ha for broadcast method and 4-5 kg/ha for line.

Time of sowing The best time for sowing is late October or early November.

Sowing method The seed is either sown or broadcast in lines. The seed is usually mixed with fine sand before airing to spread it evenly. For this line sowing is preferred as the latter method has many drawbacks like high seed, poor crop stand and difficulty in performing traditional tasks.

Distance A distance of 30 cm between lines and 30 cm between plants is generally adopted.

Seed treatment Before sowing, seeds are treated with fungicides such as Dithane M-45@ or Metalaxyl 35% WS. Can be treated with 4 g per kg of seed.

Climate Opium crop requires a temperate climate, its cultivation requires a temperature of 20-25 degrees Celsius.

Land Selection Clay loam soil, black soil is considered more suitable for the cultivation of opium. And there should be good drainage. pH of soil The value should be around 7.

Climate It is a crop of temperate climate but can be grown successfully during winter, in sub-tropical regions. Cold climate favors higher yield, whereas day/night temperature generally affects yield. Frosty, temperature, cloudy or rainy weather reduces not only the quantity, but also the quality of the opium.

Farm Preparation The seed of the poppy is very small, so the preparation of the field plays an important role, so the field is plowed twice vertically and horizontally. The soil is made friable by doing 3-4 plowing of the field. After this, beds are prepared according to the convenience of agricultural work.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers Before sowing for opium cultivation, while preparing the field, well decomposed cow dung at the rate of 25-30 t/ha should be mixed well in the soil. Nutrients essential for good production have an important role in the crop of opium / poppy / poppy. Chemical Fertilizer NPK And sulfur (sulphur) and other soil nutrients should be given only on the basis of soil test.

Weed control and Pruning The first action of weed control and weeding should be done after 25-30 days after sowing and the second action after 35-40 days by removing disease and pest affected and underdeveloped plants. After 50-50 days, the distance from plant to plant is 8-10 cm. Do it while keeping

Irrigation A careful irrigation management schedule is essential for getting a good crop of opium/poppy seeds. One light irrigation is given immediately after sowing followed by second light irrigation after 7 days when seeds start germinating. Three irrigations at an interval of 12-15 days are given till the pre-flowering stage and then the frequency of irrigation is reduced to 8-10 days at the stage of flowering and capsule formation.

Latex (vegetable-milk) collection Opium starts flowering in 95-115 days after sowing. The petals start shed 3-4 days after flowering. Capsules mature 15-20 days after flowering. Lancing of the capsule removes maximum latex (vegetable-milk) at this stage. This phase can be visually observed by compactness and a change in color from green to light green in the capsule. The stage is called industrial maturity.

Harvesting In opium, after the last incision, when the latex has stopped coming out, then the crop is left to dry for 20-25 days. After drying, the balls are collected by breaking them with hands and dried in open space and seeds are removed from the balls.
