Onion

Onion- Generally in Rabi season sowing is done in the month of October-November and transplanting is done from December to first week of January. This season is best for onion cultivation. Due to this onion coming mostly in summer, it is also called summer onion.60 percent of the area under onion is planted in this season. Onion is cultivated in the entire state except in the coastal part of Kokan, Chandrapur and Bhandara in Maharashtra during the Rabi season. Onion is cultivated in this season in all the states of North India. The crop planted at the end of November is harvested in March-April.
At this time the leaves dry well and well-dried onions are stored for more time. The longer the planting of Rabi onions is delayed, the more the yield decreases and the size of the onion remains small. Due to more delay in planting Rabi onion, it is ready in June and due to rain at that time, onion gets damaged and it does not dry well as a result, it starts rotting more in storage.

Seed quantity For raising nursery for one acre farm,4-5 kg of seeds are required.

Seed treatment To keep the seed free from diseases, treat seeds with chemical fungicide Thiram 2 gm + Benomyl 50 WP 1 gm per liter of water per kg of seeds. After chemical treatment it is recommended to treat seeds with bio-agent Trichoderma viride@2gm/kg of seeds. By doing this new plants are saved from soil borne diseases and other diseases.

Nursery Raising Seeds of improved variety of Rabi onion are planted 3 m long, 1 m wide and 20-25 cm. Sow by making raised beds. Nursery seedlings raised in 500 m square area are sufficient for one hectare area. Add 40 grams DAP, 25 grams urea, 30 grams Murate of Potash and 10-15 grams Furadan in each bed and mix it well in the soil.In the month of September-October, prepare the beds after washing Chlorpyriphos (2 ml/Litre water), Carbendazim (1 gm/Litre water) and moisten the soil of the beds and treat the soil for 25-30 days by laying 250 gauge thick white polythene. The treatment of soil by this method is called soil healing. By doing this the soil borne germs and germs get destroyed due to increase in soil temperature.

After germination After germination, to protect the plant from root rot disease, spray 2 grams thyram, 1 gram Bavistin drug in 1 liter of water and spray. About 7-8 weeks after sowing, the seedlings are ready for planting in the field.

Choice of Land Onion crop can be grown in all types of soil. Sandy loam soils are suitable for good onion production, but onion cultivation can be done easily even in medium black soils. The pH value of soil between 6.5-7.5 and containing moderate organic matter is considered best. For a good crop of onion, it is very necessary to have sufficient amount of nutrients Nitrogen, Phosphorus, Potassium, Sulfur, Micronutrients available in the soil.

Farm Preparation For onion cultivation, make the land fine by plowing 2-3 times with a plow as per convenience. If there are large lumps in the field, make it friable and level by running rotovator by tractor.

Manures and Fertilizers Dung manure or compost 200 quintals per hectare and nitrogen, phosphorus, potash and sulfur are required at 100, 50, 100, 25 kg per hectare respectively. Dung manure or compost, phosphorus and potash at the time of land preparation and nitrogen should be divided into three parts, 15 and 45 days after planting respectively. Other general rules should be followed for applying manures and fertilizers. Keep in mind that essential nutrients should be used only on the basis of soil test.

Weed control Weeds growing on the crop - Satyanashi (Kateli), amaranth, dub, motha, spider, khartuwa. Remove weeds from the onion crop by weeding. Propaquizafop 5% + Oxyflurofen 12% w/w EC can be used as a chemical weedicide. Spraying of Pendimethylene 3.5 liters per hectare in 800 liters of water for three days after transplanting does not prevent germination of weeds.

Irrigation In onion cultivation, depending on the soil type and climate, decide the amount and frequency of irrigation. First irrigation is given immediately after sowing and then as per requirement, irrigation is given at an interval of 12-18 days.

Digging It is very important to dig the onion crop at the right time. The exact time of digging depends on the season, market rate etc. Falling of 50% of the upper part of the plants indicates that the crop is now ready for digging. Harvesting of the crop is done by uprooting the onion by hand. After digging the onions leave the field for 2-3 days to remove unnecessary moisture.

Storage Generally, the storage requirement is more in Rabi onions than in Kharif as it sells less immediately in the market. The following precautions should be taken while storing onions.
1. Dry the tubers thoroughly before storage, store only well ripened healthy (4-6 cm size) shiny and solid tubers.
2. Store in moisture free ventilated houses. The thickness of the layer of onion in storage is 15 cm. Do not exceed
3. At the time of storage, rotten tubers should be removed from time to time.
