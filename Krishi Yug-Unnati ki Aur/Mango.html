Mango

Mango- Mango is a juicy fruit, mango is called the king of fruits. Along with this, it is also the national fruit of Pakistan, Philippines. Minerals, vitamins and antioxidants are found in abundance in it, due to which our body remains healthy. It is a sweet fruit and is found more in summer.Mango is also very beneficial for the skin. Applying mango on the skin also opens the clogged pores of the skin, due to which the skin becomes clear and glowing. Our country ranks first in the world in the field of mango production.

A mango is an edible stone fruit produced by the tropical tree Mangifera indica which is believed to have originated from the region between northwestern Myanmar, Bangladesh, and northeastern India. M. indica has been cultivated in South and Southeast Asia since ancient times resulting in two distinct types of modern mango cultivars: the "Indian type" and the "Southeast Asian type". Other species in the genus Mangifera also produce edible fruits that are also called "mangoes", the majority of which are found in the Malesian ecoregion.


Crop Varieties: Some of the famous mango varieties: Hapus, Dussehri, Langra, Chausa, Kesar, Badami, Totapari, Himsagar, India currently has more than 1500 varieties of mangoes.

Hybrid variety - Plants of hybrid variety of mango start bearing fruit soon and their spread is also less, due to this they can also be planted in intensive gardening. Hybrid Varieties: Amrapali - This is a cross between Dashari and Neelam.

Seed Quantity / Seed Sowing Time Rainy season: Suitable time for planting mango: To prepare mango seed plants, mango kernels are sown in June-July. Its 1600 plants can be grown in one hectare.

Time of sowing The transplanting of mango plants should be done at the beginning of the rainy season, the right time for planting the plants is July-August. Plants planted during the rainy season are less prone to die.

Distance Mango plants are planted at a distance of 10 X 10 meters, but in intensive gardening it is planted at a distance of 2.5 to 4 meters. How to prepare pits to prepare mango crop and what kind of precautions should be taken while planting trees.

Sowing Method The rainy season is considered suitable for planting mango trees all over the country. Mango orchards should be planted at the end of the rains in the areas where there is more rainfall. About 50 cm diameter one meter deep pit should be dug in the month of May by mixing about 30 to 40 kg of rotted dung manure in the soil and spraying 100 kg chloropyrifos powder and filling the pits. Plant to plant distance should be 10 to 12 meters according to the type of plants, but for Amrapali variety this distance should be 2.5 meters only.

Seed Treatment Before planting, dip the mango kernels in dimethoate solution for a few minutes. It protects the mango crop from larva. Treat the seeds with Kapton fungicide to protect the seeds from the bad effects of the fungus.

Earth Mango can be cultivated in any type of land. But it is not beneficial to grow it in more sandy, rocky, alkaline and waterlogged land, and loamy land with good drainage is considered best.

Farm Preparation For mango cultivation, in the beginning, remove the remnants of the old crops present in the field and do deep plowing of the field. After that do two to three good slant plowing in the field through cultivator. After plowing, level the field by putting a pad.

Climate In India, it is considered suitable for cultivation up to 25-27 degree centigrade temperature for mango crop.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers Generally, 170 grams per plant per year from 1 to 10 years of age of plants. Urea, 110 g Single Super Phosphate and 115 g Potash Muriat followed by 1.7 kg. 1.1.kg Can be given in two equal doses (June-July and October). Foliar spray of 3% urea is suitable before flowering in sandy areas.Apart from this, to improve the physical and chemical condition of the soil, it has been found appropriate to give 25 to 30 kg of decomposed manure per plant. For organic manure, in July-August, 250 grams of Azospirillum mixed with 40 kg of cow dung and put in the trays has shown an increase in production.

Weed Control Prevention of weeds in mango cultivation, weeding, hoeing and applying soil on the roots. And mixed farming can be done in mango field. Mixed farming helps in controlling weeds from the crop. Cultivation of pulse crops such as moong, urad, lentil and gram etc. can be done as a mixed farming. Crops like onion, tomato, radish, legumes, cauliflower and cabbage can also be used for mixed farming. Do not use millet, maize and sugarcane for mixed farming.

Irrigation In case of developed trees, it is beneficial to irrigate them at an interval of 10 to 15 days from the time they bear fruit to their ripening. For mango crop, irrigation should be done at the interval of 2-3 days in the first year of planting. After 2 to 5 years, it should be done at 4-5 intervals as per the requirement.

Harvest Period The crop period for mango is up to 3-4 years.

Harvesting Time The harvesting time of mango in India is May-June. The ripe fruits of mango should be harvested with 8 to 10 mm long stalks, so that there is no risk of stem rot disease on the fruits. At the time of harvesting, do not let the fruits get bruised and scratched, and avoid contact with the soil.

Production Capability Yield of 200-250 kg/ha, but it has been found to vary depending on the species.

Cleaning and Drying For the proper growth and development of mango plants, at least twice a year, the plates should be cleaned and ploughed.
