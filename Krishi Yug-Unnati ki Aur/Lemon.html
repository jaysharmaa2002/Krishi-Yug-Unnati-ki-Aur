Lemon

Lemon- Lemon is one of the important fruit crops. Various citrus fruits are used around the world to make food or juice. Lemon is cultivated in India the largest in the world. This is a good and profitable business. But, before starting it, you should get a good knowledge of lemon cultivation.

The lemon (Citrus limon) is a species of small evergreen tree in the flowering plant family Rutaceae, native to South Asia, primarily Northeast India (Assam). The tree's ellipsoidal yellow fruit is used for culinary and non-culinary purposes throughout the world, primarily for its juice, which has both culinary and cleaning uses. The pulp and rind are also used in cooking and baking. The juice of the lemon is about 5% to 6% citric acid, with a pH of around 2.2, giving it a sour taste. The distinctive sour taste of lemon juice makes it a key ingredient in drinks and foods such as lemonade and lemon meringue pie.

Lemons entered Europe near southern Italy no later than the second century AD, during the time of Ancient Rome.However, they were not widely cultivated. They were later introduced to Persia and then to Iraq and Egypt around 700 AD. The lemon was first recorded in literature in a 10th-century Arabic treatise on farming, and was also used as an ornamental plant in early Islamic gardens. It was distributed widely throughout the Arab world and the Mediterranean region between 1000 and 1150.An article on Lemon and lime tree cultivation in Andalusia of Spain is brought down in Ibn al-'Awwam's 12th-century agricultural work, Book on Agriculture.


Time of sowing Planting is done from December - February and June - September.

Inter Cropped Intercropping with cowpea, vegetables, French beans can be done in the first two to three years.

Distance 4.5x4.5 distance should be kept between plants. The size of the pits for transplanting new plants is 60x60x60cm. Should be. At the time of transplanting, add 10 kg of decomposed dung manure and 500 gm single super phosphate.

Seed depth The size of the pits for transplanting new plants is 60x60x60cm. Should be.

Sowing Method Reproduction plants are propagated by grafting or by air layering.

Seed quantity A minimum plant density of 208/acre should be maintained. Propagation: Plants are propagated by budding or air level.

Earth Light loamy soil with proper drainage is good for the plant. It yields quite well. Waterlogged land is not suitable for lemon cultivation. The pH value of the land should be around 5.5 to 7.5.

Preparation for Cultivation For the cultivation of lemon, in the beginning, remove the remains of old crops present in the field and do deep plowing of the field. After that do two to three good slant plowing in the field through cultivator. After plowing, level the field by putting a pad.

Climate
Friendly temperature: 20 C - 25 C
Rainfall - 75 cm-200 cm

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers
After the crop is 1 to 3 years old, the amount of manure is 6-18 kg of cow dung, 50-150 grams of nitrogen per plant.
At the age of 4 to 6 years, the amount of manure is 20-40 kg of cow dung, 200-250 grams of nitrogen per plant.
At the age of 6 to 9 years, the amount of manure is 60-90 kg of cow dung, 280-400 grams of nitrogen per plant.
At the age of 10 to 18 years, the amount of manure is 105 kg of cow dung, 400-800 grams of nitrogen per plant.
Apart from this, farmers can use chemical fertilizers, zinc sulphate and other tonic fertilizers as per the requirement. Spraying of water soluble fertilizers has a good effect on the yield. Keep in mind that chemical fertilizers should be used on the basis of soil test.

Weed control Weeds in lemon field can be controlled by hand weeding or by chemicals, after weeding out - use glyphosate 4 liters or per quart 2 liters mixed with 500 to 600 liters of water per hectare. As far as possible, use weedicide before flowering. Weedicide should not be used on main plants.

Irrigation In the cultivation of lemon, special care has to be taken of irrigation for good production. Irrigation is given at an interval of 10 to 12 days from March to June, whereas irrigation is not done in the rainy season, from September to December, irrigation should be done at an interval of 20 days. Over-irrigation is prone to root rot and stem rot diseases.

Harvesting The fruits are harvested when they attain full size. When the color of the fruits appear yellow and attractive. Then they should be cut and separated along with the stalk. Due to which the fruit remains fresh for a long time. Fruits should not be harvested in wet weather or during rains. After harvesting the oranges, take the tail with a clean damp cloth and dry it in a shady place. After this, the fruits are stuffed in a ventilated box with dry grass. Now you can close the box and send it to the market.
