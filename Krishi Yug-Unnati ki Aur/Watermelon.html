Watermelon

Watermelon- Watermelon is a summer fruit. It is green in color from outside, but red inside and rich in water and sweet. And its juice consists of 92% water with proteins, minerals and carbohydrates. Watermelon is mainly cultivated in Maharashtra, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Punjab, Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh and Uttar Pradesh.

Watermelon (Citrullus lanatus) is a flowering plant species of the Cucurbitaceae family and the name of its edible fruit. A scrambling and trailing vine-like plant, it was originally domesticated in Africa. It is a highly cultivated fruit worldwide, with more than 1,000 varieties.

Wild watermelon seeds have been found in the prehistoric Libyan site of Uan Muhuggiag. There is also evidence from seeds in Pharaoh tombs of watermelon cultivation in Ancient Egypt.

Watermelon is grown in favorable climates from tropical to temperate regions worldwide for its large edible fruit, which is a berry with a hard rind and no internal divisions, and is botanically called a pepo. The sweet, juicy flesh is usually deep red to pink, with many black seeds, although seedless varieties exist. The fruit can be eaten raw or pickled, and the rind is edible after cooking. It may also be consumed as a juice or as an ingredient in mixed beverages.

Considerable breeding effort has developed disease-resistant varieties. Many cultivars are available that produce mature fruit within 100 days of planting. In 2017, China produced about two-thirds of the world total of watermelons


Time of sowing
Along the rivers - October-November.
In plains - 15 February to 15 March.

Distance Its distance depends on the different methods of sowing. For digging a pit, the distance from row to row is 2-3.5 m. And there should be a distance of 60 cm from plant to plant.

Seed depth The seed depth of the plant should be 2-3 cm.

Sowing Method There are different ways of sowing it like planting on the beds, digging the pit, applying it according to the season and season in the bund.
Planting in beds: Plant the seeds on one side of the bed. Sow 3-4 seeds at a time and keep a healthy boot after harvesting. Plant spacing is 60-90 cm. Keep it.
Planting by digging a pit: Sow 4 seeds in a tow. Pits 60x60x60 cm Keep why Keep spacing of 2-3.5 m in two rows and spacing of 0.6-1.2 m between plants. Fill the pits well with humus and soil. After deposition, place a boot in a pit.
Weeding:This method is similar to the method of digging a pit, in this 30x30x30 cm. Make pits at a distance of 1-1.5 meters. Plant two seeds on a ridge.

Seed quantity 4 to 5 kg seed per hectare is sufficient.

Seed treatment Before sowing, do seed treatment with Carbendazim @ 2 gm or Thiram @ 2 gm/kg of seed. After chemical treatment, apply seed with Trichoderma @ 4 g/kg of seed. Dry the seeds in shade and then sow immediately.

Earth Watermelon can be cultivated from highly sandy soils to clay loamy soils, especially in the sandy lands along the banks of rivers, sandy loams with proper drainage in the plains are considered the best. 5.5 to 7.0 pH The land is suitable for this.

Farm Preparation First plowing should be done with earth turning plow and after that 2-3 times by plowing with hare or cultivator, the land should be friable and level.

Favorable Climate: Temperature - 20-30 C
Rainfall - 50-75 mm
Sowing temperature - 20-25 C
Harvesting temperature - 25-30 C

Farm yard manure or well decomposed cow dung @ 8-10 tonnes per acre per acre. Apply Urea @ 55 kg, Phosphorus @ 16 kg and Potash @ 15 kg, Urea @ 55 kg, Single Super Phosphate @ 100 kg and Potash @ 25 kg per acre. Apply phosphorus, full dose of potash and one-third of nitrogen before sowing. Apply the remaining dose of nitrogen near the vine base, avoid touching it and mix it well into the soil during the initial growth period.
When the crop is 10-15 days old, for good quality as well as increased crop growth, spray 19:19:19 + micro-nutrients @ 2-3 g/Lit of water. Prevent flower drop and increase yield by 10%. Spray Humic Acid @ 3ml + MAP (12:61:00) @ 5 g/Lit of water at the stage of spraying. Spray Salicylic Acid (4-5 tab. of Aspirin tablet 350mg)/15Ltr of water once or twice with an interval of 30 days at early flowering, fruiting and maturing stage.
Spray 13:00:45 @ 100 g + Hexaconazole @ 250 ml / 150 l of water after 55 days of sowing for rapid fruit development and to prevent powdery mildew. To increase fruit size 65 days after sowing, sweetening and color spraying is done at 00:00:50 @ 1.5kg/acre using 100gm/15Ltr of water.

Weed control After irrigation, weeds start growing. It is very important to remove them from the crop, otherwise their effect falls on the yield. Simultaneously, more plants should be removed from the stem. Only 2 or 3 plants should be kept. In this way, 2 or 3 weedings should be done in the entire crop. If there are diseases and pests plants, then they should be removed from the crop so that other plants do not get infected with pests and diseases.

Irrigation Irrigate every week during summer season. Irrigate only when needed at the time of maturity. Avoid flooding the watermelon area. When irrigating, do not over-wet the vines or vegetative parts, avoid frequent irrigation in heavy soils especially during flowering and fruit set as it will promote excessive vegetative growth. For better sweetness and taste, stop irrigation or reduce watering 3-6 days before harvesting.

Harvest period The fruits are large in size with dark green skin and pinkish red flesh. It gives a yield of 36-38 t/ha in 122-135 days.

Harvest time If the tender near the stem dries out, turns white in color when it touches the ground, turns yellow, then assume the fruit is ready to be harvested. A thumping watermelon if it looks hollow (usually in the form of a dull thump or thud) then it is ready to harvest and the immature fruit looks dense.
Do not pick immature fruits as they are only attached to the vine. Immature fruit has no sugar content or color. To harvest ripe fruit, cut 1 stem from the fruit with a sharp pruner or a pair of knives. The fruits can be stored in a cool moist environment.
