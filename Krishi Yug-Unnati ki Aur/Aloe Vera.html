Aloe Vera

Akoe Vera- Aloe Vera is a type of medicinal and prickly plant, Aloe Vera / Aloe vera is also known as Ghrit Kumari, Quargandal, or Guarpatha. Known as a medicinal plant. Aloe vera plant is a fleshy and succulent plant without stem or very short stem, whose length is up to 60-100 cm. It is spread by branches coming out from below.

Its leaves are lanceolate, thick and fleshy in color, greenish-gray, with white spots on the upper and lower surface of the leaf in some varieties. There is a row of small white teeth on the edge of the leaf. Yellow flowers are produced in the summer season. Due to the medicinal properties of aloe vera, this plant is in great demand in India.

From pharmaceutical companies to cosmetic products, face packs, healthcare, and textile companies, it buys it in large quantities. In view of the demand of aloe vera in the market, it is very beneficial to cultivate this aloe vera.


Time of sowing Aloe vera can be cultivated throughout the year except the winter months. But for good growth, it is advisable to plant aloe vera plants in February or July-August.

Distance There should be a distance of at least 40 cm between the plants.

Sowing method Nursery is prepared by sowing aloe vera seed, it should be sown by 6-8 inch seedlings. It is sown by 3-4 months old tubers having four to five leaves.

Seed quantity About 5000 to 10000 tubers are needed for one acre of farmland.

Favorable Climate Warm climate is good for the cultivation of aloe vera. It is generally cultivated successfully in dry area with minimum rainfall and in hot humid area. This plant is very sensitive to extreme cold conditions.

Land selection Aloe vera can be cultivated in different types of soil, loamy soil, sandy soil and black soil are more suitable for its cultivation. Sloping land and land with proper drainage are more favorable for its cultivation. The pH value of its soil should be 8.5.

Farm preparation Before sowing aloe vera tubers, the field should be plowed well 2-3 times. At the time of last ploughing, the field should be made flat and friable by applying cow dung manure in the field. After leveling the field, make beds according to the required distance. Keep in mind that there should not be a problem of water logging in the field.

Fertilizers and chemical Fertilizers For good growth and development of Aloe Vera, well-rotted manure (FYM) at the rate of 8-10 t/acre should be mixed well in the soil at the time of field preparation. And chemical fertilizers should be given on the basis of soil test and on the advice of agricultural experts.

Weed control To control weeds, weeding should be done from time to time as per the requirement.

Irrigation Aloe vera tubers require a light irrigation immediately after transplanting, and as needed after the first irrigation.

Harvest Aloe vera plant becomes ready in 7-8 months, you can harvest it in the eighth month. Never cut the plant from the root because the plant grows again from its root.

Production About 40 to 45 tons of thick leaves are obtained from the cultivation of Aloe Vera in one hectare.
