Groundnut

Groundnut-Groundnut cultivation is a leading crop of oilseed crops, whose botanical name is Arachis hyphegia, which is an important source of oil in our food, which uses about 80 percent of its production as oil. Is. Half a handful of peanuts contains 426 calories, 15 grams of carbohydrates, 17 grams of protein and 35 grams of fat. It is also rich in Vitamin E, K and B6. It is a good source of iron, niacin, folate, calcium and zinc.
It is grown the most in the states of Gujarat, Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu and Karnataka. In other states like Madhya Pradesh, Uttar Pradesh, Rajasthan and Punjab, it is also considered as a very important crop. Nutritional breakdown: Peanuts are an especially good source of healthful fats, protein, and fiber. They also contain plenty of potassium, phosphorous, magnesium, and B vitamins. Despite being high in calories, peanuts are nutrient-rich and low in carbohydrates


Seed Quantity:- Sowing should be done at the rate of 80-100 kg/ha of groundnut seed.

Time of sowing :-
Groundnut is usually sown with the onset of monsoon. But where irrigation facility is available, pre-monsoon sowing should be done in the last week of May or first week of June along with pre-sowing irrigation.

Friendly temperature: The optimum temperature for vegetative growth of groundnut is between 26 to 30 C. The production increase is maximum at 24-27 C. It grows well in areas receiving 50 to 125 cm of well-distributed rainfall during the growing season, an abundance of sunlight and relatively warm temperatures.

Seed Treatment :- Before sowing the seed should be treated with 3 gm Thiram or 2 gm Mancozeb or Carbendizim drug per kg of seed. After five-six hours of treatment with fungicide, the seed should be treated with Rhizobium culture of groundnut before sowing.

Earth :- Groundnut can be cultivated in different types of soils, yet for its good preparation, fertile and nutrient-rich loamy loamy soil is best. The pH value of the soil is 6.0 to 8.0.

Land preparation: For the cultivation of groundnut, before sowing, the field should be plowed thoroughly 2-3 times with a country plow or cultivator, so that the soil becomes friable, then after this, prepare the field for sowing by running the pat.

Land Treatment :- For the prevention of other insects like termites, use Chlorpyriphos at the rate of 3 liters per hectare.

Manures and Fertilizers :- Before sowing in groundnut cultivation, while preparing the field, well decomposed cow dung of 15-20 tons/acre, should be mixed well in the soil. Application of N:P:K(12.5:25:0) kg/per acre in chemical fertilizer, 250 kg gypsum/acre at preflowering stage 30 DAS (days after sowing) is required to prepare and develop as required. Keep in mind that chemical fertilizers should be given only on the basis of soil test.

Harmful diseases: Leaf spots - Light and dark brown spots appear on the leaves. In severe disease, it attacks the petiole and stem. To prevent this, the seed should be disease free and treat the seed with Thiram 3 gm or Mancozeb 2 gm/kg.
Ukta disease - It is spread by a fungus called Fusarium. Symptoms of the disease usually appear at the flowering stage of the crop. This disease can be seen between the months of September to January. The plant turns yellow and dries up. In this, the roots become dark in color and after removing the bark, black stripes are found from the root to the height of the stem.
For the prevention of this disease, it is necessary to do good deep plowing of the field during summer. Due to deep plowing in summer and intercropping of sorghum with tur, the infection of this disease is less. And sow after treating the seeds.

Diseases and pests and their prevention:- White braided, Bihar hairy caterpillar, groundnut mahu and termite are prominent. In areas with white braid problem, apply Phorate 10g or Carbofuran 3g @ 20-25 kg/ha in the field before sowing. To control the infestation of termites, use Chlorpyriphos at the rate of 3 liters per hectare.
To control the infestation of termites, use Chlorpyriphos at the rate of 3 liters per hectare.
Imidacloprid 0.5 ml/l or Dimethoate 30 EC for control of sucking insects (mahoo, thrips and whitefly). of 2 ml/l Make a solution in 500 liters of water and use it. Cunolphos 25 EC for the control of leaf borer pest. Spraying should be done by making a solution of 1 liter/ha in 500 liters of water.
Tikka, collar and stem rot and rosette disease are the major outbreaks in groundnut. As soon as the symptoms of tikka appear, for its prevention, dithane M-45 should be sprayed after making a solution of 2 g/Litre of water. Repeat spraying at 10-12 days interval. Roseate is a virus borne disease, to prevent its spread, spraying of imidacloprid solution with the value of 0.5 ml / liter of water should be done on the crop.

Weed control :- To get a good yield of groundnut, at least one weeding must be done. Due to this, the spread of roots is good, as well as air circulation in the soil also increases. And the earthing work is done automatically, which increases the production. This work should be done with a Kolpa or a hand wheel. Pendimethylene 38.7 percent 750 grams for weed control by chemical method. The active ingredients can be applied at the rate of per hectare within 3 days of sowing or 100 ml of Imathapar in standing crop. The active ingredients can be dissolved in 400-500 liters of water and used after 15-20 days of sowing. Also, one weeding and hoeing must be done 30-35 days after sowing.

Weed control :- For control of Arhar weeds, do first weeding in 20-25 days and second weeding before flowering. Weed killer Pendimethalin 2 L. per acre. 150-200 liters Put in water after 2 days of sowing. After the use of weedicide, it is beneficial to do a sleep at the stage of about 30 to 40 days. Quijalophap 5 EC is used for the control of narrow leaf or grass total weeds. Use Tergasuper 800-1000 ml 15-20 days after sowing.

Irrigation :- Groundnut is a rain-fed crop, so there is no special need for irrigation. More water than required in the field should be immediately removed, otherwise there is an adverse effect on growth and yield. For summer groundnut, it is recommended to irrigate the crop at the physical growth stages. Branching (25-30 DAS), Flowering (40-45 DAS), Peg formation (55-60 DAS), Peg penetration (65-70 DAS), Pod formation (80-85 DAS), Pod development (91- 95 DAS), pod filling stage (102-107 DAS) and post-sowing maturity with mango irrigation (115-120 DAS). *DAS (days after sowing)

Harvesting time :- The main signs of maturity are yellowing of leaves, wilting of leaves and drop of older leaves. The pod is ripe when it hardens and when the inner side of the cells is dark in color. Harvesting before maturity reduces yield due to shrinkage of seeds when they dry up. Due to delay in harvesting, the seeds are germinated in the filed as the seeds are dormant in the bunch variety.

Cleaning and Drying :- � At the time of harvesting peanuts, the pods typically contain more than 40% (wet base) moisture and vary widely in physical maturity. The pods need to be cleaned and dried to reduce the humidity to 10% or less for safe storage or marketing. � Drying should be done rapidly to prevent molding or other deterioration, but not too rapidly to reduce quality. This will help maintain the desirable flavor, texture, germination and overall quality.
