Walnut

Walnut- You know Walnut is a very beautiful and fragrant tree that has autumn. Walnut fruit is a type of dry fruit which is used for food. The outer shell of the walnut is very hard and inside is a kernel shaped like a human brain. Walnut cultivation or horticulture is done mainly in the hilly areas in the country of India.It is mostly used in the confectionery industry. In India it is cultivated in Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, Kupwara, Uri, Dras and Poonch snowy valleys of Kashmir and in states like Arunachal Pradesh.

A walnut is the nut of any tree of the genus Juglans (family Juglandaceae), particularly the Persian or English walnut, Juglans regia. A walnut is the edible seed of a drupe, and thus not a true botanical nut. It is commonly consumed as a nut. After full ripening for its edible seed when the shell has been discarded, it is used as a garnish or a snack. Nuts of the eastern black walnut (Juglans nigra) and butternuts (Juglans cinerea) are less commonly consumed.


Time of sowing Walnut seedlings are prepared in the month of May and June, about a year before transplanting in the nursery. The proper time for planting walnut seedlings is from December to March, but the month of December is more suitable.

Distance According to the growing nature of walnut varieties, the spacing of pits should be done at a distance of 10 to 12 meters.

Sowing method Walnuts are sown by direct seeding by raising nursery or by grafting method or grafting method.

Grafting method To prepare the seedlings through grafting, first pluck all the leaves of 5 to 7 months old branch of any nut parent plant 15 days before grafting. After that, remove the leafless branch from the plant and tie it well by cutting it diagonally from one side and connecting it with another wild plant. Apart from this, its seedlings can also be easily prepared by V grafting method.

Planting Method Nut seedlings prepared in nursery are planted in pits prepared in the field. For this, another small pit is prepared in the middle of the pits. In which the prepared seedlings are planted in the nursery.

Favorable climate The temperate climate regions are considered suitable for walnut cultivation. Extremely hot summer and winter are both unsuitable for walnut cultivation. Walnut plants need 20 to 25 degree temperature for initial development. After that, the maximum temperature of 35 degree in summer and minimum 5 degree in winter is suitable for the growth of its plant, if the temperature is less or more than this, the growth of plants stops.

Land selection Walnut cultivation requires fertile land with proper drainage, sandy and hard surface soils are not suitable for walnuts. Walnuts are very sensitive to alkaline soils, so it should not be planted in alkaline soils. The pH of the land for its cultivation. The value should be between 5 and 7.

Farm preparation Before planting, plowing the field thoroughly and leave it open for a few days, after that run the rotavator in the field and after making the soil friable, make the field level by applying a pat, so that there is no problem of water logging. After leveling the land, keeping the proper distance, should prepare pits of 2 feet width and 1 to one and a half feet deep. Irrigation should be done by planting trees.

Fertilizers and chemical Fertilizers While preparing the pit, well-rotted manure or vermicompost should be mixed well in the soil so that the growth of plants is good. And use chemical fertilizers on the basis of soil test.

Weed control To prevent weeds, weeding and hoeing should be done around the plants from time to time.

Irrigation Irrigation should be done immediately after planting and for this, initially the plants should be watered once a week in summer, whereas in winter season the plants should be watered at an interval of 20 to 30 days and in winter at the time of high frost reading. The effect of frost on the plants is less visible by giving light water at the earliest. Irrigation is required at the time of fruiting and flowering.

Inter crops Walnut plants start yielding after four years of planting in the field. During this, the farmer brothers can easily grow short-term horticultural crops (papaya), vegetable, medicinal and spice crops in the vacant land between the trees, due to which the farmer brothers also get continuous yield from their field.

Harvest Walnut fruits should be harvested when the upper bark of walnut fruits starts cracking. After ripening the fruits of walnuts themselves start falling apart. When about 20 percent of the fruits fall from the plant, then a tall bamboo should be taken and its fruits should be dropped from the plant. The fallen fruits under the nut should be collected and covered with the leaves of the plant. So that the bark of the fruit can be easily removed. To make its fruits more lustrous, they are kept in a special type of solution and kept. After that its fruits are dried in the sun.

Production Walnut plants normally start yielding after 20 to 25 years. But at present, different varieties of plants produced start yielding only three to four years after transplanting. Which gives annual yield after 20 to 25 years at the rate of 40 kg per plant.
