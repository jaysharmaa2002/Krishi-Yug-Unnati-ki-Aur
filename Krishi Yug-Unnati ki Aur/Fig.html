Fig

Fig- Figs are a popular fruit, which are eaten fresh and dried. In India it is cultivated in Rajasthan, Haryana, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Maharashtra, Gujarat and some parts of Uttar Pradesh. Fig is a deciduous shrub or a small tree native to the Mediterranean region and Southwest Asia, found from Pakistan to Greece. Its length can be up to 3-10 feet.

Figs are a unique fruit resembling a teardrop. They're about the size of your thumb, filled with hundreds of tiny seeds, and have an edible purple or green peel. The flesh of the fruit is pink and has a mild, sweet taste. The scientific name for the fig is Ficus carica.

You should avoid consuming too much dried or fresh figs, as they are loaded with fibre and can cause digestive issues. Figs are known for adding a burst of sweetness to all kinds of dishes and are also a rich source of fibre, vitamins and minerals


Crop Variety Fig tree is small and deciduous in nature. The land between Turkistan and northern India is believed to be its place of origin.

Improved variety -
Effective variety of figs - Bhabi F-I, BF-II and BF-III have been done. Among these, BF-III variety was found to be the best and it was named as 'Budka Anjeer' variety.
Varieties named Marseilles, Black Ischia, Poona, Bangalore and Brown Turkey are famous in India. Talking about the world, brown turkey, Brunswick and Osborne are some of the major varieties of figs.

Time of Sowing The time of planting figs is mainly from December to January or July to August, the climate of December-January is best for planting figs.

Distance Keep a distance of 6x6 meters.

Sowing Method Use breeding method for sowing. Fig plants are produced mainly by mature cuttings 1 to 2 cm thick, 15 to 20 cm long. Taking cuttings from mother plants in winter, they are pressed into the soil for calving for 1 to 2 months. In fig nursery beds, 7 kg of cow dung per square meter and 25 to 30 grams of phosphorus and 20 to 25 grams of potash manure,The bed should be added at the time of preparation. Nitrogen fertilizer should be applied one month after planting 10 to 15 grams per square meter of cuttings and the same amount should be applied after 2 months.

Seed quantity Fig plants are planted from 400 to 500 in one hectare.

Nursery/Seed Treatment Small fig plants should be given 7 to 10 kg of cow dung in 1 to 3 years and 15 to 25 kg of cow dung per plant in older plants from the age of 3 years should be applied annually. Use fertilizer according to the requirement of the soil, although the crop of figs gives good yields even without the use of fertilizers.

Climate Hot and tropical weather is best for figs. The desert climate is considered favorable for good fig production. Therefore you will find that the major varieties of figs are mostly grown in such areas. There are only a few varieties that can be grown even at temperatures below 40 degrees Fahrenheit i.e. 4 degrees Celsius. It is very important to keep the atmosphere dry at the time of development and maturity of fig fruit.Being a deciduous tree, figs can be cultivated in different types of climate, but the fig plant thrives well in warm, dry and shade-free sub-tropical and warm-temperate conditions. Is. Figs can be cultivated in a wide variety of climates, but the fig plant thrives in warm, dry and shade-free sub-tropical and warm-temperate conditions.

Earth Figs can be grown in all types of soil. Light loamy soil with proper drainage is good for the plant. It yields quite well. Waterlogged land is not suitable for orange cultivation. The pH value of the land should be around 6 to 8.

Farm Preparation Dig the pits for the plant and add 5 kg of cow dung to the pits and then add 20-25 kg of phosphorus and potash.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers Use fertilizer according to the requirement of the soil, although the crop of figs gives good yields even without the use of fertilizers. Small fig plants should be given 7 to 10 kg of cow dung in 1 to 3 years and 15 to 25 kg of cow dung per plant in older plants from the age of 3 years should be applied annually.
For good crop, it is beneficial to give 20-30 rotten dung manure or compost in January-February per tree per year. Although no major pests or diseases have been observed in figs, outbreaks of leaf and bark-eating insects have been observed under certain circumstances. For its control, spraying with 3 ml endosulfan or chloropyrifos per liter of water is beneficial.

Irrigation Fig plants do not require a lot of water and a limited amount of water once or twice a week will suffice after planting.

Harvest Period Fig trees should be mechanically trained to have a single stem initially to encourage a broad, proper crown with a strong structure. A new fig tree starts bearing fruit in about 2 to 3 years.

Harvesting Time Fig fruits ripen from May to August. They should be harvested only when the fruits are fully ripe.

Cleaning and Drying The teaching of fig plants should be in such a way that its spread in every direction is equal and sunlight can reach every part of the plant. In this, the fruit appears on the new branches emerging on one to two year old twigs. Therefore, this type of twigs should be promoted in the initial years. Heavy pruning of old trees is beneficial. The diseased and dry branches should be pruned after plucking.
