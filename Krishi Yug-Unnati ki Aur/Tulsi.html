Tulsi

Tulsi- Tulsi is considered a sacred plant since ancient times. Which is worshiped by putting it in every house, Tulsi has a very big place in Ayurveda. Tulsi is used to treat many diseases. Consuming it daily gives relief from many types of diseases. Presently it is being done as medicinal cultivation. It is found all over India. It has many types of species such as: - Occium basilicum, Gratissimum, sactum, minimum, Americananum.

Tulsi or Sacred basil (Ocimum sanctum L.) is a biennial shrub belonging to the family Lamiaceae. The plant has been revered by the people of India for its multivarious uses since vedic times. Even now, it is worshipped by many. The essential oil of sacred basil has about 71 per cent eugenol and is comparable to that of clove oil. Eugenol is widely used in perfumery, cosmetics, pharmaceuticals and confectionary industries. The juice of the leaves possesses antiseptic, diaphoretic, antiperiodic, stimulating, expectorant, anti-pyretic and memory improving properties. It is one among the few plants which purifies the atmosphere

Tulsi is a plant found in all the states of India. Tulsi plant is found up to an altitude of 1800 meters. It is commercially cultivated in the states of Uttarakhand, Jammu and Kashmir, Uttar Pradesh and West Bengal in India. There are mainly 3 types of Tulsi. First with green leaves, second with black leaves and third with some blue-violet leaves. The special thing is that it is less irrigated and least affected by diseases and pests.


CLIMATE- The crop has a wide adaptability and can be grown successfully in tropical and sub-tropical climates. Long days with high temperature have been found favourable for plant growth and oil production.

SOILS - The plant is sufficiently hardy and it can be grown on any type of soil except the ones with highly saline, alkaline or water logged conditions. However, sandy loam soil with good organic matter is considered ideal.

Preparation of the field: - First of all, after doing 1 or 2 deep plowing with deep plowing machines, the field should be leveled by applying a pedestal. After this, the right size beds should be made while making proper arrangements for irrigation and drainage.

By Seed:- Basil is propagated on a large scale by seed only. 15 - 20 cm of the ground. The weeds etc. should be prepared by digging deep. 15 ton per ha. Sowing the seed 8-10 cm by mixing sand or sand in the ratio of 1:10. should be done in rows at a distance. The depth of the seed should not be too much. 20 kg/ha after 15-20 days of deposition. Nursery raising - For propagating basil through seeds, they are to be sown in the nursery beds. The nursery should be located preferably in partial shade with adequate irrigation facilities. Soil is worked upto a depth of about 30 cm. Well rotten farm yard manure (2 kg/sq.m) is applied to the soil and prepared to a fine tilth and seed beds of 4.5x1.0x0.2 m size are prepared. As the seeds are minute, the required quantity of seeds are mixed with sand in the ratio of 1:4 and sown in nursery bed, 2 months in advance of the onset of monsoon. They germinate in 8-12 days and seedlings are ready for transplanting in about 6 weeks time at 4-5 leaf stage.

By branches: - Tulsi can also be propagated by cutting branches. Cut 10-15 such long branches and keep them in the shade and keep watering with Hazare in the morning and evening. Roots develop from these branches in a month. And the leaves begin to develop. When the branches become 5-10 cm, then they can be transplanted.

Vegetative propagation - Vegetatively it can be propagated by terminal cuttings with about 90-100 per cent success during October-December months. For this purpose, cuttings with 8-10 nodes and 10-15 cm length are used. They are so prepared that except for the first 2-3 pairs of leaves the rest are trimmed off. Later, they are planted in the well prepared nursery beds or polythene bags. In about 4-6 weeks time the rooting is complete and they are ready for transplanting into the mainfield. The plants are transplanted at a spacing of 40 cm between the row and 40 cm within the row.

Planting method: - For 1 hectare field, seedlings prepared from about 200-300 grams of seeds are suitable. The seeds should be sown in the nursery 2 cm below the soil. The seeds usually germinate in 8-12 days. After this, plants with 4-5 leaves are ready for transplanting in about 6 weeks. Line to line distance of 45 cm and plant to plant distance of 20-25 cm must be kept for high yield per hectare and good oil production. Only healthy plants should be selected for planting basil. so that the yield is good.

Fertilizers and Fertilizers: - All parts of the Tulsi plant are mostly used for medicinal use, so it is better not to use chemical fertilizers or reduce them very much. In 1 hectare field, 10-15 tons of well-rotted cow dung or 5 tons of vermi compost is suitable. If chemical fertilizers are needed, then they should be used according to the soil test. The recommended amount of phosphorus and potash should be used at the time of tillage and the total amount of nitrogen should be divided into 3 parts in 3 times.

Irrigation :- First irrigation should be done immediately after transplanting. After that irrigation should be done according to the moisture content of the soil. By the way, irrigation may be required 3 times every month in summer. If it rains during the rainy season, then there is no need for irrigation.

IRRIGATION - After transplanting irrigation is provided twice a week till one month so that the plants establish themselves well. Later, it is given at weekly interval depending upon the rainfall and soil moisture status.

HARVESTING AND YIELD - The crop is harvested at full bloom stage by cutting the plants at 15 cm from ground level to ensure good regeneration for further harvests. The first harvest is done after 90 days of planting and subsequently it may be harvested at every 75 days interval. Harvest the crop on bright sunny days to get good yield and oil quality.

Yield :- Average yield of its crop is 20-25 tonnes per hectare and oil yield of 80-100 kg. up to hectare.
