Cumin

Cumin- The botanical name of cumin is Cuminum cyaminum. Cumin is the main spice crop. More than 80 percent of the country's cumin is grown in the states of Gujarat and Rajasthan. More than 80 percent of the country's cumin is grown in the states of Gujarat and Rajasthan. It is an important spice used in Indian kitchens for flavoring various food preparations.
Cumin is also extensively used in various Ayurvedic medicines especially for conditions like obesity, abdominal pain and dyspesia. Average yield of cumin (380 kg/ha) is much lower than that of neighboring Gujarat (550 kg/ha). The present yield of cumin can be increased by 25-50 percent by the use of advanced techniques.Cumin is a spice made from the seeds of the Cuminum cyminum plant. Many dishes use cumin, especially foods from its native regions of the Mediterranean and Southwest Asia.


Time of sowing Sowing of cumin should be done between 1st November to 25th November.

Seed quantity A seed rate of 12-15 kg/ha is sufficient.

Seed treatment Before sowing the seed is treated with fungicide like Mancozeb @ 2 gm per kg seed or Trichoderma 4 to 6 gm per kg is used.

Sowing method It is sown by broadcast and line sowing.

Temperature The temperature at the time of sowing cumin should be 24 to 28C and at the time of vegetative growth, the temperature of 20 to 25C is suitable.

Land selection For the cultivation of cumin, light and loamy fertile land containing biomass is good. Cumin cultivation can be done easily in this land. Proper drainage system is essential in the land. Heavy, saline and alkaline lands are considered a hindrance for its cultivation.

Required Climate Moderate sub tropical climate is ideal for cumin cultivation. Moderately cool and dry climate is best. This well drained and cool weather yield and sub-tropical climate is ideal for cumin seed cultivation.

Farm Preparation For the cultivation of cumin, the process of turning the soil with a plow or cultivator is done at the time of first plowing in the field. After this, the soil becomes soft and friable after 3-4 ploughings. After this, the beds are made by leveling the field.

Manures and Fertilizers The amount of manure fertilizers for cumin crop should be given after checking the land. Under normal circumstances, first 5 tons of cow dung or compost should be mixed well in the field at the time of last plowing. After this, 65 kg DAP at the time of sowing. And 9 kg urea should be mixed in the field. On first irrigation, 33 kg urea should be sprayed per hectare.

Weed control Weeds are a serious problem in cumin cultivation. There is more outbreak of weeds in the cumin crop because cumin seeds grow in the initial stage and the crop is damaged, till two days after the time of sowing to control the weeds in cumin, a herbicide named Pendimethalin (Stomp) is available in the market. The available 3.3 liters should be mixed with 500 liters of water and sprayed. After this, when the crop is 25 -30 days old, then one hoeing should be done. If there is a problem of laborers, then 750 ml of weed-killer called Oxydizaryl (RAFT) is available in the market. Make a solution in 500 liters of water and spray it.

Irrigation In cumin cultivation, irrigation should be done immediately after sowing the seeds. Keep in mind that irrigation should be light and should not be done in fast flow. By doing it in a strong current, cumin gets swept away in the water and comes to a place. Give second irrigation after 7 days of sowing. After this, 4 to 5 irrigations should be given at the interval of every 20 days. Remember don't irrigate cumin flower.

Harvesting and Threshing Generally, when the seed and plant become brown in color and the crop is fully ripe, it should be harvested immediately. The plants should be dried thoroughly and threshed with a thresher to separate the grain. The grain should be dried thoroughly and stored in clean sacks.

Yield and Profit On using advanced methods, the average yield of cumin seeds is 7-8 quintal seeds per hectare. The cost of cultivation of cumin is about 30 to 35 thousand rupees per hectare. If the price of cumin seeds is Rs 100 per kg, a net profit of Rs 40 to 45 thousand per hectare can be obtained.
