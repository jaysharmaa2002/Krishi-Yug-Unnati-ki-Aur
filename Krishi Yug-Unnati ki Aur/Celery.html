Celery

Celery- Celery is a herbaceous plant. Which is grown as a vegetable since ancient times. Celery leaves have long fibrous stalks. Depending on the location and cultivar, its stalk, leaves or hypocotyl are used in cooking. Celery seed is also used as a spice and its extracts have been used in herbal medicine. Celery is used for joint pain, headache, nervousness, rheumatism, overwork, blood purification, etc.
It is rich in Vitamin C, Vitamin K, Vitamin B6, folate and potassium. It is found mostly in Mediterranean regions, in South Asia, in marshy areas of Europe and North America and in some areas of India. Ladwa and Saharanpur districts in western Uttar Pradesh, Amritsar, Gurdaspur and Jalandhar districts of Haryana and Punjab are the main salary growing areas.

Not only is garden celery better-tasting than store-bought types, but also it's less chemically-laden. In cool spring and summer regions, plant celery in early spring. In warm spring and summer regions, plant celery in mid to late summer for harvest in late autumn or early winter. Here's our advice on sowing, growing, and harvesting celery.

This cool-weather crop requires 16 weeks of cool weather to come to harvest. Celery is considered a hardy biennial, but it's grown as an annual which is mainly grown for its edible 12- to 18-inch stalks. It's not difficult to grow celery but you do need start celery from seed indoors; transplants are hard to find and do not always succeed.For us, celery is a staple in the garden because it's so useful in the kitchen-for stews, stirfries, soups, and salads.


Time of sowing Ajwain is drought tolerant for 8 to 10 days. The best time for sowing this crop is August. The seed is sown directly in the field. It takes 8 to 10 days for the seeds to rot.

Distance Row to row distance for transplanting is 30-40 cm. And the distance from plant to plant is 25-30 cm. Keep it

Seed Depth Seeds should be planted 2-4 cm apart. Sow at the depth off

Seed quantity For sowing in one hectare area, about 2.5-3.0 kg seed is required for Rabi Season crop, and 4-5 kg seed is required for Kharif crop.

Land Selection Open field for celery, where there is more sunlight and the variety of soil can be easily grown in sandy loam to clay, black soil and red soil and the pH of the soil. The value is suitable only between 6.5-9.0. It gives good yield in loamy soil with organic elements.

Climate It is a cold loving crop and is mainly grown during Rabi season in India. Moderately cool and dry climate favors good plant growth and flowering. Avoidance of high humidity is especially beneficial after flowering.

Farm Preparation For ajwain cultivation, flat and friable soil is required. To bring the soil to a good level, plow with plow 4-5 times and after plowing, ploughing is done. Salary is planted on the prepared nursery beds.

Manures and Fertilizers In general, to increase the well irrigated crop of Ajwain, 10 tonnes of well decomposed cow dung or compost can be applied and spread evenly over the field before plowing. At the time of last tillage 30 kg of nitrogen, 40 kg of phosphorus and 30 kg of potash/ha can be applied to the soil. Keep in mind that chemical fertilizers should be used only on the basis of soil test.

Weed Control After irrigation of celery, grass and other wild plants should be removed from the crop by weeding. In this way weeds are also controlled and the buried soil becomes better for air circulation and plants grow more.

Irrigation Ajwain is cultivated as both rainfed and irrigated crop. About 4-5 irrigations are required in irrigated production system. If the initial moisture after sowing is low, a light irrigation is given after 4-5 days to check germination and facilitate scab formation.

Harvesting Fruit matures in 120-140 days after sowing. Ajwain fruits are brown oval fragrant cremocarps with a single seed. Usually, the harvesting phase comes within six months. Harvesting is done using thresher or by hitting the removed plants with sticks.

Storage After harvesting, it is sorted individually according to the need. Ajwain is then stored in cellar, cold, cold store etc., so that it can be handled for a long time.
