Apple

Apple-You know, India ranks ninth in apple cultivation in the world. The major apple producing states in India are Jammu and Kashmir, Himachal Pradesh, Uttaranchal and Arunachal Pradesh. It is one of the temperate fruits. It is said about apples that "An apple a day keeps the doctor away" means that by eating an apple every day, you do not need a doctor. This fruit is rich in Vitamin B, Vitamin C and mineral salts.This fruit is considered good for heart, brain and liver patients. In our country, apart from being eaten as a fruit, sev is also used in the form of chutney, marmalade, jam, jelly, pickle etc. Apple occupies a prominent place due to its distinctive taste, aroma, color and good storage capacity. Apples are rich in carbohydrates, fats, proteins, minerals as well as many vitamins.

An apple is an edible fruit produced by an apple tree. Apple trees are cultivated worldwide and are the most widely grown species in the genus Malus. The tree originated in Central Asia, where its wild ancestor, Malus sieversii, is still found today


Planting time The best time for sowing apples is from January to March.

Plants per acre About 180 apple saplings are planted per acre.

Distance Plant to plant distance is generally 5 * 5 m, pits are dug one month before planting. The size of the pit should be 2.5 * 2.5 * 2.5 feet in good and fertile soil and 1.0 * 1.0 * 1.0 meter in hard and less nutritious soil.

Sowing Method For apple cultivation, broadcast seeding is done by budding or planting method. Budding is best done in June and grafting in December to January.

Refining Process Before planting, treat the roots of plants with Dithane M-45.

Favorable Climate Apple is a fruit of temperate climate, it is a crop grown in cold and mountainous areas. In winter, 800 to 1200 hours of extreme frost i.e. temperature less than 7 degree centigrade is suitable for flowering and fruit formation.

Earth For apple orchards, loamy land with proper drainage is suitable. It should be slightly acidic and deep for proper growth and development of apple plants. Whose pH value between 5 to 6.5 is suitable. Keep in mind that there should not be any hard rock up to a depth of at least 1.5 to 2 meters.

Farm Preparation For apple orchards, 2-3 good deep plowing of the field should be done, at the time of last plowing, make the field friable, level and weed free.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers At the time of apple plantation, 10 kg decomposed manure should be given at the rate of 1 kg neem cake cake, 70 g nitrogen, 35 g phosphorus and 70 g potassium per tree per year for 10 years. The amount of manure and fertilizers is determined on the basis of soil test and variety.

Weed control To ensure that the apple plants remain straight, tie them to the stepney with the help of wood in the beginning. The farmers should remove the weeds growing in the thalas by regularly weeding and hoeing.

Irrigation First irrigation with transplanting and second irrigation after 7 to 8 days from last irrigation is necessary. This is a multi-year plant, so apple needs more irrigation, keep irrigating according to the need of the plant.

Pruning of Plants In apple cultivation, apple plants are generally given shape through pruning, so that sunlight can easily reach everywhere. According to this, the main stem is modified every year and allowed to grow up to 3 to 4 meters and later the co-branch is cut, this method has all the properties of protection from hail and snow.

Care during flowering Flowering of apple occurs in February-March. Also, according to different varieties, the fruits of apples start ripening from August to October. In order to prevent the apple fruits from falling prematurely, sufficient moisture should be kept in the plates. In addition, adequate amounts of boron and magnesium should be given.

Fruit Picking As soon as the fruit becomes fully developed, shapely and mature. The fruits should be plucked along with the stalk without delay. Also take care not to get hurt while plucking the fruit.

Yield The yield of apple depends on the climate, soil fertility and variety. On an average 100 to 180 kg of fruit can be obtained from a tree on scientific horticulture.
