Cluster Bean

Guar The scientific name of guar (cluster bean) is 'Saya motisus tetragonolobus' (Cyamopsis tetragonolobus). It is also known as Chatarfali in Madhya Pradesh (India). It is also mostly used as animal feed. Feeding guar to the animals gives them strength and the milking capacity of milch animals increases. Gum is also made from guar, this 'guar gum' is used in many products.
Delicious vegetables are made from guar pods. Guar also has a special contribution in pulse crops. This crop is taken in the states of Rajasthan, Gujarat, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh etc. The state of Rajasthan is leading in terms of area and production of guar in India.The guar or cluster bean, with the botanical name Cyamopsis tetragonoloba, is an annual legume and the source of guar gum. It is also known as gavar, gawar, or guvar bean.The origin of Cyamopsis tetragonoloba is unknown, since it has never been found in the wild


Crop Variety :- Durgajay, Durgapura Safed, Guar Kranti (RGC-1031)

Seed Quantity:- For seed production - 12 to 15 kg per hectare.

Sowing time: It is cultivated for fodder as well as grain purpose, complete sowing from mid-July to mid-August.

Seed Treatment :- The seeds are immersed in hot water at 58C for 10 minutes and then dried at room temperature before sowing. It kills all fungal mycelium and inactivates their spores to spread disease in the crop. After that treat seed with 3 gm Thiram per kg of seed. And seed treatment with Rhizobium culture is beneficial.

Sowing Method: Distance
Row to Row - 45 cm (normal) and 30 cm (late sowing)
Plant to plant - 15-20 cm.

Depth :- Seeds should be planted 2-3 cm apart. Sow at the depth off.

Harvest period: The growing season ranges from 60-90 days (determinate varieties) to 120-150 days (indeterminate varieties).

Earth :- Guar crop can be taken in almost all types of land but it is not suitable for alkaline and water stagnation. Gives best results when grown on well drained sandy loamy soils. This crop can be grown successfully in irrigated, irrigated and very less rainfed areas. Guar cultivation can be done easily on lightly alkaline and saline land whose pH value ranges from 7.5 to 8.5.

Land preparation: The field should be left after one or two deep ploughings in the month of May. With the first rain of monsoon, the field should be prepared by doing one or two ploughings. After the last ploughing, patta must be planted so that the soil moisture is conserved. In this way, weeds grow less in the prepared field. Also, there is more accumulation of rain water.

Favorable Climate :- Sowing temperature
28-30 degrees.
harvesting temperature
30-35 degrees.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers:- While preparing the field before sowing of guar, compost manure or well decomposed cow dung should be mixed well in the soil at the rate of 20-25 t/ha. Generally, guar crop requires less fertilizers as it is a pulse crop. For better production of guar, 20-25 kg nitrogen, 40-50 kg phosphorus, 20 kg sulfur have been recommended by the scientists. Keep in mind that the necessary nutrients should be given only on the basis of soil test.

Harmful Pests and Diseases and Prevention

Harmful Pests:-
Sucking insects - Jassid, Aphid, White fly etc.
For its prevention, spray Dimethoate 30 EC, 1.7 ml per liter of water or Imidachloroprid, 0.2 ml per liter of water and make second spray after 10 to 12 days.
Termites- For its control, mix chlorpyriphos or phorate 10g or quinalphos 1.5% full at the rate of 25 kg per hectare in the soil well before sowing.

Harmful diseases:
Root rot - For its prevention, seed treatment should be done before sowing.
Anthracnose and Alternaria leaf spot - For the prevention of this disease, 2 kg of Dithane M-45 (0.2%) in one thousand liters of water at an interval of 15 days. Spraying should be done according to the active component/ha.
Bacterial blight - For its prevention, spray copper oxychloride by making a solution of 2.5 grams per liter of water.

Weed control :- Keep the field clean by weeding and weeding during the initial growth of the crop. Pendimethalin 750 ml for chemical weed control. Spraying of M/s in 200 Ltr of water per acre within 24 hours of sowing.

Irrigation :- Generally, crops sown in July do not require irrigation. But in case of no rain, one irrigation must be done at the time of pod formation.

Harvesting :- It is harvested in late October and early November. The crop sown for use as fodder should be harvested at the time of flowering. Plow the cultivar crop in the form of green manure in the field only at the time of legumes. The pods start bearing 60-90 days after sowing depending on the variety of guar. Harvesting of green beans should be done at an interval of 10 to 12 days.For seed preparation, the harvested crop should be harvested after the pods are ripe. The ripe crop should be cut with the help of a drati and left to dry in the sun for a few days. After this the grains should be separated with the help of cavity or thrasher.

Production capability :- The average yield of guar seed in India is about 10 to 17 q/ha. For fodder crop should be harvested after flowering or at the early stage of pod formation (50 to 85 days after sowing). 250 to 300 quintals of green fodder per hectare is obtained from Guar crop. In this way, maximum profit can be earned from guar crop by adopting advanced farming techniques.
