Fennel

Fennel- As you know, fennel is a major commercial and spice crop. Fennel is commercially cultivated as an annual herb. Its grains are small in size and green in colour. Fennel is used in making pickles and to increase aroma and flavor in vegetables. Fennel also has medicinal properties. It is used for digestion, treatment of constipation, diarrhoea, sore throat and headache.
Oil is also extracted from fennel seeds. Fennel is mainly cultivated as a spice, it is mainly cultivated in Gujarat, Madhya Pradesh, Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Punjab and Haryana.

Fennel is a perennial, pleasant-smelling herb with yellow flowers. It is native to the Mediterranean, but is now found throughout the world. Dried fennel seeds are often used in cooking as an anise-like spice. Fennel's dried ripe seeds and oil are used to make medicine. Fennel is used by mouth for excessive crying in infants (colic), indigestion (dyspepsia), menstrual cramps (dysmenorrhea), and symptoms of menopause, but there is no good scientific evidence to support these uses. In foods and beverages, fennel oil and fennel seed are used as flavoring agents. In other manufacturing processes, fennel oil is used as a flavoring agent in certain laxatives, and as a fragrance component in soaps and cosmetics.

Fresh fennel bulb is a good source of vitamin C, a water-soluble vitamin critical for immune health, tissue repair, and collagen synthesis ( 2 ). Vitamin C also acts as a potent antioxidant in your body, protecting against cellular damage caused by unstable molecules called free radicals ( 3 )


Time of sowing In Kharif, fennel can be sown in the month of July and in Rabi season, it can be sown from the last week of October to the first week of November.

Distance In the method of transplanting in lines, the line to line distance should be 60 cm and the plant to plant distance should be 45 cm.

Sowing method Sowing of fennel is done in two ways. The first is done by sprinkling and the second by transplanting in lines.

Seed Quantity For sowing fennel seeds, 8-10 kg seed is required for direct sowing and 3-4 kg seed per hectare is required in planting method.

Seed Treatment Before sowing the seed, fennel seed should be treated with Trichoderma organic fungicide @ 8 to 10 g/kg seed after treatment for eight hours with the fungicide Carbendazim or Captan @ 2.5 to 3 g/kg of seed.

Favorable Climate Dry and cool climate is best for good production of fennel. The optimum temperature for germination of seeds is 20 to 29C and the crop grows well at 15 to 20C. The temperature above 25C inhibits the growth of the crop. Due to prolonged cloudiness in the sky at the time of flowering or ripening of the crop and high humidity in the air, the chances of outbreak of scorching disease and mahu pest increase.

Land Selection Fennel can be cultivated in almost all types of soil except sandy soil. For its good yield, loamy loamy soil containing organic matter is suitable. For its good yield, loamy loamy soil containing organic matter is suitable. It grows well even in well drained lime soils. The pH of the soil should be from 6.5 to 8.

Farm Preparation While preparing the field for the cultivation of fennel, first plowing should be done with soil turning plow and after 3 to 4 ploughings with indigenous plow or cultivator, make the field level by leveling it and making it uniform. At the time of last plowing of cultivation, 4-5 tonnes of decomposed cow dung should be mixed with 4-5 tonnes per acre and rake should be done so that the manure gets mixed well in the soil.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers At the time of field preparation, apply well decomposed cow dung at 4-5 tonnes per acre. Apply nitrogen 20 kg, two to three times in equal quantity. First nitrogen is applied as initial dose and rest nitrogen is applied after 30 or 60 days of sowing. Apply phosphorus manure on the basis of soil test. Use it only if the test fails.

Weed control To control weeds, weeding should be done from time to time as per the requirement.

Irrigation Irrigate before sowing for good germination. Apply first irrigation after 10-15 days of sowing. Irrigate at 15-25 days interval depending on soil type and climate. Do not allow the crop to be short of water at the time of flowering and seed formation. Drip irrigation can be adopted for irrigation of fennel crop. This method consumes less water. Due to this method of irrigation, the required amount of water reaches the plants.

Harvest Harvest when the clusters turn green to light yellow in color. It is harvested by breaking bunches. After that they are dried in the sun for 1-2 days and kept in the shade for 8-10 days.

Storage Fennel should be stored in such a way that it can be protected from moisture. Before storing the sacks in the godowns, wooden beams should be installed at the bottom. The distance of the sacks from the wall is 50-60 cm. Should keep Pesticides should never be used. Insects should be protected by giving smoke according to the opinion of experts. To store for a long time, it should be filled in a plastic bag and stored in a safe place. It is also kept in cold storage at commercial level.

Production By adopting the above advanced methods of agriculture, an average yield of 15 to 23 quintals per hectare of fennel is obtained. Whereas the yield of Lucknowi fennel is 5 to 8 quintals per hectare.
