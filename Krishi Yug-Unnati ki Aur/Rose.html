Rose

Rose- As you know rose is a beautiful and beautiful flower given by nature. People like it more because of its attractive texture, beautiful shape, captivating color. The red rose is also considered a symbol of love. By cultivating roses scientifically, flowers can be taken almost throughout the year. Generally it is a winter flower. In this season, the shade of rose flower is made on sight. Its one flower has from 5 petals to many petals. It is available in many varieties and different colours, whose demand is increasing day by day in the markets.

A rose is a woody perennial flowering plant of the genus Rosa, in the family Rosaceae, or the flower it bears. There are over three hundred species and tens of thousands of cultivars.

They form a group of plants that can be erect shrubs, climbing, or trailing, with stems that are often armed with sharp prickles.Their flowers vary in size and shape and are usually large and showy, in colours ranging from white through yellows and reds. Most species are native to Asia, with smaller numbers native to Europe, North America, and northwestern Africa. Species, cultivars and hybrids are all widely grown for their beauty and often are fragrant. Roses have acquired cultural significance in many societies.

Rose plants range in size from compact, miniature roses, to climbers that can reach seven meters in height. Different species hybridize easily, and this has been used in the development of the wide range of garden roses.


Variety Selection Select the best variety according to the place where it is planted. Each crop has a different variety for different locations. There are also many different varieties of roses, which should be selected correctly according to the field.

Plant preparation time and method
Method:- Rose is cultivated by T-budding method. In this method, wild rose cuttings are planted in June or July.
Distance: - After grafting, these cuttings are planted at a distance of 15 cm in the bed. After that the branches that emerge are removed. After this, a good quality rose twig is attached to it and it is tied tightly to the top with polythene.
Time: - The soil mixed with fertilizer remains filled in polythene, after some time the twig emerges in it. By the month of August these plants are ready for planting.

Method of planting plants in the soil Plant the rose plant about 15 cm above the ground. Cut and remove the polythene while planting the plant, while removing the polythene, the soil filled in the polythene should not be broken. After this, press the soil of the field well from all sides. Irrigate it immediately after transplanting.

Favorable Climate Cold and dry climate is suitable for rose cultivation. Rose is cultivated in the plains and hilly areas of North and South India during winters. For this, the day temperature is around 25 to 30 degree centigrade and the night temperature is 12 to 14 degree centigrade.

Land Selection It is cultivated on almost every type of soil. But humus-rich loam, sandy loam or matiyar loam are considered suitable. Whose pH value is 5.5 to 6.5. A place with good drainage is best for the growth of plants. Plants do not grow properly in shady places.

Farm Preparation Before planting, two or three good deep plowing of the field should be done, in the last plowing the field should be prepared friable, level and weed free with good drainage.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers For good yield of roses, vermi compost or well-rotted manure should be mixed well in the soil while preparing the field. Use chemical fertilizers only on the basis of soil test.

Weed control To prevent weeds in rose cultivation, weeding and hoeing is very important. By weeding, the roots get proper amount of air, due to which the growth of the plant is better. And do weeding and hoeing as required.

Irrigation Irrigate immediately after planting, irrigating continuously to give moisture to the new pen. Irrigate weekly in winter and 4-5 days in summer.

Plucking Flowers When one or two petals of the flower bloom, cut the flower and separate it from the plant. For this, a sharp knife or blade can be used. It is imperative to place the flower in a pot filled with water immediately after cutting it.

Storage After this, keep it in cold storage, where the temperature should be around 2 to 10 degrees. After this, after grading the flowers, send them to the market.

Production The farmers get a lot of profit from the cultivation of roses. Its cultivation starts giving flowers in about four months. About 30 to 40 kg of flowers are found on one acre of land.

Profit About 200 to 300 quintals of flowers can be obtained from one acre in a year. Whose annual income can reach about 15 lakhs.
