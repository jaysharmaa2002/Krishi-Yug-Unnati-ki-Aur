Isabgol

Isabgol- Isabgol is an extremely important medicinal crop. It ranks first in the export of medicinal crops. After investing 10 to 15 thousand from Isabgol cultivation, farmers are earning 2.5 to 3 lakh rupees in three to four months. Business related to cultivation of medicinal plants has always been a great opportunity,In which maximum profit can be earned by investing minimum money and among these businesses is the cultivation of Isabgol and its related business. In India, it is mainly produced in Gujarat, Rajasthan, Punjab, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, Chhattisgarh and Madhya Pradesh in about 50 thousand hectares. Neemuch, Ratlam, Mandsaur, Ujjain and Shajapur districts are prominent in Madhya Pradesh.

Isabgol also known as Psyllium husk is derived from the seeds of the plant Plantago ovata. It has its roots in the Sanskrit words "asp" and "ghol," together meaning "horse flower". The plant has long and narrow leaves and contains around 70% soluble fibre and 30% insoluble fiber. It is a herb that is native to Asia, the Mediterranean region and North Africa. Known as a common remedy for effective weight loss and clearing out the toxins from the digestive tract, isabgol has a myriad list of health benefits to offer

Isabgol is a good remedy that has been used as a laxative since ages to relieve constipation. The insoluble fiber present in Isabgol causes the stool to soften and expand, thereby improving gut movement. Add two teaspoons of Isabgol in a glass of warm milk and consume it before you go to bed every night for a few weeks.


Crop Variety G1 (Gujarat-1), G1 (Gujarat-1), G2 (Gujarat-2) G2 (Gujarat-2), TS-1-10 (TS 1-10), EC-124345, Niharika (Nebula), Haryana Isabgol 1-5 (Haryana Isabgol 1-5), Jawahar Isabgol-4, (Jawahar Isabgol-4)

Time of sowing Early sowing of Isabgol Last week of October to second week of November is considered ideal time for sowing of Isabgol. Delay in sowing beyond the first week of December saw yield reduction.

Seed Quantity (seeds/acre) Seed quantity 4-5 kg/ha.

Sowing method The seeds are very small, so by mixing equal quantity of sand in it, they can be sown in a row or by sprinkling method.

Distance Row to row distance is 30 cms. And the distance from plant to plant is 4 - 5 cm. Should keep

Depth The seed should not fall deeper than one to two centimeters.

Seed Treatment Seed should be treated with Kaptan or Thiram or Mancozeb fungicide at the rate of 2.5 g/kg seed to prevent seed borne disease root rot.

Favorable climate Isabgol is a cool season rabi crop, and requires dry sunny weather during its maturity season. Even light rain and cloudy sky can lead to seed shortages and reduced yield or production.

Earth For the cultivation of Isabgol, well drained black soil or loam or sandy loam soil whose pH is less. The value 7 - 8 is suitable. The sandy loam soil in which the amount of fossils is high is considered best.

Farm preparation For abundant production in the cultivation of Isabgol, it is necessary to prepare the land well. For this, first plowing should be done with soil turning plow and after 2-3 plowing with cultivator, the field should be made fine. After this, level the field by making the soil finely by applying a pat. After this, it is necessary to have proper moisture in the field before sowing for good germination. Therefore, it is good to sow the field after getting it washed.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers Isabgol crop should be mainly used without chemical fertilizers and pesticides. Because it is a medicinal crop, organic manure like, farm yard manure (FYM), vermi-compost, green manure etc. can be used. Chemical fertilizers should be used as per requirement, 30 kg/acre and 25 kg/acre urea is applied.

Weed control To prevent weeds in the crop of Isabgol, do two-three weeding and hoeing so that weeds do not harm the crop.

Irrigation The germination of Isabgol seeds starts after a week. Give second irrigation after 3 to 4 weeks and third irrigation at the time of formation of spikes. Generally, Isabgol crop requires a total of 3 to 4 irrigations in its entire growth period. Isabgol crop takes about 110 to 120 days to mature.

Storage time It takes about 3 to 4 months for the Isabgol crop to mature.

Harvest Harvesting of Isabgol should be done carefully because there is a danger of rain at the time of harvesting Isabgol crop, farmers should do it only after knowing the weather before harvesting Isabgol crop, otherwise there can be a lot of damage, the crop is on the upper side where flowers and The fruits are ripe, they should be cut and sifted, after that in a day or two they should be taken out with the help of thresher machine.

Yield rate The yield of Isabgol depends on many factors like variety, climate, soil and other crop management practices, but on an average, 4 - 6 quintals of good crop can be obtained on one acre of land.
