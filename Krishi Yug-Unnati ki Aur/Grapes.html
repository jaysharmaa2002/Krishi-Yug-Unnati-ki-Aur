Grapes

Grapes- Grapes are a fruit. Grapes are a powerful and beautifying fruit. Grapes are considered the best among fruits. It is a very famous crop of the world and is grown commercially in most of the countries. It is an evergreen vine whose leaves shed once a year. Grapes are rich in water, sugar, sodium, potassium, citric acid, fluoride, potassium sulfate, magnesium and iron.

A grape is a fruit, botanically a berry, of the deciduous woody vines of the flowering plant genus Vitis. Grapes can be eaten fresh as table grapes, used for making wine, jam, grape juice, jelly, grape seed extract, vinegar, and grape seed oil, or dried as raisins, currants and sultanas. Grapes are a non-climacteric type of fruit, generally occurring in clusters.

Grapes are a type of fruit that grow in clusters of 15 to 300, and can be crimson, black, dark blue, yellow, green, orange, and pink. "White" grapes are actually green in color, and are evolutionarily derived from the purple grape. Mutations in two regulatory genes of white grapes turn off production of anthocyanins, which are responsible for the color of purple grapes. Anthocyanins and other pigment chemicals of the larger family of polyphenols in purple grapes are responsible for the varying shades of purple in red wines. Grapes are typically an ellipsoid shape resembling a prolate spheroid.

Raw grapes are 81% water, 18% carbohydrates, 1% protein and have negligible fat. A 100gm(3+1/2 ounce) reference amount of raw grapes supplies 288 kilojoules (69 kilocalories) of food energy and a moderate amount of vitamin K(14% of the Daily value), With no other micronuterients in the significant content.

Sowing Time Planting is done from October to January. Rare planting is also done during June-July where monsoon arrives late.

Sowing

  • Plant dormant, bare root grape vines in early spring.
  • Most grape varieties are self-fertile. To be sure, ask when you are buying the vines if you will need more than one plant for pollination.
  • Select a site with full sun. If you don't have a spot with full sun, make sure it gets at least morning sun. A small amount of afternoon shade didn't hurt. Your soil needs to be deep, well-drained and loose. You also need good air circulation.
  • The grape vines have to be trained for some sort of training to grow upwards. This will also reduce the risk of disease. Planting requires support.
  • Before planting grapes, soak their roots in water for two or three hours.
  • Space the vines to 6 to 10 feet (16 feet of muscadine).
  • For each vine, dig a planting hole 12 inches deep and 12 inches wide. Fill with 4 inches of topsoil. Cut off broken roots and set the vine slightly deeper in the hole than it was growing in the nursery. Cover the roots with 6 inches of soil and mulch down. Fill in with the rest of the soil, but don't till it down.
  • Give water at the time of planting.

Seed treatment The seeds are refrigerated at 2 - 4 C for at least three months. We start using them after three months of cold treatment. After cold treatment, grape seeds are soaked in distilled water for 24 hours. Then we washed the seeds thoroughly to remove any residual pieces of pulp and skin.

Earth The best soil types for grape cultivation are dry loamy, loamy loamy soil rich in organic matter. Do not use soil that has low drainage.

Farm preparation For the cultivation of grapes, in the beginning, remove the remnants of old crops present in the field and do deep plowing of the field. After that do two to three good slant plowing in the field through cultivator. After plowing, level the field by putting a pad.

Climate The ideal climate for growing grapes is the Mediterranean climate. In their natural habitat, vines flower and produce during hot and dry periods.

Fertilizers and Manures Grapes basically need nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, calcium, magnesium, sulphur, iron, manganese, zinc, copper and boron. Use chemical fertilizers only on the basis of soil test.

Weed Control The weeds between the rows of grape vines are removed by tractor operated means. Within rows, weeds are removed by hand with a shovel. Sometimes weed killers are sprayed on the weeds after growing like Glyphosate @ 2.0 kg/ha or Paraquat @ 7.5 kg/ ha. Keep in mind, avoid spraying chemical weedicide on the leaves of grapes.

Irrigation Grapes are strictly perennial crop and regularly irrigated. For flood irrigation, 5-7 days during summer, during winter and 15-20 days in rainy season - interval is maintained for drip irrigation, 40-50, 30-40, 20-30 liters of water per day. Bell, water is planted.

Harvesting Time The harvesting season usually falls between August and October in the Northern Hemisphere and February and April in the Southern Hemisphere. With different climatic conditions, grape varieties, and wine styles, the harvesting of grapes can occur anywhere in the world every month of the calendar year.

Production rate The average yield obtained from a vineyard is about 20 to 33 tonnes per hectare in the second or third year of planting. The yield increases in subsequent years. The life of a grape is 15 years and it is expected to produce and harvest during the entire life span.

Cleaning and Drilling Cleaning involves separating the dried fruit, removing the stems and foreign material, and rinsing off-grade raisins inside. As water is used to eliminate foreign materials such as dust and sandy soil through multiple washings, this can lead to further re-hydration.
