Peach

Peach-Peach is a fruit-bearing deciduous tree. It is successfully cultivated in the mountainous and sub-mountainous parts of India. Fresh fruits are eaten and fruits are used to make jams, jellies and chutneys. The amount of sugar in the fruit is sufficient. Peach kernel oil is used to make a variety of cosmetic products and medicines.It is rich in iron, fluoride and potassium. Peach cultivation in India is being done successfully mainly in the high valleys of Punjab, Uttaranchal, Himachal Pradesh, Jammu and Kashmir and the plains of North India.

Raising of root stock: For raising root stock, wild peach is propagated through seeds. Seeds are kept in moist sand for about 10-12 weeks for stratification. The germination and vigour of the seedling can be improved by pre-sowing treatment of seeds with thiourea (5g/l water), GA3 (200mg/l water) or IBA (100 mg/l water). The seeds are sown in beds about 5 cm deep and 15 cm apart at a row to row spacing of 20 cm in the month of October-November. Beds are mulched with dry grass followed by light irrigation.

Grafting: Tongue grafting in the month of November is commercial method of multiplication. A smooth slanting cut of 4-5 cm is made on the one year old rootstock at about 15-20 cm above the ground level and another downward cut is given starting approximately 2/3rd from the top of the slanting cut and about 2 cm in length. This formed a tongue like structure on the stock. Similar cut is also made on the lower side of the scion exactly matching the cut given on the rootstock. The scion of 20-25 cm length having 2-3 buds of previous season growth is then fitted lightly with the rootstock and tied with polythene strips. The union completed within 30-45 days and after that polythene strip is also removed to avoid girdling.


Time of sowing Ring budding is done in the month of April or May on the root stalk of peach. The plant prepared by this method is ready for planting in the main field by the month of December-January.

Distance A distance of 6 x 6 meters is kept between the plants prepared on the root stalk of peach.

Seed Depth For planting the tree, the peach seeds are 5 cm apart. deep and 12-16 cm. Keep the distance between the plants.

Sowing Method For sowing, grafting method is used initially and then transplanting is done in the main field.

Seed quantity The amount of plants for transplanting peaches is suitable for the proper spacing and location.

Favorable Climate The climate should not be too cold and not too hot for the cultivation of peach. And this crop requires a temperature of less than 7 degree Celsius for certain period of time.

Land selection The best soil for the cultivation of peach is sandy loam, but it should be deep and well drained. The pH value of the soil should be from 5.5 to 6.5, as well as it should be rich in flora. It is successfully cultivated in the mountainous and sub-mountainous parts of India.

Farm Preparation For peach cultivation or horticulture, the first plowing of the field should be done with a soil-reversing plow, after 2 to 3 ploughings should be done with a horizontal slant or other machine, after which the cultivation should be made level. And 15 to 20 days before transplanting or sowing, dig pits of 1 x 1 x 1 meter and leave it in the sun.

Fertilizers and chemical Fertilizers For good growth and development of peach plant, organic manure and dung manure is required. After filling these pits with a mixture of 125 g nitrogen, 50 g phosphorus, 100 potash and 25 ml chlorpyriphos, give irrigation to the pits prepared for cultivation of peach. And the amount of manure should be given every year in increasing order.

Weed control To control weeds, weeding should be done from time to time as per the requirement.

Irrigation Watering should be done immediately after transplanting peach plants. The crop needs irrigation at the time of flower germination, cutting stage, fruit development. Drip irrigation method is very beneficial for irrigation in peach cultivation.

Harvest The main harvesting time for the peach crop is from April to May. Their bright color and soft flesh show signs of ripening. Peach is harvested by shaking the tree.

Storage After harvesting they are stored at normal temperature and used for making squash etc.

Production According to the cultivation of peach, the yield of peach is 90 to 150 quintals per hectare under normal conditions.
