Custard Apple

Custard Apple- Sitaphal (Sharifa) is a very tasty and sweet fruit. Which is also known as the fruit of the poor. Sitaphal (Sharifa) It is basically found in the forests and is found on the ridges of the fields etc. This fruit is found in the forests of the states of Madhya Pradesh, Bihar, Jharkhand, Uttar Pradesh, Andhra Pradesh and Assam.The origin of cilantro is originally considered to be tropical America, so the people there call it as Custard Apple. And also called Sugar Apple.

Citrus fruits are mainly consumed fresh, as they have a rich, creamy, sweet taste. They are very tasty, nutritious, rich in sugar, protein and phosphorus with a highly digestible pulp. They are also used in juices, sorbets, desserts, wine and ice cream recipes.Powdered dried raw fruits, seeds and leaves are used as insecticides. The leaves, stems and seeds contain fibre, oil and various alkaloids.

Custard apple is a common name for a fruit, and the tree which bears it, Annona reticulata. The fruits vary in shape, heart-shaped, spherical, oblong or irregular. The size ranges from 7 to 12 cm (2.8 to 4.7 in), depending on the cultivar. When ripe, the fruit is brown or yellowish, with red highlights and a varying degree of reticulation, depending again on the variety.

The flesh varies from juicy and very aromatic to hard with an astringent taste. The flavor is sweet and pleasant, akin to the taste of 'traditional' custard. The custard apple is native to the Americas, but has been found on the island of Timor as early as 1000 CE


Time of sowing Planting should be done in July-August or February-March.

Seed quantity An estimated 350-400 saplings can be planted in one hectare.

Seeding Method The best way to plant it is to plant the seeds in polythene bags filled with soil and when the plants are ready, then separate the polythene bags at the bottom.

Before Planting 60 x 60 x 60 cm for cilantro plant during summer. Pits of size 5 x 5 m. are prepared at a distance. After keeping these pits open for 15 days, 5-10 kg in top soil. Decomposed manure of cow dung, 500 grams cake of Karanj and 50 grams of NPK. The mixture should be mixed well. After this, press the pit well and make a plate around it and give water. If there is no rain, then irrigation of the plants on 3-4 days is good for plant establishment.

How to Plant Plant the plant along with the pindi in the prepared pit in the garden.

Land Selection Sitaphal can be cultivated in all types of soil. But loamy soil with good drainage is suitable for its growth and production. Its yield is good even in weak and rocky soil. pH of soil The value should be between 5.5 and 7.

Climate Areas with hot and dry climate, where there is no frost, are more suitable for the plant of Sitaphal. Its crop is damaged in frosty areas.

Farm Preparation Plow the field properly and make the field friable, level and weed free.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers Sitaphal trees give fruits every year, so for good yield, rotten dung manure and chemical fertilizers should be given in proper quantity. In a fully grown tree of Sitaphal, 20 kg. Cow dung, 40 g. Nitrogen, 60 g. phosphorus and 60 g. Potash should be given per tree per year. And the essential nutrients of the soil should be given on the basis of soil test.

Weed Control Weeding should be done from time to time by plowing once in the month of August-September, which will eliminate weeds and grass and conserve moisture in the areas where water is scarce. Pruning should be done to give proper structure to new plants for 3 years.

Irrigation Watering should be done immediately after planting. Watering of cilantro plants is necessary in summer. Therefore, irrigation should be done at an interval of 15 days at this time. After one or two irrigations after the end of rain, the size of the fruit becomes large.

Fruit Picking The fruits of Sitaphal (Sharifa) should be taken only when they are somewhat hard, because they burst if left on the tree for a long time. Therefore, suitable stage should be selected for its harvesting. For this, when the space between the two bulges on the fruit increases and their color changes, then it should be understood that the fruit is in the stage of ripening. Raw fruits should not be broken because these fruits do not ripen properly and the amount of sweetness also decreases from them.

Production It starts bearing fruit after three years of planting. A 4-5 year old plant produces 50-70 fruits whereas a full grown plant gives up to 100 fruits.
