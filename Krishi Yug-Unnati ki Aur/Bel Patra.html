Bel Patra

Bel Patra-Bel or Belpathar, Bilva, Belpatra, is a fruit tree native to India. Bael is one of the ancient fruits of India. Bel tree is considered sacred in Hinduism. The history of this tree is also found in the Vedic period. Bael is known as 'Shriphal' since ancient times. The leaves of this tree are traditionally used to make offerings to Lord Shiva. The fruits of the bael are 5-17 cm in diameter. Their light green shell is hard and smooth. turns green to golden yellow when cooked.On breaking of which sweet fibrous fragrant pulp comes out. This pulp contains many small, large seeds. Bael is a fruit rich in nutritional (Vitamin-A, BC, minerals, carbohydrates) and medicinal properties, which can be grown in different types of barren land (usar, ravine, khadar, arid and semi-arid). Many preservatives (sharbat, marmalade) can be made from it.Apart from India, its trees grow naturally in southern Nepal, Sri Lanka, Myanmar, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Vietnam, Laos, Cambodia and Thailand. Apart from this, it is cultivated all over India as well as in Sri Lanka, Northern Malay Peninsula, Java and Philippines and Fiji Archipelago.


Time of sowing The best time for sowing is the month of May-June.

Sowing method Bael plants are mainly formed by kallikayana or grafting on the root stem prepared by seed.

Planting Method For planting saplings, pits of 3x3x3 feet should be dug in the field in the month of April-May and the pits should be left open so that they get good sunlight and the pits should be free from underground insects. And at the time of planting, the pits should be filled with 3-4 baskets of rotted cow dung, 20-25 kg. Mixing sand and 1 kg of lime should be filled up to a height of 6-8 inches.

Distance Bael trees are planted at a distance of 6-8 meters in the month of July-August.

Planting time The vine plant should be planted in the prepared pits in the month of July-August.

Seed Treatment The seeds are immersed in water for about 12 hours after which they are sown directly in the field or in the nursery.

Favorable climate Vine is a plant of sub-tropical climate. It can grow at an altitude of 4000 feet above sea level, where the temperature rises to 49 degree centigrade in summer and falls to -7 degree centigrade in winter.

Land selection Bael tree can be grown in many types of soil, it can also be easily grown in swampy, alkaline, stony soil. For this the pH of the soil. The value should be 5-8.

farm preparation For vine cultivation, clean the land thoroughly to make it weed free. Plow his field well. After that the pits should be dug.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers For good production in vine cultivation, 10 kg should be given to one year old plant. In cow dung manure or vermicompost and chemical fertilizers, 500 grams of nitrogen, 250 grams of phosphorus, 500 grams of potash should be applied per tree per year. Manure and fertilizers should be applied in full quantity in the month of June-July.

Weed control To control weeds, weeding should be done as needed.

Irrigation One or two years of irrigation is required for good growth of new plants. Established plants can live well without irrigation.

Fruit Picking When the color of the fruits changes from dark green to yellowish green, then harvest the fruit by 2 cm. Should be done with the stalk. While plucking, care should be taken that the fruits should not fall on the ground. Due to this the skin of the fruit gets cracked, due to which the fruit rots from inside.

Storage The fruits of the bael should be stored in a dry place free from moisture.

Use Bael is a beneficial fruit, so its maximum use should be promoted. Dysentery, loss of appetite and other stomach disorders can be relieved by eating raw fruits in marmalade and candy, or roasting them. And it is also used in making sherbet, jam, taffy, bael powder.
