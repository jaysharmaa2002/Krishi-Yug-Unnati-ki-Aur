Sunflower

Sunflower Sunflower, the name "Helianthus" is derived from 'helios' meaning 'sun' and 'anthus' meaning 'flower'. It is called a sunflower because it follows the sun, always towards its direct rays. It is an important oilseed crop of the country. Sunflower oil is most popular because of its light colour, bland flavour, high smoke point and high level of linoleic acid which is good for heart patients.
Sunflower seeds contain about 48- 53 percent edible oil. It has been grown in India after the shortage of edible oil in 1969-70. Sunflower has a special place in oilseed crops. It is also a special oilseed crop in our country after groundnut, mustard, rapeseed and soybean.Sunflower is one of the most important oil seed crop grown in temperate countries. It is a major source of vegetable oil in the world. In India it has gained popularity due to the national priority of vegetable oil production. India is one of the largest producers of oilseed crop in the world.


There are many varieties of sunflowers.
Sunflower is mainly divided into two species.
1. Complex species- Surya, Jwalamukhi, Mardan, MSFH 4.
2. Hybrid Species - KV SH1, SH-3322, FSH-17, Kaveri 618.

Time of sowing Sowing of this crop is done in Kharif season from 15-25 July, in Rabi season from 20 October to 10 November and Zaid crop from 20 February to 10 March.

Seed quantity Use seed rate of 2-3 kg/acre for sunflower sowing. Seed rate for hybrid use is 2-2.5 kg/acre.

Seed treatment Before sowing for faster germination, soak the seeds in water for 24 hours and dry in shade. Then treat the seed with Thiram @ 2 gm/kg of seed. This will protect the seeds from soil borne pests and diseases. To protect the crop from swampy mildew, treat the seeds with Dhatalakshay@6gm/kg of seed. Treat the seed with Imidacloprid 5-6 ml/kg of seed.

Distance 60 cm in two rows and 30 cm between two plants. Keep a distance

Seed depth Seeds should be planted 4-5 cm apart. Sow at the depth of

Sowing method Sowing is done by digging a pit. Apart from this, seeds are made by making beds or bunds with the sowing machine.

Land selection
Sunflower crop is grown in different types of lands.
Sandy land is more favorable for this crop.
The pH value of the soil should be between 6-5 to 8.
Acidic lands are not suitable for sunflower cultivation. This crop tolerates mild alkalinity.

Farm preparation For the preparation of the field, a deep plowing is done with the soil turning plow. Grass, straw and stalks present in the field should be collected and burnt. Harrow should be run 2-3 times to make the soil of the field friable. After every plowing or harrow, it is necessary to plant a pad to conserve moisture in the land.

Amount of manure and fertilizer For sunflower crop, one month before sowing, 10 tonnes of decomposed cow dung should be applied per hectare. 80 kg for the cultivation of progressive castes. Nitrogen, 60 kg. Phosphorus and 40 kg potash should be used per hectare. For hybrid species, 150 kg nitrogen, 80 kg phosphorus and 70 kg potash should be used per hectare. Two third of the total quantity, nitrogen and complete phosphorus and potash should be used at the time of sowing.Remaining quantity of nitrogen should be used after 45 days. For growth of crop, take 19:19:19 spray at 5 gm/Ltr of water twice after 5-6 leaves and 8 days interval. 2 grams per liter at the time of flowering. Spray Boron. Keep in mind that chemical fertilizers should be used only on the basis of soil test.

Harmful pests and prevention Tobacco caterpillar: It is the main insect of sunflower and attacks in the month of April-May, they make their food on the leaves. Destroy the caterpillars along with the leaves. If infestation is observed, apply Fipronil SC 2ml. Spray at the rate of 1 liter of water. In case of infestation, take two sprays with an interval of 10 days or spinosad 5 ml. per 10 litres. Water or Nuvan + Indoxacarb 1 ml Take spray of this mixture per liter of water.
American caterpillar: This insect feeds on plants and grains. This causes mold and flowers to die. Its trunk is green to brown in color. To prevent this, apply 4 pheromone cards per acre. If danger increases, take spray of Carbaryl@1kg or Acephate@800gm or Chlorpyriphos @1Litre per acre in 100Ltr of water.
Hairy caterpillar: The caterpillar eats the leaves from the underside due to which the plants dry up. Its trunk is yellow in color and hair is black. Collect and destroy the snails. If attack is observed, apply Fipronil SC 2 ml. Take spray of this mixture per liter of water. In case of severe attack, take 2 sprays at 10 days interval or spinosad 5 ml. Spray it by mixing it in 10 liters of water.
Teela: Its attack occurs at the time of formation of eyes. This causes the leaves to curl and look burnt. If attack of sap-sucking insects is observed on 10-20% of the boots then take spray of Neem seed Karnal Xtrak@50gm/Ltr of water.

Diseases and Prevention Kungi: This disease causes loss of up to 20 percent of the yield. If rust infestation is observed, take spray of Tridemorph@1gm or Mancozeb@2gm/Ltr of water to control it. Second spray at 15 days interval or Hexaconazole 2 ml. Spraying of M/s per liter of water twice at an interval of 10 days.
Root rot: Plants with this disease become weak and mature early. The ash-coloured spots appear on the stem, after pollination, the plant suddenly dries up. To prevent this, after 30 days of sowing apply Trichoderma viride 1 kg mixed with 20 kg compost or sand. Also take spray of Carbendazim@1gm/Ltr of water.
Alternaria blight disease: This disease reduces seed and oil yield. Dark brown and black spots first appear on the lower leaves, which later reach the upper leaves. As the damage progresses, these spots also reach the top of the stem. If infestation is observed, take spray of Mancozeb@3gm/Litre of water four times at an interval of 10 days.
Bloating of flowers: Initially brown spots appear on the flowers. Later these spots enlarge and develop mold which eventually turns black. If its damage is observed at the time of flowering or formation, take spray of Mancozeb@2gm/Litre of water.

Weed control Weeds like Motha, Bathua, Krishnanil and Hirankhuri are found in the sunflower crop grown in Rabi season. For weed control in sunflower, 2 to 3 hoeing is necessary. The first weeding should be done 20 days after sowing the seeds. After which weeding should be done again after 15 to 20 days. And at the same time soil should be applied to the roots of the plants. - Pendimethalin 30 EC for chemical weed control. Appropriate amount should be sprayed in the field before sowing or after two days of sowing.

Irrigation Irrigation is not required for sunflower crop grown in Kharif season. If there is a lack of rain, one irrigation can be done in this season. Generally 3-4 irrigations are required for the crop grown in Rabi season. These irrigations should be done after 40, 60 and 90 days of sowing. - More water is required for the crop of Zayed season. A total of 6 to 7 irrigations have to be given.

Harvesting Harvest when sunflower leaves are dry and flowers turn yellow. Do not delay harvesting as this results in drop of leaves and increases the risk of termites. After plucking the flowers dry them for 2-3 days. The seeds are easily removed from the dried flowers. Threshing can be done with a machine.

Storage After threshing, dry the seeds before storage and the water content in them should be 9-10 percent. Its species, growing season and irrigation etc. have a special effect on the yield of sunflower. The yield of sunflower crop grown in Rabi season is 10-20 qtl/ha.
