Fenugreek

Fenugreek- Fenugreek is a vegetable whose plant is smaller than 1 foot. Its leaves are used to make greens and its grains are used as spices. It is very beneficial in terms of health. The fenugreek plant is a small plant with green leaves, small white flowers and pods that contain small, golden-brown seeds. This plant can grow up to 2-3 feet tall. Indians generally use fenugreek plant in vegetables or parathas.

Fenugreek seeds are golden-brown seeds of the fenugreek plant. The seeds are mainly used in cooking and in medicine. Rajasthan is the main fenugreek producing state in India. Other fenugreek producing states are Madhya Pradesh, Tamil Nadu, Rajasthan, Gujarat, Uttar Pradesh and Punjab.

Fenugreek is believed to have been brought into cultivation in the Near East. It is uncertain which wild strain of the genus Trigonella gave rise to domesticated fenugreek. Charred fenugreek seeds have been recovered from Tell Halal, Iraq (carbon dated to 4000 BC), and Bronze Age levels of Lachish and desiccated seeds from the tomb of Tutankhamen.Cato the Elder lists fenugreek with clover and vetch as crops grown to feed cattle.

In one first-century A.D. recipe, the Romans flavoured wine with fenugreek.[8] In the 1st century AD, in Galilee, it was grown as a staple food, as Josephus mentions it in his book, the Wars of the Jews. The plant is mentioned in the 2nd-century compendium of Jewish Oral Law (Mishnah) under its Hebrew name tiltan.


Improved varieties of Fenugreek To get maximum yield from fenugreek crop, farmers should give priority to improved varieties over local varieties. Some of the popular and good yielding varieties are as follows- Hisar Sonali, Hisar Suvarna, Hisar Mukta, AFG-1, 2 and 3, RMT-1 and 143, Rajendra Kranti, Co-1 and Pusa Kasuri. etc. are the major approved species.

Time of sowing The last week of October and the first week of November are the best time for sowing this crop.

Distance Use row to row spacing of 22.5 cm.

Seed depth 3-4 cm on the bed sow seeds at a depth of

Sowing method It is sown by hand sprinkling.

Seed Quantity For sowing in one acre field, use seeds of 12 kg per acre.

Crop Circle Kharif crops like paddy, maize, green moong and green fodder crops can be grown along with fenugreek.

Seed Treatment Soak the seeds in water for 8 to 12 hours before sowing. To protect the seeds from soil borne pests and diseases, treat the seeds with Thiram@4gm and Carbendazim 50%WP@3gm/kg of seed. After chemical treatment, treat seeds with Azospirillium 600 gm + Trichoderma viride 20 gm per acre per 12 kg of seeds.

Favorable Climate Fenugreek requires a cool climate for good growth and yield. This crop has some degree of frost tolerance.

Land Selection Fenugreek can be cultivated in all types of soils, but loamy and sandy soils are more suitable for this. It contains organic matter. pH of soil Value 6-7 is suitable for the growth and development of the crop. It can also be cultivated in heavy and less alkaline lands with proper drainage management.

Farm Preparation For the cultivation of fenugreek, 2 to 3 plows should be done on the land with cultivator or country plow. After every plowing, the field should be made level by applying a pat. At the time of last plowing apply well decomposed manure of 10-15 tonnes per acre. There should be moisture in the field at the time of sowing for proper germination.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers For fenugreek crop, apply well decomposed cow dung at the time of last plowing of 10-15 tonnes per acre. At the time of sowing apply 5 kg Nitrogen (12 kg Urea), 8 kg Potassium (50 kg Super Phosphate) per acre. Triacontanol hormone 20 ml after 15-20 days of germination for good growth. Spray per 10 liters of water. Spray of NPK (19:19:19) at 75gm/15Ltr of water after 20 days of sowing also helps in good and rapid growth.

Harmful diseases and prevention Buttermilk disease (powdery mildew)- For the control of disease, spray 500 liters solution of 0.1 percent carathane LC or 0.2 percent soluble sulfur at the rate of per hectare. If needed, it should be repeated after 10 to 15 days. Apart from this, 15 to 20 kg of sulfur powder should be sprinkled on the crop.
Powdery mildew- For the control of this disease, 400 to 500 liters of solution per hectare with 0.2 percent concentration of any one copper fungicide like blightox, phytolan, blue copper or Difolton should be sprayed. Spraying can be repeated after 10 to 15 days if required.
Root rot- To prevent this, seed should be sown after treating it with some fungicide like thyme or captan. Adoption of proper crop rotation, summer plowing etc. are also helpful in reducing the disease.

Harmful pests and prevention
Mahu (Aphid): If infestation is observed, take spray of Imidacloprid@3ml@10Ltr of water or Thiamethoxam@4gm/10Ltr of water.
Termites- For the control of termites, chlorpyphos should be mixed with water at the rate of 4 liters per hectare in the crop and given with irrigation.

Weed Control First weeding is done after 25-30 days of sowing and second weeding after 30 days of first sowing. To control weeds chemically, application of Fluchloralin@300gm/acre is recommended. Also take spray of Pendimethalin@1.3Ltr/acre with 200Ltr of water within 1-2 days of sowing if soil moisture remains inside. . When the plant reaches 4 inches high, tie it up to prevent it from disintegrating.

Irrigation In the beginning after sowing, moisture is good for the germination of seeds, so apply light irrigation. Irrigate before sowing for early germination of seeds. For proper yield of fenugreek, apply three to four irrigations before sowing. Water shortage should not be allowed at the time of pod development and seed development as it causes heavy loss in yield.

Harvesting Farmers growing fenugreek for the purpose of greens should start harvesting fenugreek after a month of sowing. Fenugreek crop gets ripe in 140-150 days. For grains, harvest it when the lower leaves turn yellow and fall and the pods turn yellow. Use a sickle for harvesting. After harvesting, tie the bundle of the crop and keep it in sunlight for 6-7 days. Sort it when it is dry, then clean and grading.

Storage The fenugreek seeds should be cleaned and dried well and filled in bags and kept in a ventilated moisture-free place.
