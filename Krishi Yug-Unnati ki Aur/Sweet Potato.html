Sweet Potato

Sweet Potato- Sweet Potato is a rich source of Vita Carotene, and is also used as an antioxidant and alcohol. It is a perennial vine, which has lobed or heart shaped leaves. It is cultivated in about 2 lakh hectares in India. Its major producing states are Bihar, West Bengal, Uttar Pradesh and Orissa. India ranks 6th in Sweet Potato production.

The sweet potato or sweetpotato (Ipomoea batatas) is a dicotyledonous plant that belongs to the bindweed or morning glory family, Convolvulaceae. Its large, starchy, sweet-tasting, tuberous roots are used as a root vegetable. The young shoots and leaves are sometimes eaten as greens. The sweet potato is distantly related to the common potato (Solanum tuberosum), both being in the order Solanales. Although the darker sweet potatoes are often referred to as "yams" in parts of North America, the species is not closely related to true yams.Cultivars of the sweet potato have been bred to bear tubers with flesh and skin of various colors.

Ipomoea batatas is native to the tropical regions of the Americas. Of the approximately 50 genera and more than 1,000 species of Convolvulaceae, I. batatas is the only crop plant of major importance-some others are used locally (e.g., I. aquatica "kangkong"), but many are poisonous. The genus Ipomoea that contains the sweet potato also includes several garden flowers called morning glories, though that term is not usually extended to Ipomoea batatas. Some cultivars of Ipomoea batatas are grown as ornamental plants under the name tuberous morning glory, used in a horticultural context.


Time of sowing In irrigated conditions, the crop rotation of sweet potato + paddy is adopted. Sow sweet potato crop in the month of December-January after the second harvesting of paddy.

Distance The distance between the rows is 60 cm. And the distance between the plants is 30 cm. keep of

Seed Depth Sowing of bales 20-25 cm. Sow at the depth.

Seed quantity For sowing in one acre, use 25,000-30,000 cut vines or 280-320 kg bales.

Seed Treatment Put the lumps in a plastic bag and soak them in high amount of sulfuric acid for 10-40 minutes.

Climate The temperature of 21 to 27 degree is considered suitable for the cultivation of sweet potato. Its crop is grown successfully in places with temperate and temperate climates, and it can be grown easily where there is an annual rainfall of 75 to 150 cm.

Earth Sweet potatoes can be grown in all types of soils. For this sandy and loamy soil is more favorable. But it gives better production in more fertile and well drained soil. It should not be cultivated in light sandy and heavy clay soils, because the development of knots is not good in it. For this the pH of the soil should be 5.8-6.7.

Farm Preparation For the cultivation of sweet potato, plow the field 2-3 times before sowing. The field should be prepared friable, level and weed free.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers To get good yield for the cultivation of sweet potato, following types of fertilizers and fertilizers should be given per hectare. Dung manure - 200-250 quintals per hectare, Nitrogen - 50 kg Phosphorus - 50 kg Potash - 50 kg.

Weed Control Cover the sweet potatoes with mulch of your choice to protect them from cold weather. This will stop the growth of weeds. Before germination of weeds apply Metribuzin 70% WP 200 gm or Elaclor 2 ltr/acre.

Irrigation In the beginning, sweet potatoes require a lot of water. Over time you can reduce the amount of water and then water only once a week. Initially, add water daily, then reduce it by one day every week.

Harvest period (Convolvulaceae - Convolvulaceae) is a one-year plant of the family, but it can behave like a multi-year-old under favorable conditions. It is a flowering plant. Its metamorphic root originates from the stems of the stem, which penetrates into the ground and swells and a large amount of carbohydrate is stored in those swollen roots. Root is red or brown in color and it stores food inside itself.

Harvesting Time Digging of sweet potato is done in November, when there is moisture in the field at the time of digging, it is convenient to dig.

Production Capability Sweet potato yield varies from variety to cultivar as the average yield is generally observed to be 15 to 25 tonnes per hectare.

Cleaning and Drying Cover the sweet potatoes with mulch of your choice to protect them from cold weather. This will stop the weeds from growing, the vines will not grow very much, and the energy will be focused on the production of the tubers. After cutting the vines from the nursery, they should be kept in shade for two days, due to which the development of roots is good in them. The vines should be immersed in a solution of 0.05 percent Borax or Manocrotophos drug for 10 minutes. After that the vines are suitable for planting in the main field.
