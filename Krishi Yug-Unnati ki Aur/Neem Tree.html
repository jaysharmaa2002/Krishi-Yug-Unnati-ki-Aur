Neem Tree

Neem Tree- Neem is a medicinal and valuable tree. Neem is a tree full of all qualities, each part of which is useful for us, which starts from the neem leaf till the roots and bark of neem. Due to the miraculous properties of neem in our Ayurveda scriptures, it has been described as nectar. Neem bark is used for skin diseases, neem twig is used for teeth whitening, and by chewing neem leaves, where blood is purified.If we talk about the same farming, then organic manure from neem and oil from neem seeds, which is also called nimboli, can also be used in agriculture and as a beauty product. In India, neem is found all over India, except in cold and high areas, and a good profit can be earned by cultivating neem very comfortably in almost every type of soil. Neem leaf is used for leprosy, eye disorders, bloody nose, intestinal worms, stomach upset, loss of appetite, skin ulcers, diseases of the heart and blood vessels (cardiovascular disease), fever, diabetes, gum disease (gingivitis), and liver problems.


Time of sowing The best time for planting neem plants is July-August.

Distance Neem plants are planted at a distance of 5X5 meters and on the bunds of the field for plantation.

Sowing method Neem plants are planted by direct seed sowing, nursery preparation by seed and grafting method.

Planting Method The prepared plant should be planted at a distance of 5X5 m or 4X4 m at a distance of 45X45X45 cm. Planted in deep pits.

Seed quantity The number of plants per acre is about 250 saplings are planted at a distance of 4x4 meteres and about 180 plants are planted at a distance of 5x5 meters.

Favorable climate Tropical and sub-tropical climate is suitable for neem cultivation, but the temperature of 25 to 30 degree Celsius is suitable for this. Neem cultivation in India can be done successfully in almost all regions of the climate.

Land selection Neem can be cultivated in almost all types of soil but it is suitable for rocky, sandy, deep loamy, soil. To get good yield from Neem, the pH value of the soil is more than 6.2 is suitable.

Farm preparation For the good development of neem plants, level the field by plowing the field well two or three times and at the time of last plowing, the field should be made flat and friable. The field should be kept weed free. After this, pits should be excavated according to the appropriate distance and size.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers For good growth of neem plants, while preparing the field, manure or vermi compost should be applied. And at the rate of per pit, 10 grams of urea, 20 grams of super phosphate, 30 grams of murate of potash, and 1.2 kg of neem cake should be filled in the pit.

Weed control To control weeds, weeding should be done from time to time as per the requirement.

Irrigation One irrigation should be done immediately after planting neem plant. Irrigation is not required during monsoon season. If monsoon is delayed, apply irrigation as required. For the first two years, irrigation should be continued in the summer months of April, May-June at an interval of about 15 days.

Harvest The leaves of the trees fall during February-March, the fruits (Nimboli) mature in the month of June-July they can be plucked when mature. Its harvest and monsoon come together in India. Due to which it is difficult to dry Nimboli, the fruits are dried in the shade.

Storage Nimboli is best stored in a dry place, ideal for godown stores and not good for cold storage.
