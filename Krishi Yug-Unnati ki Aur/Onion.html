Onion

Onion- Onion is an important vegetable and spice crop in India. It also contains small amounts of protein and some vitamins. Onion has many medicinal properties. Onions are used in soups, pickles and in salads. Generally, the onion producing states of India are Maharashtra, Gujarat, Uttar Pradesh, Orissa, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, MP, Andhra Pradesh and Bihar.
MP Onion is mainly cultivated in Khandwa, Shajapur, Ratlam, Chhindwara, Sagar and Indore. In general, onion is cultivated in all the districts. Onion exports from India to Malaysia, UAE Exported to Canada, Japan, Lebanon and Kuwait.It is critical for India to successfully store rabi onions to maintain its market supply during lean months. Additionally, scientific management of onion production in all the three seasons is also essential in augmenting regular supply.So, the Rabi onion crop is the mainstay of India. Normally, the price of the onion is lower during these months due to greater supply.


Improved Varieties: Improved varieties with red peel :- Pusa Red, Nashik Red, Hisar-2, Agri Found, BL 67, U.D.101, and U.D.103
Improved varieties with white skin :- Arka Pragati, Pusa Safed, Patna Safed, White Greno.
Hybrid improved varieties :- Aristocrat, Umpire, Christa, VL-67
Improved varieties grown in Kharif :- Bhimraj, Bhima Red, Bhima Super, Niphad-53, Agrifound, Arka Kalyan, Arka Niketan, Arka Pragati, Agri Found Dark Red.

Seed Quantity:- Seed quantity-10 kg per hectare.

Seed treatment :- Before sowing, treat the seeds with Thyram @ 2 g/kg or Bavistin @ 2 to 3 g/kg seed.

Sowing time:- Onion nursery is sown around 15th June.

Earth :- Onion can be grown in different types of soils, but loamy soils with proper drainage and alluvial soils are considered best for its cultivation, onion should not be grown in more alkaline or marshy soils, to grow it, pH should be increased.

Land preparation: Land preparation is of great importance in the successful production of onions. The first plowing of the field should be done with a soil-reversing plow, after that 2 to 3 ploughings should be done by cultivator or harrow, after each plowing, make sure that the moisture remains safe and at the same time the soil becomes friable.

Manures and Fertilizers :- Dung manure or compost 200 quintals per hectare and nitrogen, phosphorus, potash and sulfur are required at 100, 50, 100, 25 kg per hectare respectively. Dung manure or compost, phosphorus and potash at the time of land preparation and nitrogen should be divided into three parts, 15 and 45 days after planting respectively. Other general rules should be followed for applying manures and fertilizers. Keep in mind that essential nutrients should be used only on the basis of soil test.

Harmful pests and diseases and their prevention:-
Onion Angmari - Brown spots on the leaves, later the leaves dry up - for this spray Dithane Z-78 with 0.15%.
Powdery mildew - The leaves first become yellow, green and elongated and circular spots appear on those leaves. Later these leaves start turning and drying. To control it, spray 0.35% copper oxychloride fungicide.
Throat rot - When its outbreak occurs, the scales start falling apart. To prevent this, protect the crop from insects and moisture.
Thrips of Onion - Its pupae insects suck the juice by piercing the leaves and roots. As a result, white streaks appear on the leaves and the whole crop looks white. Prevention: Spray insecticide for its prevention.

Weed control :- Weeds growing on the crop - Satyanashi (Kateli), amaranth, dub, motha, spider, khartuwa. Remove weeds from the onion crop by weeding. Propaquizafop 5% + Oxyflurofen 12% w/w EC can be used as a chemical weedicide. Spraying of Pendimethylene 3.5 liters per hectare in 800 liters of water for three days after transplanting does not prevent germination of weeds.

Irrigation :- In Kharif season crop, irrigate immediately after planting, otherwise delay in irrigation increases the chances of plant death. Irrigate the onion crop grown in Kharif season when the monsoon goes away as needed. If required, apply light irrigation at an interval of 8-10 days. If water stagnates in the field due to excessive rainfall or any other reason, then make arrangements to remove it soon, otherwise the possibility of fungal diseases increases in the crop.

Digging of onion tubers: Kharif onion crop is ready for digging in about 5 months in the month of November-December. As soon as the onion knot takes its full size and the leaves start drying, stop irrigation about 10-15 days before and crush the tops of the onion plants with the help of foot. Due to this the tubers become solid and their growth stops. After this, the tubers are dug and dried by keeping them in rows in the field itself.

Storage :- Storage of onions is an important function. Some varieties have high storage capacity like Pusa Red, Nashik Red, Bellary Red and N-2-4. The An-53, Arligreno and Pusa Ratnar have less storage capacity. Varieties that have low food refractive index and high evaporation speed and total evaporation have low storage capacity.
Storage capacity is more than size. Moisture free and ventilated place is suitable for onion storage. And proper temperature is required. Average yield of Kharif onion is 300-350 q/ha.
