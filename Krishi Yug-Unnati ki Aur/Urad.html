Urad

URAD- is a major pulse crop. Urad has been cultivated since ancient times. The crop of urad gets ready to ripen in a short period of time. Its crop is suitable for Kharif, Rabi and summer seasons. Urad is mainly used in the form of pulses in our country. In the irrigated areas of Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, Rajasthan, Haryana, Madhya Pradesh, it is possible to increase the annual income of farmers by cultivating short duration (60-65 days) pulse crop, Urad. In addition, soil conservation/fertility can also be promoted.
Mainly two types of species are found, first for production in Kharif such as ï¿½ Shekhar-3, Azad Urd-3, Pant Urd-31, WWV-108, Pant U-30, I.P. U-94 and PDU-1 are mainly used for production in Zayed Pant U-19, Pant U-35, Type-9, Narendra Urd-1, Azad Urd-1, Uttara, Azad Urd-2 and Shekhar-2 are species. There are some such species, which give production in both Kharif and Zaid, such as Type-9, Narendra urd-1, Azad urd-2, Shekhar urd-2 These species can be grown in both the crops.
Seed Rate :- Average 8-10 kg per acre.


Sowing time:- The appropriate time for sowing urad in Kharif season is the second fortnight of June (15th June 30th June). Late sowing should be avoided.

Seed Treatment:- Before sowing of urad, treat the seed with 3 grams thiram or 2.5 grams dithane M-45 per kg seed. For biological seed treatment, Trichoderma fungicide is used at the rate of 5 to 6 grams per kg of seed.


Earth :- Urad is cultivated in different types of land. Light sandy, loamy or medium type of land with good drainage of water is more suitable for urad. The land between pH value 7-8 is fertile for urad. Acidic and alkaline soil is not suitable.

Field preparation: For the cultivation of urad, before sowing, plow the field thoroughly with native plow or cultivator 2-3 times, so that the soil becomes fine, then after this, prepare the field for sowing by running the pad.

Climate :- Moist and warm weather is necessary for the cultivation of urad. The optimum temperature at the time of growth is 25-35 degree centigrade. Urad is easily grown in areas with 700-900 mm of rainfall. It is not suitable for cultivation in places with high water logging. Excess rainfall at flowering stage is harmful. The grain gets spoiled when it rains at the ripening stage.

Chemical Fertilizers and Manures :- While preparing the field for urad crop, 15-20 tonnes of well decomposed cow dung per acre should be mixed well in the soil. Apply chemical fertilizers radially before sowing. Rainfall Condition: 12.5 kg Nitrogen + 25 kg Phosphorous + 12.5 kg Potassium Oxide + 10 kg Sulphur/acre. Other nutrients should be used on the basis of soil test.

Harmful pests and diseases and their prevention:- Pod borer insect: The nectar of this insect feeds on the grains by piercing the beans. Due to which there is heavy loss to the yield.
Prevention: Spraying of Monocrotophos at the rate of one liter per hectare in 500 liters of water should be done.

White fly: It is the major pest of urad crop. Which acts as a carrier of the yellow mosaic virus.
Prevention : Triszofos 40 EC. Spray one liter of water in 500 liters of water. Spray 100 ml of Acetamaprid or Imidacloprid or 25 Ltr of 51 Imethoate solution in 500 Ltr of water per hectare.

Semi-looper (Semi Luper) - It mainly feeds on tender leaves and sieves the leaves.
Prevention - Quinalphos or Profenophos 50 EC. Make a solution of 1 liter in 500 liters of water and spray it.

Aphid - It sucks the juice of Mulankur. Due to which the growth of plants stops.
Prevention - Profeno + Cyper 1 liter or Chlorpyrifos 20 EC. Spraying should be done by mixing a solution of 1 liter 600 liters of water.

Yellow Mosaic Virus Disease - This is a common disease of urad and is spread by virus. Its effect is visible only after 4-5 weeks. In this disease, firstly yellow spots appear on the leaves in circular form. Within a few days the entire leaves turn yellow. Eventually these leaves turn white and dry up.
Prevention - Control of the disease is possible by the prevention of white fly. Sowing of urad yellow mosaic disease resistant variety Pant U-19, Pant U-30, UG218, TPU-4, Pant Urad-30, Barkha, KU-96-3 needed.

Leaf twist disease - Appears as cyanosis on young leaves on the central veins of the leaf. In this disease, the leaves turn upwards above the central veins and the lower leaves turn inwards and the growth of leaves stops and the plant dies.
Prevention - This is a viral disease. Which is transmitted by thrips. For thrips, acephate 75 percent sp. Or 2 ml dimethoate per liter should be sprayed and the crop should be sown on time.

This disease is spread by fungus. Its symptoms appear as small spots on the leaves.
Prevention - Spraying should be done by mixing Carbendazim + Mancozeb in a solution of 1 kg 1000 liters of water.

Weed control :- Weeds cause more damage to crops than expected. Therefore, for more production, proper use of other modern sleep decongestants should be done while conducting weeding-weeding, kulpa and dora etc. on time. After sowing the crop but before the germination of seeds, weed control can be done by sprinkling 1.25 kg of Pendimethalin in 1000 liters of water.

Irrigation :- Generally, Kharif crop does not require irrigation. If there is a lack of rain, an irrigation must be done at the time of pod formation. Urad crop requires 3 to 4 irrigations in Zaid. First irrigation should be done in the form of Paleva and other irrigation should be done at an interval of 15 to 20 days as per the requirement of the crop. It is very important to have proper moisture in the field at the time of flowering and grain formation.

Harvesting time :- When the pods and plants dry out, the grains become hard, and the grain should have a moisture percentage of 22.5% at the time of harvesting. Pod disintegration (spreading of the crop's seeds as they mature) is a common problem in pulses. Therefore, the pods should be picked as soon as they are ripe. Harvested crop should be picked up after 2-3 days.

Storage :- After harvesting the plant is spread on the floor to dry. Leave the cut stems to dry in the sun. To get the right variety, keep the harvested crop from one variety to another. To remove the moisture in the urad grains, it is necessary to dry them well in the sun. The crop can then be stored. The crop should be stored in a moisture free place.

Production :- The yield is 10-15 quintal per hectare in pure crop and 6-8 quintal per hectare in mixed crop.
