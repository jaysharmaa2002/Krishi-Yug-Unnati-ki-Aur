Rye

Rye- Rye has a special place in Rabi oilseed crops. In the areas where paddy could not be cultivated due to less rainfall, the kharif crops can be compensated by early cultivation of rye in those vacant fields.

The grain of mustard is small and black, small round-rounded rye is often found in red and black grains. White colored rye is also found in foreign countries.

Mustard seeds are very similar to mustard seeds. Simply, mustard is slightly smaller than mustard. Rye seed oil is also extracted. Rye has a prominent place in Rabi oilseed crops. Its cultivation is more profitable in the condition of limited irrigation.


Time of sowing The best time for sowing of mustard is the last week of September to the first week of October.

Distance For sowing of mustard, the row to row distance is 30 cm. And the plant to plant distance is 10 to 15 cm. Keep it For sowing of mustard, the spacing of rows is 45 cm. And the plant to plant distance is 10 cm. Keep it

Seed depth Seed 4-5 cm Must be sown deep.

Sowing method Use only sowing machine for sowing.

Seed quantity The quantity of seed required is 1.5 kg per acre. After 3 weeks of sowing, destroy weak plants and leave healthy plants in the field.

Seed treatment To protect the seed from soil pests and diseases, treat the seeds with Thiram @ 3 gm/kg of seed.

Suitable climate Rye is cultivated in India in the winter season. This crop requires 18 to 25 Celsius temperature. Rain, high humidity and cloudy atmosphere in the atmosphere are not good for rye crop at the time of flowering. If this type of weather happens, then there is more chance of mahu or chapa coming on the crop.

Land Selection Rye can be cultivated in all types of soils. Rye can be cultivated in sandy to heavy clay soils. But sandy loam soil is most suitable. This crop can tolerate mild alkalinity. But the soil should not be acidic.

Farm Preparation For rye cultivation, plow the land with indigenous plow or cultivator two or three times and after each plowing, make a ploughing. The beds should be soft, wet and flat for the seeds to germinate evenly. The crop sown on seed bed germinates well.

Manures and Fertilizers At the time of preparation of field for cultivation of rye, well decomposed cow dung at the rate of 7-12 t/acre should be mixed well in the soil. Get the soil tested for proper use of fertilizers. Apply 40 kg Nitrogen (90 kg Urea), 12 kg Phosphorus (75 kg Single Super Phosphate) and 6 kg Potassium (10 kg Murate of Potash) per acre to mustard crop. In areas with low rainfall, apply all the compost before sowing.For rye crop, apply half the amount of manure before sowing and half at the time of first watering. Keep in mind that chemical fertilizers should be used only on the basis of soil test.

Weed control For weed control in rye cultivation, do 2-3 weedings in 15 days interval.

Irrigation Sowing the crop after irrigation. After sowing, three irrigations are required at an interval of three weeks to get a good crop. Use more organic fertilizers to conserve soil moisture.

Harvesting and Threshing The more ripe the crop, the greater the risk of pods cracking. Therefore, the crop should be harvested when the plants turn yellow and the pods turn brown. The grain is separated by drying the crop by beating it with thresher or sticks. After drying the seeds, put them in sacks or in drums. And store it in a moisture free place.

Production By cultivating the above advanced technology of rye, the yield of 15 to 20 quintals per hectare in unirrigated areas and 20 to 30 quintals per hectare in irrigated areas is obtained.
