Marigold

Marigold- The demand for flowers in the market is a great deal for marigold cultivation. Some species of marigold like Hazara and Pavar species are capable of giving flowers throughout the year. This species is very important from the point of view of commercial farming.Its cultivation has low cost and high profit. From this crop, which is ready in two and a half months, two crops are taken in a month. One bigha costs one thousand to one and a half thousand rupees. Whereas the yield can be taken up to 3 quintals. Its price in the market is 70 to 100 per quintal.Marigold flower is sold in the market for Rs 70 to 80 per kg, when its demand increases in festivals and marriage events, the price goes up to Rs 100 per kg.

Tagetes is a genus of annual or perennial, mostly herbaceous plants in the sunflower family Asteraceae. They are among several groups of plants known in English as marigolds. The genus Tagetes was described by Carl Linnaeus in 1753


Time of sowing It is sown in India at different times according to the variation of climate. In North India, it is sown at two times, the first time from March to June and the second time from August to September.

Seed quantity The quantity of marigold seed to be planted depends on the varieties selected. The seed of hybrid varieties is 700 to 800 grams per hectare and seed of common varieties at 1.25 kg per hectare is sufficient.

Nursery preparation for marigold cultivation Marigold seeds are first grown in the nursery. Plow the nursery by adding rotted dung manure. Make the soil friable and add sand to it, make beds and put the seeds of balls in it. Filter the cow dung and cover the seed from above. Irrigate when seeds start setting. After reaching 8-10 cm, uproot the plants from the nursery and plant them in the prepared field.

Printing Method It is planted in flat beds. The spacing between plants depends on the variety.

Distance In transplanting African varieties, 60 cm line to line and 45 cm plant to plant distance is done. At the same time, in transplanting of other varieties, 40 cm is kept from plant to plant and the same line to line distance.

Famous Varieties
African Marigold - Climax, Colerate, Crown of Gold, Cupid Yellow, First Lady, Phulki Fru First, Joint Sunset, Indian Chief Glitters, Jubilee, Man in the Moon, Mammoth Mum, River Side Beauty, Yellow Supreme, Spun Gold etc., Yeh Useful for commercial farming.
Maxon Marigold -Tagetes lucida, Tagetes lemoni, Tagetes manuta etc. are prominent.
French Marigold-Bolero Goldie, Goldie Stript, Golden Orange, Golden Gem, Red Coat, Dainty Marietta, Red Head, Golden Bal etc. The plant of these species is like a spreading bush, the plants are small and look good.
Hybrid varieties - Nuggetera, Souffred, Pusa Orange Marigold, Pusa Basanti Marigold etc.

Favorable Climate It is cultivated in autumn. But it is not able to tolerate frost. It is cultivated in the autumn in the plains of North India and in the summer in the hilly areas of North India.

Earth Sandy loam soil is ideal for the cultivation of marigold. Land with proper drainage, whose pH is The value should be between 7.0 to 7.5.

Farm Preparation For marigold cultivation, 2-3 good deep plowing of the field should be done before transplanting. In the last plowing, the field should be made loose, level and weed free by applying pat. Also, good drainage should be arranged in the field.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers
For good yield in marigold cultivation, 250 to 300 quintals per hectare of rotten dung manure is suitable along with 120 kg nitrogen, 80 kg phosphorus and 80 kg potash per hectare while preparing the field.
Method - Full amount of phosphorus and potash and half quantity of nitrogen should be given while preparing the field, remaining half quantity of nitrogen should be given twice in equal quantity, first one month after transplanting and remaining two months after transplanting.

Weed Control It is essential to keep the marigold field clean from weeds. To control weeds, keep weeding and hoeing from time to time and give 10 to 12 cm high soil on the plants. So that the plant does not fall when it flowers.
Irrigation Irrigate as per requirement in marigold cultivation. Although irrigation is not required much in its cultivation, only two to three irrigations make the cultivation flourish, while the yield is up to two and a half to three quintals per bigha.

Plucking and Harvesting Flowers should always be plucked early in the morning. Flowers get spoiled due to strong sunlight. Place the flowers diagonally with a sharp knife in a clean vessel or pot.

Storage After harvesting, spread the flowers in a shady place. Harvesting should be done only after the flowers have fully bloomed. To keep cut flowers longer, keep them at 8 degree centigrade temperature and 80 percent humidity.

Production Produce marigold on the fertility of the land and proper care of the crop. Usually 125 to 150 quintals per hectare is obtained as a yield. Flower production up to 350 quintals per hectare is obtained from some of the improved varieties. Whose market price is 70 to 100 per kg.
