Cashew

Cashew- Cashew tree is a fast growing tropical tree that produces cashew and cashew seed. Whose fruit is very popular for dry fruits. Import export of cashew is also a big business. Cashew is a major foreign exchange receiving crop of India. Although cashew is cultivated commercially and on a large scale in Kerala, Maharashtra, Goa, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh, Orissa, West Bengal, Chhattisgarh, Gujarat and North-Eastern regions,But some districts of Jharkhand state which are adjacent to Bengal and Orissa also have huge potential for its cultivation. Many nutrients are found in cashew nuts such as potassium, copper, zinc, celium, iron, magnesium etc. which are good for our health.

The cashew tree (Anacardium occidentale) is a tropical evergreen tree that produces the cashew seed and the cashew apple accessory fruit. The tree can grow as high as 14 m (46 ft), but the dwarf cultivars, growing up to 6 m (20 ft), prove more profitable, with earlier maturity and greater yields.

The cashew seed is commonly considered a snack nut (cashew nut) eaten on its own, used in recipes, or processed into cashew cheese or cashew butter. Like the tree, the nut is often simply called cashew. Cashew allergies are triggered by the proteins found in tree nuts, and cooking often does not remove or change these proteins.

In 2019, four million tonnes of cashew nuts were produced globally, with Ivory Coast and India as the leading producers. As well as the nut and fruit, the plant has several other uses. The shell of the cashew seed yields derivatives that can be used in many applications including lubricants, waterproofing, paints, and, starting in World War II, arms production.The cashew apple is a light reddish to yellow fruit, whose pulp and juice can be processed into a sweet, astringent fruit drink or fermented and distilled into liquor.


Improved Varieties National Cashew Research Center has recommended high cashew for different states. The major varieties of cashew are T-40, BPP-1, BPP-2, Vegurla-4, Ullal-2, Ullal-4 etc. The varieties which are good for Orissa, Madhya Pradesh, Bengal, Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka can also be cultivated in the state of Jharkhand.

Planting Time The months of May-July are the right time for plant preparation.

Planting Method Planting Planting We can plant cashew nut by both the methods of planting seeds or planting saplings. For cultivation by seed method, we have to plant two seeds in a pit, and when it takes the form of a plant after 5 years, then it is to be planted separately. It happens in the month of July-August and in this way we get the plant in 2 years.

Distance We should keep in mind that water should not stagnate near the pit and the distance between two plants should be 4 * 4 or 5 * 5 meters.

How to plant Cashew plant can be prepared like soft wood grafting or gifting pen. Cashew plant should be planted in a square manner at a distance of 700-800 cm, and after the preparation of the field, 60 * 60 * 60 cm should be planted in April-May. Make pits at a distance. Now we have to leave the pits open for 15 days to 20 days, after that 2 kg DA. Mixture of P. or Rock Phosphate, 5 kg. Dung manure or compost is to be mixed in the soil and filled in the pit.

Planting time in the field Planting of cashew plant should be done at the time of rain and after that the thali should be made and weeding and weeding of weeds should also be done in the trays from time to time. Dry grass should also be spread in the plates so that water can also be conserved.

Favorable Climate Cashew crop is tropical. It grows well in hot climates. In places where there is frost or cold, there is an effect on the cashew crop. 700 m High place whose temperature is 200 C.G. Cashew cultivation is good there. Places receiving rainfall up to 60-450 cm in a year are considered suitable.

Land Selection Although cashew can be grown in many types of soils, but red and laterite soils along the coast are considered suitable.

Farm Preparation For the preparation of the field, first of all, after cleaning the field, deep plowing should be done 2-3 times. The bushes of the field have to be uprooted and destroyed, so that there is no problem in growing the cashew plant.

Fertilizers and chemical Fertilizers For good production in cashew cultivation, every year 10 to 15 kg of cow dung or vermi compost and the right amount of chemical fertilizers should also be added to the plant. In the first year, 300 grams of urea, 70 grams of murate of potash, 200 grams of rock phosphate are to be applied to each plant. In the second year, it is to be fertilized twice and after the third year, the plants should be given 600 grams. Rock Phosphate, 1 kg Urea and 200 gm Murate of Potash should be given half-and-half in the month of May-June and September-October every year.

Weed control Controlling weeds is a part of the task of horticulture management for good growth and good harvest of cashew plant. Before applying the first dose of fertilizer and manure, weeding should be done to remove weeds. And later weeding should be done as per requirement. Another method of weed control should be use of mulching.

Irrigation Cashew is generally a rain-fed crop. However, in any crop, timely irrigation gives good production. In the first one to two years of planting, irrigation is required till the soil is well rooted. The next stage of irrigation is carried out during flowering and fruit setting to prevent fruit drop.

Cutting - Pruning In order to give good structure to the cashew plant in the beginning, along with training, pruning and pruning of the tree is needed. Remove branches or twigs below to allow the tree trunk to grow to a meter. Dry and dead twigs and branches should be removed as needed.

Fruit harvesting and storage Cashew plant starts giving crop from the third year onwards. Generally good cashews are brownish-green in colour, smooth and full. Cashew fruit is not plucked, only the fallen fruit is collected. After drying the collected fruit thoroughly in the sun, the cashew nuts are sorted and packed.

Production Every year, on an average, 8 to 10 kg of nuts are obtained from a tree. Around 10 to 15 quintals of nuts are found in one hectare. Those who get edible cashews after processing.
