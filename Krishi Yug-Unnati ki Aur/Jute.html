Jute

JUTE, Jute is a dicotyledonous, fibrous plant. Its stem is thin and cylindrical. There are fibers of jute, jute and similar plants. Its fibers are used for making sacks, rugs, tents, tarpaulins, sackcloth, ropes, low-grade clothes and paper.
The word 'Jute' is understood to be derived from Sanskrit 'Jata' or 'Jute'. In Europe, the term is used for the first time in the 18th century, although there this substance was being imported under the name of "pat" from before the 18th century.
Jute is one of the important natural fibers in the context of cotton cultivation and use. Farming is dependent on climate, weather and soil. About 85% of the world's jute cultivation is concentrated in the Ganges Delta. This fertile geographical area is shared by Bangladesh and India (mainly West Bengal). China also dominates the cultivation of jute. On a smaller scale, Thailand, Myanmar (Burma), Pakistan, Nepal and Bhutan also cultivate jute.

Time of sowing Jute is sown in February on slope lands and March to July in high altitude lands.

Seed quantity Sowing in rows with seed drill is sufficient to yield 4 to 5 kg of seeds per hectare for Capsularis varieties and 3 to 5 kg for Olitorius. For sprinkling sowing, 5 to 6 kg seed is required.

Seed treatment Before sowing the jute seed should be sown after treating it with Thiram at the rate of 3 gm or Carbendazim 50 WP at the rate of 2 gm/kg of seed.

Sowing method Jute should be sown behind the plow, the line to line distance should be 30 cm and the plant to plant distance 7 to 8 cm and the depth should not be more than 2 to 3 cm. Using multirow jute seed drill, 4 lines are sown in one go and one person can sow one acre in a day.

Suitable land Light loamy and loamy soils are suitable for the cultivation of jute. It is not appropriate to cultivate it in more waterlogged land. Because there is a risk of plant damage and loss of quality due to waterlogging for a long time. The pH of the land for its cultivation. The value should be normal.

Favorable climate Hot and humid climate is suitable for the cultivation of jute. Its plants need rain. But abnormal rainfall damages its yield. It is cultivated in summer and rainy season. Due to this, the effect of winter is not seen on its crop.

Farm preparation One plowing with an earth-turning plow and then 2 to 3 ploughings with a native plow or cultivator, plowing the land with a pad and preparing the field for sowing. Since the jute seed is very small, the soil needs to be friable, so that the jute seed has good deposition. It is considered good for proper moisture deposition in the soil.

Manures and Fertilizers To get more yield from jute cultivation, it is necessary to give proper amount of fertilizer in its cultivation. For this, at the time of plowing the field, put 25 to 30 tons of rotten old cow dung in the field and mix it well in the soil. In addition to organic manure, if the farmer brother wants to use chemical fertilizers in his fields, then for this the last crop of the field At the time of tillage, add 90 kg of nitrogen, phosphorus and potash in the ratio of 2:1:1 in the field and mix it in the soil. After that give half the amount of nitrogen with irrigation twice to the plants.

Kits and diseases and their prevention Jute crop is generally affected by two diseases- root and stem rot and these diseases sometimes destroy the crop completely. To avoid this, seeds should be sown only after treatment. To prevent these diseases, Trichoderma viridi should be used at the rate of 5 gm per kg seed and 2.5 kg per hectare mixed with 50 kg of decomposed cow dung.
Semilooper, apion, stem bivil pests are infested on jute crop. To control these pests, 1.5 liters of dicafal can be dissolved in 700 to 800 liters of water and sprayed at 40 to 45 and 60 to 65 and 100 to 105 day stages of the crop. For the control of these pests, neem-produced chemical Azadirachtin 0.03% solution should be made and sprayed at the rate of 1.5 liters.

Weed control Like all crops, its crop also needs weed control. In jute cultivation, weeding is done for weed control. The first weeding of its plants should be done after 25 to 30 days after planting the seed, when the plant becomes about one fit. After this, one more hoeing of the plants should be done at an interval of 15 to 20 days. For chemical weed control, spraying of pendimethylene or fluchloralene should be done on the plants immediately after sowing the seeds.

Irrigation Jute plants require very little irrigation. Because it is cultivated at such a time, when the rainy season remains throughout the whole of India. At the time of its transplantation in June, there is a monsoon season. But after rain, when the moisture content starts decreasing in the field or if there is no rain on time, then irrigation should be done in the field.

Pruning and rotting of jute plants It can be harvested after 100 to 120 days of jute crop to get the best fiber. When harvested early, the yield of fiber is usually low. But the fiber is better than the late harvested crop. Small and thin diameter plants should be sorted and tied in separate small bundles (15 to 20 cm) and left in the field for two to three days for leaves to fall.
After keeping the bundles of cut jute plants in water for 2 to 3 days in a standing condition, after placing them in a row or two in a pond or lightly running water to a depth of 10 cm, cover them with weeds of water and cover them with a heavy stone. Should be pressed with a piece, and care should be taken that the bundle should not touch the bottom surface of the pond. Under normal condition, in 15 to 20 days, the plant becomes capable of removing the fiber by rotting.
The use of bacterial culture increases the quality of the fiber along with saving 4% time in decomposition.

Fiber Removal and Drying The fibers of each rotten jute plant should be removed separately, washed thoroughly in running clean water, hanging parallel to a wire, bamboo etc. and dried in strong sunlight for 3 to 4 days. The fiber should be turned over during the drying period. By adopting intensive methods, a yield of 25 to 35 quintals per hectare of fiber can be obtained.
