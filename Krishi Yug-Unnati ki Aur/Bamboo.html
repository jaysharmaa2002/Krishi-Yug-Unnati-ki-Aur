Bamboo

Bamboo-As you know bamboo is a herbaceous plant, the English word 'Bambu' is derived from the Indian word 'Mumbu' or 'Bambu'. Bamboo can grow very fast. In some species, it grows up to 1 meter in a day. The most useful part of bamboo is the stem. The length of the stems ranges from 30 to 150 feet and the width varies from 1/4 inch to one foot. Bamboo is used for making bats, ladders, baskets, mat baskets, bottles made of bamboo, furniture, toys, agricultural machinery and other decorations.Apart from this, it is used in making paper. Now bamboo is also being used to give a modern look to the houses. Apart from this, pickles are also made from its edible species at some places. It is found in all places in India, Orissa, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Maharashtra, Kerala, Manipur, Uttar Pradesh, West Bengal, Bihar and are found in the north-western states.


Selection of Varieties There are about 136 species of bamboo, of which many bamboo varieties are used for different purposes, with about 10 varieties being the most commonly used. You have to choose the species by seeing for what purpose you want to cultivate bamboo. If you are cultivating bamboo for furniture, then choose a related species.

Time of sowing Planting time for bamboo cultivation is in the month of July.

Distance Bamboo plant is planted at a distance of 3 to 4 meters.

Sowing Method Nursery preparation is done through seeds and sowing is done by rhizomes.

Seed quantity Farmers can plant about 1500 to 2500 saplings in one hectare according to the need and species.

Favorable climate Bamboo plants can be grown in a variety of climates. Dry climate is suitable for its cultivation.

Land Selection Well drained sandy soils are suitable for bamboo cultivation, although bamboo can be cultivated in many types of soils.

Farm Preparation For bamboo cultivation, the soil should be tilled and levelled by plowing the field well before transplanting. Proper drainage should be arranged and the field should be kept weed free. After that, appropriate size pits should be dug in the field as per the requirement.

Fertilizers and chemical Fertilizers Bamboo plants do not require special nutrients for growth. However, for proper growth, vermicompost or dung manure can be added to the soil at the time of field preparation.

Weed control To control weeds, weeding should be done from time to time as required.

Irrigation Irrigation is necessary for the rapid growth of plants. Plants require water immediately after transplanting. In the absence of moisture in the soil, their growth is adversely affected.

Interclass crop Bamboo plant is planted at a distance of three to four meters. Therefore, in the middle of it, other crops can be cultivated. This will result in additional manpower to the farmer. Its leaves can be used as fodder for livestock.

Harvest Bamboo cultivation becomes ready in 3 to 4 years. Harvesting should start in its fourth year.

Subsidy for bamboo cultivation
For the cultivation of bamboo, saplings will be provided by the government from the government nursery for free.
The average cost per plant in three years would be Rs 240. Out of this, Rs 120 per plant will be given by the government.
Apart from the North East, 50 percent of the government and 50 percent of the farmers will be engaged for its cultivation in other areas. Of the 50 percent government share, 60 percent will be held by the Center and 40 percent by the state.
In the North East, 60 percent will be planted by the government and 40 percent by farmers. In 60 per cent government money, 90 per cent will be shared by the central government and 10 per cent by the state government.

Benefits of bamboo cultivation If you plant 1500 to 2500 saplings in one hectare and grow other crops between these plants, then after 4 years you will start earning about 3 lakh rupees. The special thing in the cultivation of bamboo is that its plant lasts for about 40 years, it does not need to be planted again and again. Once applied with this, it can give you income for many years. Apart from this, if you plant bamboo at 4 x 4 meters on the bund of the field along with other crops, then one hectare will start earning about 30 thousand rupees from the fourth year.
