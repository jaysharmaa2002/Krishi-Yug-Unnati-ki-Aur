Pappermint

Pappermint- Detailed information for cultivation of Mentha: Mentha cultivation is done on a large scale by farmers in districts like Badaun, Rampur, Moradabad, Bareilly, Pilibhit, Barabanki, Faizabad, Ambedkar Nagar, Lucknow etc. For the last few years, Mentha has been making its place as a major crop of Zaid in these districts.

Its oil is used for fragrance and in making medicine. Mentha (mint) is an active herb. Mint is used in oil, tooth paste, mouth wash and as a flavoring in many dishes. Mentha is found mostly in the world in Angola, Thailand, China, Argentina, Brazil, Japan, India and Paraguay. Uttar Pradesh and Punjab in India are the mint producing states of India.

Peppermint (Mentha X piperita, also known as Mentha balsamea Wild) is a hybrid mint, a cross between watermint and spearmint. Indigenous to Europe and the Middle East, the plant is now widely spread and cultivated in many regions of the world. It is occasionally found in the wild with its parent species.

Although the genus Mentha comprises more than 25 species, the one in most common use is peppermint. While Western peppermint is derived from Mentha piperita, Chinese peppermint, or "Bohe", is derived from the fresh leaves of Mentha haplocalyx.Mentha piperita and Mentha haplocalyx are both recognized as plant sources of menthol and menthone and are among the oldest herbs used for both culinary and medicinal products.


Recommended Species:
1. Mentha spicata MSS-1 (Native mint)
2. Kosa (best for early and late planting) (Japanese mint)
3. Himalaya Gomti (M.A.H.-9)
4. MSS-1 HY-77
5. Mentha Piperata-Cookerel

Time to Plant Mentha Roots in the Nursery Mentha roots are sown in the nursery in the month of August. The nursery is made in a high place so that it can be prevented from waterlogging. Water should be drained when it rains heavily.

Sowing time The proper time for sowing the roots of Mentha is from 15th January to 15th February. Late sowing reduces the amount of oil and gives less yield. For late sowing, the plants should be prepared in the nursery and transplanted in the field from March to the first week of April. For late cultivation of Mentha, choose Kosi species.

Refining of geostationary Before transplanting, the ground floor should be treated with a solution of 2 grams of Carbendazim per liter of water by immersing it for 5 minutes.

Sowing method For planting Japanese mentha, the line spacing should be 30-40 cm, native mint 45-60 cm and Japanese mint should keep plant-to-plant distance of 15 cm. Roots should be transplanted into the furrows at a depth of 3 to 5 cm. Light irrigation should be given immediately after transplanting. 8-10 cm pieces of 4-5 quintal roots are suitable for sowing / transplanting.

Favorable climate It is considered very good to have rain during the growing of Mentha, keep in mind that the atmosphere is clear at the time of harvesting and sunlight remains completely.

Earth For the cultivation of Mentha, sandy loam and matiyari loam land with a pH value of 6-7.5 with good drainage of sufficient biomass is suitable.

Farm preparation By plowing the field well, the land is made level. Immediately after transplanting Mentha, light water is applied in the field, due to which the Mentha plant should be fine.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers Generally, it is beneficial to use fertilizers on the basis of the recommendations of the soil test. For good yield of mentha under normal conditions, 120-150 kg nitrogen, 50-60 kg phosphorus, 40 kg potash and 20 kg sulfur should be used per hectare. Full amount of Phosphorus, Potash and Sulfur and 30-35 kg of Nitrogen should be applied in the furrows before sowing/transplanting.Remaining nitrogen should be given at about 45 days after sowing/transplanting, but at 70-80 days and 20 days after the first harvest.

Some precautions for crop protection
Termites:-Termites damage the roots, as a result, there is a bad effect on the deposit. After infestation, the plants dry up. In case of infestation of termite in standing crop, apply Chlorpyrifos @ 2.5 liters per hectare along with irrigation water.
Hairy caterpillar:-It lives on the lower surface of the leaves and feeds on the leaves. Due to which the percentage of oil is reduced. To protect the crop from this pest, spray Dichlor Vas at the rate of 500 ml or Fenvalerate 750 ml per hectare by dissolving in 600-700 liters of water. The eggs laid in the herd and the caterpillars eating in the herd in the initial stage should be collected and destroyed. Moths should be killed by attracting them with light.
Leaf Wrapping Insect:- Its nectars eat while wrapping the leaves. For its prevention, spray Monocrotophos 36 EC @ 1.0 liter per hectare by making a solution in 600-700 liters of water.
Disease Root rot: In this disease the roots turn black. Pink spots appear on the roots. Before sowing/transplanting, treatment of earthworms with 0.1% Carbendazim cures this disease. Apart from this, use disease free earthworms.
Foliage: Dark brown spots appear on the leaves. This causes the leaves to turn yellow and fall. For the diagnosis of this disease, mix 2 kg fungicide named Mancozeb 75 WP in 600-800 liters and spray it at the rate of per hectare.

Weed control To prevent weeds in Mentha crop, weeding should be done. For weed control by weed control chemicals, dissolve 3.3 liters of Pendimethalin 30 EC per hectare in 700-800 liters of water and spray it after sowing/transplanting as soon as oat comes.

Irrigation The type of soil irrigated in Mentha depends on the temperature and intensity of winds. First irrigation in Mentha should be done immediately after sowing/transplanting. After this, irrigation should be continued at an interval of 20-25 days and it is necessary to irrigate after each harvesting.

Harvesting Mentha crop is usually harvested twice. It is done at about 100-120 days after the first harvest when the plants start flowering. Plants should be cut at a height of 4-5 cm from the ground surface. Second harvesting should be done at about 70-80 days after the first harvesting. After harvesting, leave the plants in open sunlight for 2-3 hours, after that dry the harvested crop lightly in shade and quickly extract the oil from the machine by distillation method.

Yield= About 250-300 quintals of herb or 125-150 kg in two cuttings. Oil per hectare is obtained.
