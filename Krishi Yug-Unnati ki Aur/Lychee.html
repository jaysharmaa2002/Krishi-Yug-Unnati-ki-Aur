Lychee

Lychee- Lychee is known as a fruit. Due to its attractive color, taste and quality, litchi fruits are maintaining their special place not only in India but in the world. India is the second largest producer of litchi in the world after China. In India, it is cultivated only in Jammu and Kashmir, Uttar Pradesh and Madhya Pradesh.But in view of the increasing demand, it is now being cultivated in Bihar, Jharkhand, Chhattisgarh, Orissa, Punjab, Haryana, Uttaranchal, Assam and Tripura and West Bengal etc.

Lychee, (Litchi chinensis), also spelled litchi or lichi, evergreen tree of the soapberry family (Sapindaceae), grown for its edible fruit. The fruit is usually eaten fresh but can also be canned or dried. The flavour of the fresh pulp is aromatic and musky, and the dried pulp is acidic and very sweet.


Variety Bihar:- Shahi, China, Desi, Purbi, Rose Fragrant, Kasba, Mandrji, Heavenly Bedana, Early Bedana, Tricoliya, Swarna Rupa
Uttar Pradesh:- Early Large Red, Early Bedana, Late Large Red, Late Bedana, Muzaffarpur (Shahi), Rose Fragrant, Kulkatiya, Extra Early Green, Pink, Pickle, Sour, Dehradun, Piazzi
West Bengal:- Bombay, Elachi, Early Elachi, Late China, Desi, Purbi, Kasba, Muzaffarpur
Punjab and Haryana:- Saharanpur, Dehradun, Muzaffarpur, Late Seedless, Early Seedless, Rose Fragrant, Early Large Red, Late Large Red, Kulkatiya, Sour, Pink

Time of sowing It is sown in the month of August-September, immediately after the monsoon. Sometimes it is sown in Punjab till the month of November. Two year old plants are selected for sowing.

Distance For sowing, the row-to-row distance is kept 8-10 meters and plant-to-plant distance is kept 8-10 meters for square mode.

Seed Depth A few days ago, dig pits of 1 m x 1 m x 1 m in the sun. The pits are filled with about 40 kg decomposed compost, 2 kg neem/Karanj cake, 1 kg bone meal/ single super phosphate and 200-300 g muriate of potash. Incorporating about 2 baskets of soil from the root zone of older lychee trees encourages mycorrhiza growth.

Sowing Method It is sown directly by sowing the seed and planting the seedling.

Soil Lychee can be grown in many types of soil. Light loamy soil with proper drainage is good for the plant. It yields quite well. Waterlogged land is not suitable for litchi cultivation. The pH value of the land should be around 7.5 to 8.

Preparation for Cultivation For the cultivation of litchi, in the beginning, remove the remnants of the old crops present in the field and do deep plowing of the field. After that do two to three good slant plowing in the field through cultivator. After plowing, level the field by putting a pad.

Climate Lychee is a sub-tropical fruit and thrives best under moist subtropical climate. It generally prefers low altitudes and can be grown up to an altitude of 800 m. Lychee cannot tolerate frost during winter and dry summer temperatures should not fall below 40.5 0C in summer and freezing point in winter.

Harvest Priod The lychee plant has a long growing period with four distinct phanophages. Depending on the age of the tree, there are four growths in litchi plants. Young non-bearing stage (1-3 years), youth bearing stage (6-10 years), junior adult bearing stage (11-20 years) and senior adult bearing stage (21 years and over).

Fertilizers and Manures Apply vermicompost, poultry manure, organic fertilizer, FYM, vermiwash, neem cake and mulching for growth and development. Fertilizers should be applied about 1 year after transplanting. The application should be very light and spread evenly, but the fertilizer should not touch the trunks of the trees.

Irrigation Once the fruit setting is over and the fruits are 1 cm in size, watering is done at the rate of 1350 liters per tree per week. There are four main methods of watering litchi plants: (1) surface irrigation, (2) sprinkler irrigation, (3) drip irrigation and (4) sub-irrigation.

Harvesting Time After flowering, it takes 70 to 100 days for fruit ripening in most of the commercial varieties of India. On an average, the yield of a litchi tree is 40-100 kg. Fruit annually depending on variety, season, nutrition and age. Harvesting is usually done in May and June.

Yield Rate On an average, a litchi tree produces 40 to 100 kg of fruit annually depending on the variety, season and age. Yield in Litchi Fruit Cultivation:- Generally the yield of any crop depends on the variety, climate and farm management practices. On an average, the yield of a litchi tree ranges from 40 to 100 kg per year.

Cleaning and Drying Only ripe fruits that have not been stolen by insects should be used for drying. The drying process should begin as soon as possible (within 1 to 2 hours) after picking, if the fruit shows dust or spray deposits, it should first be washed in a dilute sodium hydroxide solution (0.25 percent).
