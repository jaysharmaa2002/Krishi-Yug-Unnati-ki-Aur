Sandalwood

Sandalwood-Among medicinal and commercial crops, sandalwood is a tree whose wood is associated with Indian culture and civilization. If we talk about the path of worship in Hinduism, then the importance increases even more. Benefits of Sandalwood: Sandalwood is mostly used for the manufacture of oil, incense, medicine, perfume and beauty material.But apart from this, do you know that the sandalwood tree is considered very sacred and useful, according to mythological times, sandalwood paste was also used as a treatment and medicine for Ayurveda.

Sandalwood is a class of woods from trees in the genus Santalum. Sandalwood oil is extracted from the woods for use. Sandalwood is often cited as one of the most expensive woods in the world. Both the wood and the oil produce a distinctive fragrance that has been highly valued for centuries.


Time of sowing If you want, you can also do good farming with sandalwood seeds, the time from August to March of the year is considered to be the best and suitable. And it takes 15 to 20 years to grow.

Sandalwood Nursery For improved cultivation of sandalwood, if you want, you can plant it through nursery or seed. If one acre is taken as an estimate, about 435 plants are required. You can plant it in your fields in the last week of April or in the month of October. On an average, if we talk about a tree, which is fully developed, we can get up to 40 kg of wood. From which you can get an idea that a tree can give you an income of lakhs.

Suitable Climate If we talk about the suitable climate and weather for the cultivation of sandalwood, then hot and dry temperature is considered good for it, in addition, if there is a temperature of 12 C to 35 C in an area with medium rainfall and sunshine, then it is good. yield occurs.

Land selection For the cultivation of sandalwood, it is necessary to have fertile land, for this such land is needed in which there is no water logging. For this, loamy soil is considered suitable, apart from this the pH value of the soil should be between 6.5 to 7.5.

Land Preparation For sandalwood cultivation, the field is prepared in the month of April-May, a deep plowing is required before sowing. The capacity of the soil is increased by plowing 2-3 times, 30 -40 cm between the beds. m. There should be a distance.

Manures and Fertilizers There is no need of much fertilizer in sandalwood cultivation. Initially manure is required at the time of crop growth.

Weed Control To control weeds, weeding should be done. Also, use mixed farming. Mixed farming helps in controlling weeds from the crop.

Irrigation After planting, drip technology or micro spring can be used for irrigation. During the rainy season, sandalwood trees grow rapidly, but during the summer season it has to be irrigated more. Irrigation depends on soil moisture and weather.

Sandalwood Harvesting Sandalwood tree becomes suitable for harvesting in 15-20 years. The roots of the sandalwood tree are very aromatic. Therefore, instead of cutting down its tree, it is uprooted along with the root.

Identification of real sandalwood The best way to test sandalwood is to rub it on any solid place, rub it on the floor until it becomes hot, on doing this, a fragrant smell comes from it, then you will know that it is The real sandalwood Looking at the floor and its identification signs has been found to be the best and easiest way to prove it is real or fake.
