Pear

Pear-Pear is a fruit of temperate zone. It is a medium-height tree reaching 10-17 m (33-56 ft), often with a tall, narrow crest shape at the top. Some of its species are also shrub forms, whose height is not high. Pear is grown at an altitude of 1,700-2,400 meters above sea level.This fruit is the main source of protein and vitamins. Due to its adaptability to a variety of climates and soils, pears can be cultivated in sub-tropical to temperate regions of India.

Pears are fruits produced and consumed around the world, growing on a tree and harvested in the Northern Hemisphere in late summer into October. The pear tree and shrub are a species of genus Pyrus in the family Rosaceae, bearing the pomaceous fruit of the same name. Several species of pears are valued for their edible fruit and juices, while others are cultivated as trees.

The tree is medium-sized and native to coastal and mildly temperate regions of Europe, North Africa, and Asia. Pear wood is one of the preferred materials in the manufacture of high-quality woodwind instruments and furniture.

About 3000 known varieties of pears are grown worldwide, which vary in both shape and taste. The fruit is consumed fresh, canned, as juice, or dried. The pear is native to coastal and mildly temperate regions of the Old World, from Western Europe and North Africa east across Asia. It is a medium-sized tree, reaching 10-17 m (33-56 ft) tall, often with a tall, narrow crown; a few species are shrubby.


Time of sowing January is the best month for planting saplings, for planting saplings, one year old plants are used.

Distance Plants are grown by keeping a distance of 6-7 meters between the plants.

Sowing method Plants are prepared by grafting method by sowing of pears by preparing nursery by seeds.

Seed germination method For planting seeds, choose a high yielding tree and get seeds from its ripe fruit. After removing the seeds, keep sand in wooden boxes for 30 days in the month of December and irrigated with water. Sow the seeds in the nursery in the month of January and the seeds germinate within 10 days. These plants are ready for grafting by January of the next year.

Planting Method Square or rectangular method is adopted for planting trees, but in hilly areas, slope method is used. And by T-budding or tongue grafting of pear parts, the lower parts of the cantha tree are used. Grafting is done in December-January month and T-budding is done in May-June month.

Favorable Climate Pear cultivation requires moist sub-tropical and dry temperate climate for good yield. The temperature of 10-25 C is favorable for its cultivation.

Land selection Loamy soil is most suitable for the cultivation of pears. In this deep, well drained, fertile soil, which is not hard till 2 m depth, gives good yield.

Farm Preparation Before planting, deep plowing should be done to make the soil level and friable. For planting after plowing, dig pits of size 1x1x1m and leave a month before transplanting in November by filling it with soil with 500 grams of cow dung. Apply 500 g single super phosphate fertilizer at the time of planting. After planting the plant, give light irrigation also.

Fertilizers and Chemical Fertilizers
*Vermi compost or well decomposed cow dung (FYM) should be given to get good yield in pear cultivation.
*When the tree is 3 years old, mix 10 kg of cow dung, 100-300 g urea, 200-300 g single phosphate and 200-450 g murate of potash in the soil and irrigate it.
*Apply 25-35 kg cow dung, 400-600 g urea, 800-1200 g Single Phosphate (SSP), 600-900 g Murate of Potash per tree after 4-6 year old trees.
*Apply 40-60 kg cow dung, 700-900 g urea, 1400-1800 g single phosphate, 1 kg Murate of Potash (MOP) per tree in 7-9 years old tree.
*Apply full dose of cow dung, single super phosphate and muriate of potash in the month of December. Half dose of urea should be applied in early February before flowering and the remaining half dose should be applied in the month of April after harvesting of fruits.

Weed control To control weeds, weeding should be done from time to time as per the requirement.

Irrigation An irrigation should be given immediately after planting pear seedlings. Irrigate at an interval of 5 to 7 days in summer and 15 days in winter. Irrigation should be done according to soil moisture and weather. The problem of excessive waterlogging in the field will affect the fruit color and quality and increase the chances of disease. So do better drainage. For good farming and a strong system of irrigation, drip irrigation system should be used.

Intercrops Crops like urad, moong, tauri in Kharif season and wheat, peas, gram etc. can be cultivated as inter-cropping in kharif season till the trees become fruitful.

Cutting and Pruning The branches of the plant are pruned to make them stronger, higher yields and better quality fruits. For this, the twigs of disease-ridden, destroyed, broken and weak branches are cut.

Harvest Pear fruits are harvested from the first week of June to the middle of September. Green fruits are plucked after the fruits are fully ripe in nearby markets and transported to distant places.

Harvest time Ripening of hard varieties of pears takes about 145 days, while normal soft varieties require 135-140 days.

Storage Sort the fruits after harvesting, then the fruits can be stored in fiber boxes and taken to the market, treat the fruits with 1000 ppm ethephon for 4-5 minutes so that the unripe fruits are ripe or they can be stored within 24 hours. Put it in ethylene gas for 100 ppm and then store it at 20 C. The fruits can be stored for 60 days in a store at 0-1 degree centigrade temperature and 90-95% humidity.

Production On an average, 4-5 quintals of fruits are produced per tree of pear.
